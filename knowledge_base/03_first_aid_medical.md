## Medical Care Requirements

### Minor Medical Care Consent
- For Carbon Health visits, must have **Carbon Health's Consent for Medical Care of a Minor form signed by a parent**
- Keep copies of all student medical consent forms accessible

### When to Seek Medical Care

#### Emergency (Call 9-1-1 or go to ER)
- Serious injury/illness threatening life or limb
- Loss of consciousness
- Severe allergic reaction
- Difficulty breathing
- Chest pain
- Severe bleeding
- Head injury with confusion
- Suspected broken bones (severe)
- High fever with altered mental status

#### Urgent Care
- Minor injuries (sprains, strains)
- Minor burns or cuts
- Mild allergic reactions
- Moderate fever
- Ear infections
- Minor infections
- Dehydration requiring IV fluids

#### Use First Aid Kit
- Minor cuts and scrapes
- Blisters
- Minor burns (first degree)
- Headaches
- Minor stomach upset
- Insect bites/stings
- Sunburn
- Motion sickness

## First Aid Kit Maintenance

### Restock Needs Tracking
- Use "Current" and "Restock Needs" columns in inventory list
- Check inventory after each session
- Restock between sessions
- Keep notes on frequently used items

### Storage Requirements
- Keep first aid kits in accessible location
- All staff should know location
- One kit per team/excursion group minimum
- Carry emergency action plan in first aid kit at all times

### Emergency Action Plan
- Emergency Action Plan must be carried in first aid kit at all times
- All staff and students must be made aware of plan during onboarding/orientation

## Medical Emergency Response Protocol

### Immediate Response Steps
1. Assess the situation and ensure scene safety
2. Call for help (9-1-1 for emergencies, Campus Director for non-emergencies)
3. Apply first aid as necessary and appropriate to training level
4. Contact SSB Campus Director
5. Contact parents/guardians as appropriate
6. Document incident thoroughly
7. Follow up care and monitoring

### Contact Hierarchy for Medical Issues
1. **Life-threatening emergencies:** Call 9-1-1 immediately
2. **Campus Director:** Notify immediately after calling 9-1-1 or for non-emergency medical issues
3. **SSB 24/7 Helpline:** +1.858.779.0555
4. **Parents/Guardians:** Notify per protocol
5. **Risk Management Director:** Maria Balmaseda - 347-210-4976

## Quarantine and Isolation Procedures

### During Program Quarantine/Isolation
- Student(s) or staff member(s) will quarantine or isolate as needed
- Location determined by Campus Director
- Must meet isolation standards
- Consider current campus/government mandates

### Post-Program Isolation
- May be at same campus or another location
- If another location, name, address, and location to be provided by Campus Director
- Coordinate with student's family for travel arrangements if needed

## Special Medical Situations

### Student Medications
- Students responsible for self-administration of medications
- Medication lists maintained by Campus Director
- Special dietary restrictions and allergies documented
- Emergency medications (EpiPens, inhalers) location known to all staff

### Mental Health Support
- **Counseling Services After Hours:** 510-642-9494
- Campus Director to be notified of any mental health concerns
- Follow campus protocols for mental health emergencies

### COVID-19 Protocols
- COVID testing available at Carbon Health locations
- Positive cases: use N95 masks from first aid kit
- Follow current CDC and campus guidelines for isolation
- Notify Campus Director and Risk Management immediately

## Documentation Requirements

### Incident Reporting
- Document all first aid kit usage
- Complete incident reports for any medical treatment
- Note medications dispensed from kit
- Record parent/guardian notifications
- Photograph injuries if appropriate (with consent)

### Medical Records Access
- Keep student medical information confidential
- Only share on need-to-know basis
- Campus Director maintains master medical files
- Emergency medical information accessible to all staff

## Training Requirements

### Staff Certification
- All staff should have current First Aid/CPR certification
- Know location of all first aid kits
- Familiar with Emergency Action Plan
- Trained on when to call 9-1-1 vs. when to use first aid kit
- Know how to contact Campus Director and emergency services

### Medication Administration
- Staff generally do NOT administer medications
- Students self-administer
- Exception: emergency situations (EpiPen, etc.) with proper training
- Document any assistance provided
