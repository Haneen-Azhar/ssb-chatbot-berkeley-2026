## Operational Details Extracted

### Daily Schedule

**Standard Day Structure:**
- 7:00 AM - 7:45 AM: Breakfast with mentor groups
- 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM: Academic courses (AM oversees, mentors support)
- 12:00 PM - 1:00 PM: Lunch with mentor groups
- 1:00 PM - 6:00 PM: Afternoon activities (CRW, clubs, free time)
- 6:00 PM - 7:00 PM: Dinner with mentor groups
- 7:00 PM - 9:30 PM: Evening activities (SPA leads, all mentors support)
- 9:30 PM - 10:00 PM: Back to room
- 10:30 PM: Bed checks (account for all students, Rose & Thorn check-in)
- 10:30 PM - 11:00 PM: Team huddle (mandatory for all staff)

**Commuter Schedule:**
- Check-in: 8:30 AM (8:45 AM Monday/Tuesday)
- Check-out: 6:00 PM - 6:15 PM
- Not permitted in residential areas or student bedrooms
- Welcome in common spaces

**Special Days:**
- Day 1 (Sunday): Arrival, check-in, orientations, tours
- Day 2 (Monday): All Student Orientation, Instructor Orientation, Club Fair (ALL HANDS)
- Wednesday: Academic excursions
- Saturday/Sunday: Weekend excursions (ALL HANDS ON DECK)
- Final Friday: Soiree, check-out, departures

### Staff Roles (Detailed)

**Program Director (PD):**
- Behind-the-scenes support
- Handles complex issues when CD exhausted resources
- Weekly check-ins with CDs
- ALWAYS involved in: behavioral contracts, dismissals, room searches, major crises

**Campus Director (CD) - The CD Dozen:**
1. Know the campus
2. Set up operations
3. Prepare programming
4. Assign responsibilities
5. Coordinate arrivals
6. Build community
7. Run daily rhythm
8. Oversee entire program
9. Support people
10. Reinforce policies
11. Lead program closing
12. Close out campus

**Academic Manager (AM) - The AM Six:**
1. Oversee academics (CRW, excursions, classrooms)
2. Support instructors
3. Manage academic logistics
4. Partner with leadership
5. Support mentors and students
6. Solve problems early

**Summer Program Assistant (SPA) - The SPA Six:**
1. Coordinate transportation
2. Run activities smoothly
3. Manage logistics
4. Support excursions
5. Enhance student experience
6. Support program closeout

**Mentors - The 6 M's:**

5. Facilitator
6. Teammate

### Check-In/Check-Out Procedures

**Arrival Day Workstations:**
1. Check-In Station (verify info)
2. REACH Station (app download and login)
3. Swag Station (lanyard, name tag, t-shirt, bag)
4. Cards & Keys Station (PEX/meal card, room key, fob)
5. Academic Uniforms Station (scrubs, lab coats by course)

**Post-Check-In Timeline:**
- 5:30 PM: Icebreakers in mentor groups
- 6:00 PM: Dinner (pizza) with mentor groups
- 7:00 PM: Light orientation (all students)
- 7:45 PM: Commuters depart
- 8:00 PM: Residence hall tour (residential)
- 8:30 PM: Unstructured time (RCR and Roommate Contract completion)
- 10:30 PM: Bed checks (verify forms completed)

**Required Completion Day 1:**
- Room Condition Reports (RCR)
- Roommate Contracts
- Both checked during bed checks

**Departure Day:**
- Room inspections
- Material collection (keys, fobs, cards, uniforms)
- Zero out PEX/Bill cards
- Airport drop-offs (stay until through security)
- Inventory completion

### Activity Logistics

**Club Fair (1st Monday):**
- ALL HANDS ON DECK
- Cupcake social + club fair setup
- Students visit all tables (passport system)
- Choose 1 club per week (some campuses allow 2)
- No switching once selected
- Clubs meet up to 4 times during session

**Minimum Clubs:**
- Small campus: 4 clubs
- Large campus: 7 clubs

**Common clubs:** Team Sports, Art & Chill, Dance, Craft, Study, Reading, Movie, Yearbook (2 weeks), Soiree Planning (2 weeks)

**Evening Activities:**
- Movie Night, Karaoke, Flag Painting, Talent Show, Cultural Exchange, Science Fair, Game Night, Dance Party, Trivia

**Weekend Excursions:**
- ALL HANDS ON DECK (all mentors required)
- No time off Saturday or Sunday nights
- Academic and recreational excursions
- Roll call before boarding bus, count on bus, count at destination
- Maintain supervision at all times

**Activity Budget:**
- $200 per student per session for non-academic activities
- Includes Friday activity, evening activities, club supplies
- Does NOT include academic excursions, meals, academic supplies

1. Academic Courses (8:30 AM - 9:10 AM window)
2. College Readiness Workshops (upon arrival)
3. Activities - Afternoon (at start)

- Students request via app
- Parent must approve
- CD reviews and approves/denies

**Communication:**
- Direct messaging to students
- Group messaging to mentor groups or full program
- Push notifications for urgent info
- NEVER use personal phone numbers or social media

### Key Policies

**Staff Time Off:**
- Minimum 1 night off (6 PM - 8 AM) per week
- No time off Saturday, Sunday, or final night of session
- Must stay within 2 hours of campus when off duty
- Between sessions: Friday evening (when dismissed) to Sunday 9 AM

**Staffing Ratios (Must maintain at all times):**
- High School: 1 mentor to 10 students
- Middle School: 1 mentor to 8 students

**Quiet Hours:**
- Sunday - Thursday: 11:00 PM - 8:00 AM
- Friday - Saturday: 1:00 AM - 8:00 AM

**Curfew:**
- Students back on campus by 9:30 PM
- In room ready for bed checks by 10:30 PM

**Rule of 3:**
- Students NEVER travel alone
- Minimum groups of 3 at all times
- Exceptions: own room, bathroom, official 1-on-1 with staff

**Banned Staff Practices:**
- Sharing room with student
- Alone with student in closed room
- Touching student when no one else around
- Sharing personal phone numbers
- Social media with students
- Relationships with other staff during program
- Alcohol or substances during program

### Coaching vs. Performance Management

**Coaching First Approach:**
- Does staff understand expectations?
- What support do they need?
- What tools/resources missing?
- Have expectations been clearly communicated?

**Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) when:**
- Issues continue after coaching
- Accountability conversations documented
- Student/staff safety or experience impacted
- Repeated operational concerns
- Failure to improve after coaching

**Document using:** SSB Staff Coaching and Performance Management Report

**Always involves PD and HQ**

### Pre-Program Training (PPT) New Model

**Structure:**
1. **Knowledge Building** - Slidedeck with crucial info and policies
2. **Scenario** - Immediate practice of skill just learned
3. **Facilitator Questions** - Check understanding, explain complex situations

**Example Module: Bed Checks**
- Knowledge: How to do bed checks, what to look for, how to mark in REACH
- Scenario: Practice checking in students, identifying issues
- Questions: "After bed checks on night one, should students ever have 'Checked In' location?" (Answer: No)

**Goal:** Mentors leave PPT having practiced their role and feel confident going into first session

## Key Takeaways

### Most Critical Operational Procedures

1. **Daily Schedule is Non-Negotiable**
   - Breakfast at 7:00 AM, Academic 9-12, Lunch 12-1, Activities 1-6, Dinner 6-7, Evening 7-9:30, Bed Checks 10:30 PM, Team Huddle 10:30-11 PM

2. **Student Safety First Always**
   - EpiPens carried by designated staff
   - Rule of 3 enforced constantly
   - Bed checks account for ALL students
   - Medical info reviewed and known

3. **REACH is Primary Operating System**
   - All attendance taken via REACH
   - Communication through REACH
   - Leave requests via REACH
   - Must be taken during specific time windows

4. **Staff Communication Flow is Structured**
   - PD ↔ CD ↔ AM ↔ SPA ↔ Mentors
   - Escalate appropriately
   - Document everything

5. **Mentors are Backbone of Program**
   - Deep relationships with ~10 students
   - Daily touchpoints through meals and activities
   - Trusted confidants and safety monitors
   - Model SSB values

6. **All Hands on Deck Days are Sacred**
   - Monday night (Club Fair)
   - Saturdays and Sundays (excursions)
   - Arrival and departure days
   - No exceptions for time off

7. **Documentation is Essential**
   - Incident reports
   - Parent communications
   - Attendance daily
   - Medical incidents
   - Code of Conduct violations

8. **Coaching Before Performance Management**
   - Support staff first
   - Clear expectations
   - Document conversations
   - PIP only when coaching fails

9. **Student Code of Conduct Must Be Signed**
   - During orientation (Day 2)
   - Sexual Harassment Policy read verbatim
   - Consequences clearly explained
   - 3-step discipline: Warning → Contract → Dismissal

10. **Budget Management is Tight**
    - $200 per student for non-academic activities
    - Track all spending
    - Upload receipts daily
    - Stay within budget

### Common Staff Mistakes to Avoid

1. Not taking attendance during required REACH windows
2. Skipping mentor groups (crucial for student well-being)
3. Being alone with student in closed room
4. Not carrying EpiPen when assigned
5. Leaving students unsupervised
6. Not documenting incidents immediately
7. Trying to handle major issues without escalating to CD
8. Violating student privacy by sharing medical info
9. Not maintaining 1:10 or 1:8 ratio
10. Taking time off on restricted days (Saturday, Sunday, final night)
