# Meal and Activity Logistics - Summer Springboard 2026

## Meal Schedule and Structure

### Standard Meal Times

| Meal | Time | Duration | Location |
|------|------|----------|----------|
| Breakfast | 7:00 AM - 7:45 AM | 45 min | Campus dining hall |
| Lunch | 12:00 PM - 1:00 PM | 1 hour | Campus dining hall or off-campus |
| Dinner | 6:00 PM - 7:00 PM | 1 hour | Campus dining hall or off-campus |

**Breakfast:** Always on campus at dining hall

## Dining Hall Meals (On-Campus)

### Standard Procedure

**Walking to Dining Hall:**
1. Mentors meet students at designated location
2. Walk together as mentor group
3. Maintain supervision throughout
4. Use as relationship-building time

**In Dining Hall:**
1. Students use meal cards to enter (or campus access)
2. Mentors sit with their mentor groups
3. Ensure students select adequate food
4. Monitor for dietary compliance
5. Continue building relationships
6. Address any issues immediately

**Leaving Dining Hall:**
1. Ensure all students finished eating
2. Return trays appropriately
3. Account for all mentor group members
4. Proceed to next activity together

### Dining Hall Locations (Campus Specific)

**UC Berkeley:**
- Crossroads Dining Hall (primary)
- Students use Crossroads meal cards

**Other campuses:** See campus-specific info in Summer Staff Portal

### Dietary Restrictions and Allergies

**Critical Information:**
- 159 students across all programs require EpiPens
- Many students have food allergies or dietary restrictions
- All documented in medical forms and REACH

**Staff Responsibilities:**

**Before Program:**
1. Review medical info for students in your mentor group
2. Note all allergies, intolerances, restrictions
3. Identify students with EpiPens (mentor must carry)
4. Communicate needs to dining services

**During Program:**
1. Read all food signage carefully in dining hall
2. Help students identify safe foods
3. Ask dining staff if unsure about ingredients
4. Monitor students with severe allergies during meals
5. Keep EpiPen accessible if assigned
6. Report any reactions immediately

**Common Dietary Needs:**
- Food allergies (nuts, shellfish, dairy, eggs, etc.)
- Vegetarian
- Vegan
- Gluten-free
- Halal
- Kosher
- Lactose intolerant
- Religious restrictions

**If Food Not Available:**
- Contact dining services immediately
- Find alternative on-site if possible
- Document issue
- Report to CD
- May need to purchase appropriate food

### EpiPen Protocol

**Designated Staff:**
- Specific mentors assigned to carry EpiPens for students with prescriptions
- Named in medical documentation
- Trained on use

**Requirements:**
1. Carry EpiPen at ALL times during program hours
2. Know student's name, face, and allergy
3. Recognize signs of anaphylaxis
4. Know how to administer
5. Keep EpiPen accessible (not locked in office)

**If Allergic Reaction Occurs:**
1. Administer EpiPen immediately if severe
2. Call 9-1-1
3. Contact CD
4. Stay with student
5. Monitor vital signs
6. Be prepared to administer second EpiPen if needed (after 5-10 min)
7. Document everything

See First Aid and Medical Procedures document for full protocol.

### Hydration

**Staff responsible for:**
1. Ensuring students drink water throughout day
2. Reminding students to hydrate, especially in heat
3. Pointing out water fountains/filtration stations
4. Encouraging reusable water bottle use
5. Monitoring for signs of dehydration

**Provided:**
- Snacks and water at Director's Training
- Water access at all campus locations
- Students encouraged to bring reusable water bottles

### Between Session Staff Meals

- Breakfast: $10 reimbursement
- Lunch: $20 reimbursement
- Dinner: $20 reimbursement

**If eating out during night off:**
- Personal expense
- Not reimbursed

## Off-Campus Meals

### Student Meal Funds

**Allocation per meal:**
- Lunch: $20
- Dinner: $20-$25

**Distribution Methods (Campus Specific):**

**PEX Cards (Berkeley, UCLA, MIT, NYSID):**
- Students assigned individual PEX cards
- CD adds funds daily ($20 per meal)
- Funds roll over if not fully used
- Students encouraged to track balance
- CD can check balances via PEX admin dashboard

**Bill Credit Cards (Oxford):**
- Similar to PEX system
- CD assigns funds to student cards
- $20 per meal
- Managed through Bill app

**Cash (Some Campuses):**
- CD withdraws from Bank of America ATM
- Distributes cash to students
- $20 lunch, $20-25 dinner
- Students responsible for funds
- No receipts required from students

### Mentor Group Off-Campus Meals

**Occasionally mentor groups eat together off-campus**

**Process:**
1. CD approves activity
2. Same fund amounts apply ($20-25 per student)
3. Mentor uses PEX card to cover group
4. Mentor submits receipt through PEX app
5. Mentor supervises students throughout

### Food Delivery Policy

**Students may order food delivery:**
- Must be during designated meal periods
- Student's own expense (not program funds)
- Must stay within campus boundaries for delivery
- Must coordinate with roommates/floor

### Off-Campus Dining Supervision

**Students on own with funds:**
- Rule of 3 always applies
- Must stay within boundaries
- Must return by designated time
- Check out/in via REACH if required

**Students with mentors:**
- Mentor supervises entire time
- Ensures safety and appropriate behavior
- Models good dining etiquette

## Clubs

### Club Fair (1st Monday Evening)

**Timing:** 7:00 PM - 9:30 PM (part of evening activity)
**ALL HANDS ON DECK**

**Setup:**
1. Light all-resident activity on one side (cupcake social, game night)
2. Club Fair on other side of common area
3. Mentors create marketing materials for clubs

**Student Participation:**
- Students visit all club tables

- 30-60 minutes typically

## Weekend Excursions

### Saturday and Sunday: ALL HANDS ON DECK

**All mentors required**
**Extended hours**
**No time off on Saturday or Sunday nights**

### Types of Excursions

**Academic Excursions:**
- Related to course content
- Usually Wednesday afternoons, but can be weekends
- Examples: lab visits, company tours, guest speakers, field research
- Course mentor POC attends
- Educational objectives
- May require special transportation

**Recreational Excursions:**
- Fun and team building
- Examples: Six Flags, beach, city exploration, museums, sporting events
- All students participate
- Relationship building focus
- Create memories

**City/Campus Exploration:**
- Saturday often includes local area exploration
- Students may have free time within boundaries
- Rule of 3 applies
- Mentor groups may split up with supervision
- Students given meal funds

### Excursion Planning (SPA and CD)

**Pre-Session:**
1. All excursions confirmed and linked in Master Schedule
2. Transportation arranged (coach buses usually)
3. Tickets/admissions purchased
4. Dietary accommodations arranged
5. Emergency plans in place

**Week Before:**
1. Confirm all reservations
2. Brief staff on schedule and roles
3. Review student medical needs
4. Prepare materials (tickets, waivers, etc.)
5. Check weather and backup plans

**Day Before:**
1. Final confirmation with vendors
2. Brief students on expectations
3. Review schedule with staff
4. Pack first aid kits
5. Prepare attendance materials

### Excursion Day Logistics

**Morning Departure:**

**Before Boarding Bus:**
1. Students meet at designated location
2. Mentors take roll call with mentor groups
3. Account for ALL students
4. Load buses by mentor group when possible
5. Ensure medical supplies onboard (first aid, EpiPens)

**On Bus:**
1. Count students again once boarded
2. Assign seating if needed (behavior management)
3. Review expectations and schedule
4. Review safety rules
5. Mentors sit distributed throughout bus
6. Monitor behavior during ride
7. Keep energy positive

**At Destination:**
1. Count students as they exit bus
2. Gather in designated meeting spot
3. Review meeting times and locations
4. Review boundaries for free time
5. Distribute phones/radios to staff
6. Review emergency procedures
7. Ensure students have mentor contact info

**During Excursion:**
1. Maintain supervision at all times
2. Mentor groups stay together or divide with clear plan
3. Designated meeting times and locations
4. Regular headcounts
5. Monitor for student issues (illness, conflict, etc.)
6. Capture photos for blog/marketing
7. Ensure students eating and hydrating
8. Enforce Code of Conduct

**Meals During Excursion:**
- Pre-arranged (Six Flags meal plan)
- Funds provided to students ($20 lunch, $20-25 dinner)
- Designated meal time and location
- Ensure all students eat

**Return to Bus:**
1. Gather at designated time and location
2. Count students before boarding
3. Count students once on bus
4. Do NOT leave until all accounted for
5. Secure all belongings

**Return to Campus:**
1. Count students as they exit bus
2. Brief students on rest of evening
3. Ensure smooth transition to next activity
4. Debrief with staff

### Excursion Safety Protocols

**Medical:**
- First aid kit on bus and at site
- EpiPens with designated staff
- Know location of nearest hospital
- Have emergency contact info readily available
- At least one staff member with CPR/First Aid

**Communication:**
- Radios or phones for staff
- Emergency contact card for each student
- CD or designee has master student list
- SSB 24/7 Helpline number shared with all staff
- Campus emergency number available

**Supervision:**
- Maintain ratios (1:10 for HS, 1:8 for MS)
- Never leave students unsupervised
- Clear meeting times and locations
- Buddy system or Rule of 3
- Staff positioned throughout activity

**Boundaries:**
- Clear boundaries established
- Students briefed on where they can/cannot go
- Consequences for violations
- Regular check-ins

**Weather:**
- Monitor forecast
- Have backup indoor plan
- Appropriate clothing communicated to students
- Delay or cancel if dangerous
- Stay flexible

### Common Excursion Challenges

**Student Gets Separated:**
1. Immediately notify CD
2. Check last known location
3. Call/text student if they have phone
4. Send staff to search common areas
5. Contact venue security
6. Do not panic students
7. Account for all other students
8. Call 9-1-1 if not located quickly

**Student Gets Sick:**
1. Remove from activity
2. Assess severity
3. Contact CD
4. Administer first aid if needed
5. Seek medical care if necessary
6. Contact parents
7. One staff stays with student, others continue supervision
8. Document incident

**Bus Breakdown:**
1. Stay calm
2. Keep students on bus if safe
3. Contact bus company
4. Contact CD if not on bus
5. Arrange alternate transportation if needed
6. Communicate delays to campus/parents
7. Keep students comfortable and engaged

**Weather Cancellation:**
1. Activate backup plan
2. Communicate to students
3. Keep morale up
4. Execute alternative activity
5. Make most of situation

## Free Time and Unstructured Activities

### Daily Free Time (8:30 PM - 9:30 PM)

**Student Rules:**
- Cannot leave residence hall
- Must be in groups of 3+
- Stay in common areas or rooms
- Quiet hours approaching (11 PM)

**Student Options:**
- Socialize in common areas
- Play games
- Homework/studying
- Relax in rooms
- Use recreation facilities (gym, pool if available)

**Staff Supervision:**
- Be visible in hallways and common areas
- Available for questions or support
- Monitor for rule violations
- Ensure safety
- Build relationships through casual interaction
- Not formal programming, but still responsible

### Boundary Map and Rule of 3

**During Free Time Off-Campus (if allowed):**
- Students must stay within boundary map
- Shown and provided during orientation
- Rule of 3 always applies
- Must check out/in via REACH if leaving campus
- Must return by curfew (9:30 PM back on campus)

**Common Boundary Locations (Example: UC Berkeley):**
- Campus academic buildings
- Nearby restaurants and shops
- Public spaces
- Off-limits: far from campus, dangerous areas

**Enforcement:**
- Violations of boundaries = Code of Conduct issue
- May result in warning, contract, or dismissal
- Emphasize safety reason for boundaries

### Food Delivery

**Rules:**
- Only during designated meal periods
- Student's own expense
- Must coordinate with roommates
- Stay within boundaries for pickup if meeting delivery

## Activity Safety Considerations

### General Safety

**For All Activities:**
1. Appropriate supervision ratio maintained
2. Staff positioned throughout activity
3. Know location of first aid kit
4. Know location of nearest bathroom
5. Know emergency exits
6. Have emergency contact readily available
7. Monitor for injuries or illness
8. Enforce Code of Conduct
9. Watch for excluded/isolated students
10. Keep students engaged and positive

### Activity-Specific Safety

**Physical Activities (Sports, Dance):**
- Warm up properly
- Hydration breaks
- Monitor for overexertion
- Appropriate space and equipment
- Watch for injuries
- Students with physical limitations accommodated

**Food-Related (Cooking Club, Food Activities):**
- Know student allergies
- Prevent cross-contamination
- Supervise use of heat/sharp objects
- Proper food handling
- Clean workspace

**Outdoor Activities:**
- Check weather
- Sun protection
- Insect precautions
- Appropriate clothing
- Stay within boundaries
- Account for students regularly

**Water Activities (Pool, Beach):**
- Students must pass swim test
- Lifeguard on duty
- Buddy system
- Boundaries clearly marked
- No swimming alone
- Enforce all posted rules
- See Water Safety in Code of Conduct

**Arts and Crafts:**
- Ventilation for fumes
- Safe use of tools
- Protect clothing
- Clean up properly
- Dispose of materials safely

**Technology Use (Video Games, Movie):**
- Age-appropriate content
- Time limits
- Monitor screen content
- Encourage breaks
- Social interaction, not isolation

### Monitoring Student Behavior

**Watch for:**
- Exclusion or bullying
- Inappropriate interactions
- Boundary violations
- Phone misuse
- Dress code violations
- Disrespect to staff or peers
- Disengagement or isolation
- Signs of illness or distress

**Respond:**
- Address issues immediately
- Separate students if conflict
- Enforce consequences consistently
- Document concerns
- Report to CD as needed
- Create inclusive environment
- Redirect inappropriate behavior

1. Plan thoroughly in advance
