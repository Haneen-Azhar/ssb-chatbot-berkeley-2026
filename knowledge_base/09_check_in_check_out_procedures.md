# Check-In and Check-Out Procedures - Summer Springboard 2026

## Student Arrival Day Check-In

### Overview
- **When:** Sunday, Session start day
- **Residential Students:** 2:00 PM - 5:30 PM
- **Commuter Students:** 4:00 PM - 5:30 PM
- **All Hands on Deck:** All staff required 9:00 AM - 11:00 PM

### Pre-Check-In Setup (9:00 AM - 2:00 PM)

#### 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM: Swag and Check-in Prep
**Assigned mentors complete:**
- Prep swag bags
- Put name tags in lanyards
- Organize name tags alphabetically
- Hang name tags on dorm room doors
- Set up tents and tables in courtyard
- Put up directional signs
- Blue painter's tape arrows on sidewalks from streets to courtyard

#### 12:30 PM - 1:30 PM: Setup Workstations

**Station 1: Check-In**
- Computers for student check-in
- Student information verification
- REACH access ready

**Station 2: Swag**
- SSB Lanyards with name tags (alphabetized)
- SSB bags
- T-shirts (sized and organized)

**Station 3: Cards and Keys**
- PEX cards (pre-assigned to students)
- Meal cards (Crossroads or campus-specific)
- Room keys
- Building fobs

**Station 4: Academic Uniforms**
- **High School Scrubs:** Emergency Medicine, Pre-med, Surgery, Pediatrics
- **High School Lab Coats:** Biotechnology, Nursing
- **Middle School Scrubs:** Medicine
- Organized by size and course

**Setup Music/Entertainment:**
- Large speaker for music and announcements
- Variety of games in courtyard and main lounge
- Tables arranged for dinner service

### Airport Pickup Procedures (11:00 AM - 5:00 PM)

#### Before Departure
**Assigned mentors must:**
1. Wear SSB orange staff t-shirt and lanyard
2. Get SSB signs from office
3. Get first aid kits from office
4. Take picture ID
5. Get list of students picking up with phone numbers
6. Message students in advance, including photo of themselves

#### At Airport
**Location:** Wait in baggage claim area
**Exception:** If unaccompanied minor, report to airline desk and follow their instructions

**Pickup Protocol:**
1. Greet student with SSB sign visible
2. Verify student identity
3. Have student call parents to confirm pickup
4. Collect luggage together
5. Proceed to transportation
6. Check student in via REACH once in vehicle

**Safety:**
- Never leave student unattended
- Stay with student until safely in vehicle
- Track all pickups
- Report any delays to CD immediately

### Check-In Process Flow (2:00 PM - 5:30 PM)

#### Student Arrival Sequence
**Each student goes through stations in order:**

**1. Check-In Station (First Stop)**
- Mentor checks in student on computer
- Verify information correct/complete
- Answer parent questions
- Direct to next station

**2. REACH Station**
- Help student download REACH app
- Assist with login
- Check student into program on REACH
- Explain how they'll use app

**3. Swag Station**
- Find student's name tag and lanyard (alphabetical)
- Give appropriate size SSB t-shirt
- Hand out SSB bag
- Explain swag items

**4. Cards and Keys Station**
- Assign pre-assigned PEX card (track card number in master list)
- Give meal card with instructions
- Hand out room key
- Give building fob
- Demonstrate how fob works

**5. Academic Uniform Station (if applicable)**
- Check student's course
- Find correct uniform type and size
- Explain when they'll wear it
- Answer questions

#### Room Escort (After All Stations)
**Assigned mentors:**
1. Show students/parents to dorm room
2. Demonstrate fobs for building entry and elevator
3. Point out main lounge
4. Point out SSB office
5. Escort to actual room
6. Point out floor bathroom and amenities
7. Answer any questions
8. Let them settle in

#### Engagement During Check-In
**Mentors not at stations:**
- Engage students in games in courtyard
- Interact with quiet, isolated students
- Introduce students to one another
- Create welcoming atmosphere
- Build excitement for program

### Special Populations Check-In

#### Commuter Students (4:00 PM - 5:30 PM)
**Check-in includes:**
- Complete regular check-in stations
- **Skip:** Room escort (no dorm assignment)
- Receive information sheet:
  - Where to meet Monday morning (front gates, 8:30 AM)
  - Commuter Mentor introduction
  - Daily check-in time (8:30 AM Monday/Tuesday, then varies)
  - Daily check-out time (6:00 PM - 6:15 PM)
  - Location of orientation

**Important Reminders for Commuters:**
- Communicate with parents about travel/pickup
- Monday & Tuesday: check in at 8:45 AM (later for orientation)
- Daily check-out: 6:00 PM at main gates
- Not permitted on residential floors or in student bedrooms
- Welcome in lobby, courtyard, common spaces

#### Agent Students
**Requires extra attention:**
- May arrive in groups
- Special tracking in Campus Tracker (mark as agent student)
- Any incidents must include Eva (HQ) in communication
- Contact agent before family in non-emergencies
- VIP agents (ASTEX, La Route des Langues) get special care

#### Sponsored Students (Non-Profit Partners)
**Organizations:** Minds Matter, Summer Search, HCZ, Take Stock in Children, SEO Scholars, school districts
- Loop in partner on any student-related incidents
- Same check-in process
- Track partnership in system

### Post-Check-In Schedule (Arrival Day)

#### 5:30 PM - 6:00 PM: Icebreakers in Mentor Groups
**All mentors with assigned groups:**
- Gather students into mentor groups
- Lead icebreaker activities
- Get to know you exercises
- Start building rapport

#### 6:00 PM - 7:00 PM: Dinner in Mentor Groups
**Pizza and salad typically:**
- Mentors eat with mentor groups
- Continue building relationships
- Casual conversation
- Monitor student interactions

#### 7:00 PM - 7:45 PM: All Student "Light" Orientation
**All mentors with their groups:**
- Students sit with mentor groups
- Mentors model good behavior
- Help manage students during presentation
- Answer questions as needed

**Topics Covered:**
- Welcome
- Brief program overview
- Where to meet tomorrow
- REACH app (most important)
- Commuter information

#### 7:45 PM - 8:00 PM: Commuter Departure
**Assigned mentors:**
- Check out commuters using REACH
- Remind: arrive at front gates 8:30 AM Monday for orientation
- Verify safe pickup

#### 8:00 PM - 8:30 PM: Residence Hall Tour (Residential Only)
**All mentors with their residential groups:**

**Tour includes:**
- Laundry room (demonstrate how machines work, help with app download)
- SSB office
- Trash and recycle room
- Kitchen (if applicable)
- Courtyard
- Front desk
- Gym/recreation areas
- Emergency exits
- Common areas (lounges, study spaces, game rooms)

**Reminders to students:**
- Breakfast time and meeting location
- Complete Room Condition Report (RCR) tonight
- Complete Roommate Contract tonight
- Both will be checked during bed checks

#### 8:30 PM - 9:30 PM: Unstructured Time
**All mentors on duty, visible and available:**

**Student rules:**
- Cannot leave residence hall
- Must be in groups of three
- Can socialize in common areas
- Can organize rooms

**Mentor responsibilities:**
- Be visible in hallways and common areas
- Help students access RCR and Roommate Contract
- Ensure students safe and following rules
- Build relationships through casual interaction

#### 9:30 PM - 10:00 PM: Back to Room
**Students return to rooms to prepare for bed**
**Mentors monitor hallways**

#### 10:30 PM: Bed Checks
**Assigned mentors:**
- Visit each room
- Account for all students
- Do quick check-in (Rose and Thorn)
- Verify RCR completed
- Verify Roommate Contract completed
- Mark students as checked in on REACH
- Note any concerns

#### 10:30 PM - 11:00 PM: Team Huddle
**All staff:**
- Debrief arrival day
- Share shout-outs
- Report any concerns
- Review Monday schedule
- Confirm assignments

### Check-In Day Cleanup (5:30 PM - 8:30 PM)

**Any available staff:**
- Take down tents, return to office
- Return unused swag, t-shirts, uniforms to office
- Return unused cards, keys, lanyards to office
- Return speaker and games to office
- Clean up dinner tables
- Store leftover food, plates, napkins, cups

## Daily Commuter Check-In (Monday - Friday)

### Morning Check-In (8:30 AM - 8:45 AM)

**Location:** Front gates or designated check-in area
**Staff:** Assigned commuter mentors (rotation)

**Procedures:**
1. Arrive 15 minutes early to prepare
2. Have REACH open and ready
3. Greet each commuter student
4. Check in student via REACH
5. Verify student has materials for day
6. Direct to first activity
7. Note any late arrivals

**Monday & Tuesday Exception:** 8:45 AM check-in time (orientation schedule)

**Tracking:**
- Mark attendance in REACH
- Note time of arrival
- Report absences to CD immediately
- Contact parents if student more than 15 minutes late

### Evening Check-Out (6:00 PM - 6:15 PM)

**Location:** Main gates
**Staff:** Assigned commuter mentors (rotation)

**Procedures:**
1. Gather all commuters at designated location by 6:00 PM
2. Account for all commuter students
3. Check out each student via REACH
4. Verify safe pickup (parent/guardian present)
5. Last student must leave by 6:15 PM
6. Note any late pickups
7. Contact parents if delayed
8. Stay until all students safely picked up

**Safety Protocol:**
- Never leave student waiting alone
- If parent significantly late (15+ min), contact CD
- Document late pickups
- Student stays with mentor until safe pickup

## Leave Requests During Program

### Approval Process (REACH)

**Students can request leave through REACH app:**

**Required Information:**
1. Destination
2. Pick up person (host)
3. Transportation method
4. Return time
5. Parent approval required

**Staff Approval (CD reviews):**
1. Click Leave Management > Manage Leave (or Leave > Approvals on mobile)
2. Review student request details
3. Confirm contact with pickup person
4. Verify parent approval
5. Approve or deny with comment if needed

**Some students have pre-approved leave requests from before program**

**Airport View:** Shows students leaving/returning in next 24 hours (note: not related to actual airport pickups)

### Adding Leave Requests on Behalf of Student
**CD or assigned staff can add:**
1. Navigate to Leave Management
2. Click Add New Request
3. Fill in all details
4. Ensure parent contacted and approved
5. Submit

### Monitoring Leave
- Check REACH regularly
- Verify return times
- Follow up if student late returning
- Document any issues

## Student Departure Day Check-Out

### Final Day Timeline
**Friday of session end**

#### Morning (7:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
- Final breakfast
- Students pack belongings
- Final academic session or activities
- Room cleaning begins

#### 12:00 PM - 1:00 PM: Final Lunch
- Students eat together one last time
- Mentors with mentor groups
- Begin goodbyes

#### 1:00 PM - 3:00 PM: Soiree/Closing Ceremony
- All staff support event
- Student presentations or activities
- Certificates distributed
- Formal program conclusion
- Photos and celebrations

#### 3:00 PM - 6:00 PM: Check-Out

### Check-Out Process

#### Student Room Check-Out
**Assigned mentors:**
1. Meet student at room
2. Review Room Condition Report from arrival
3. Inspect room for damage
4. Note any new damage (photos if needed)
5. Student removes all belongings
6. Collect room key
7. Collect building fob
8. Collect meal card
9. Collect PEX card (check for remaining balance, zero out)
10. Sign off on room check-out

**Damage Protocol:**
- Minor wear and tear: no charge
- Significant damage: document thoroughly
- Take photos
- Report to CD immediately
- CD coordinates with campus and family

#### Materials Collection
**All students return:**
- Room keys
- Building fobs
- Meal cards (or PEX cards depending on campus)
- Any borrowed equipment
- Academic uniforms (if applicable)

**Track all returns in master spreadsheet**

#### Final REACH Check-Out
**Students checked out of program:**
1. Verify all materials collected
2. Verify room inspected
3. Check student out via REACH
4. Mark departure time

### Departure Transportation

#### Airport Drop-Off
**Assigned mentors:**
- Verify flight times in advance
- Coordinate transportation
- Leave campus with adequate time
- Wear SSB staff shirt and lanyard
- Bring student list
- Check students in with airline
- **Stay at airport until student passes through security**
- Call HQ to confirm student through security
- Document drop-off time

**Unaccompanied Minors:**
- Check in at airline desk
- Follow airline specific protocols
- May need special forms
- Stay until airline confirms custody

#### Parent Pickup
**Check-out area (courtyard or designated space):**
1. Student waits in supervised area
2. Mentor verifies parent/guardian identity
3. Student checks out via REACH
4. Luggage loaded
5. Goodbyes
6. Document pickup time

**Last student must be picked up by designated time (typically 6:00 PM)**

### Late Pickups/Departures
**If student not picked up by deadline:**
1. Contact parents immediately
2. Student stays with assigned mentor
3. Provide dinner if needed
4. Continue supervision until safe pickup
5. Document late pickup
6. May incur additional fees (per policy)

### Back-to-Back Students

**Students staying for multiple sessions:**
- Do NOT fully check out
- Keep room assignment (usually)
- Keep keys and fobs
- Brief break between sessions
- Mentors assigned to supervise
- Limited activities during transition
- Ensure meals provided
- Safety and comfort priority

**Staff Coverage:**
- Hours divided among team
- CD coordinates schedule
- Usually 1-2 mentors adequate depending on number of students

## Between Session Arrivals

### Sunday Evening/Monday Morning Arrivals
**New session starts Sunday:**
- Full check-in process same as Session 1
- Airport pickups coordinated
- All hands on deck

**Staff may have limited time off if covering back-to-back students**

## Special Check-In/Check-Out Scenarios

### Early Departure (Student Leaves Mid-Program)

**Voluntary:**
1. Family contacts CD
2. CD discusses with student
3. Coordinate departure logistics
4. Complete room check-out
5. Collect all materials
6. Check out via REACH
7. Document reason for early departure
8. Tuition non-refundable

**Dismissal (See Discipline Procedures):**
1. PD and HQ involved
2. Student informed of removal
3. Student calls parent to inform
4. CD or mentor speaks to parent
5. Coordinate transportation (family expense)
6. Student supervised until departure
7. Complete check-out process
8. Ensure student safe passage
9. If airport drop-off, stay until through security
10. Debrief with remaining students and staff
11. Complete incident report

### Medical Departure
1. Coordinate with medical provider
2. Contact parents immediately
3. Staff accompanies student if hospitalized
4. Pack student belongings if unable
5. Coordinate return to campus or direct departure
6. Complete check-out if not returning
7. Document thoroughly
8. Follow up with family

### Emergency Evacuation
**See Emergency Procedures document**
- Account for all students
- Follow campus emergency protocols
- Reunify with families as coordinated
- Document all actions

## Common Issues and Solutions

### Issue: Student Arrives Without Required Forms
**Solution:**
1. Check in student anyway
2. Note missing forms
3. Contact parents immediately
4. Forms must be submitted within 24 hours
5. CD may restrict participation until forms received

### Issue: Room Key Doesn't Work
**Solution:**
1. Test key immediately during check-in
2. Contact campus facilities
3. Get replacement key
4. Document key issue
5. May take time, have student wait comfortably

### Issue: Student Arrives at Wrong Time
**Solution:**
1. Check in student anyway
2. Adjust as needed
3. May need to wait for mentor availability
4. Keep student supervised and comfortable
5. Contact parents if significantly early/late

### Issue: Parents Want Extended Tour
**Solution:**
1. Balance parent needs with other arrivals
2. Assign specific mentor if time allows
3. Provide map and information
4. Set boundary on tour length during peak check-in
5. Offer to answer questions via email after

### Issue: Missing PEX Card or Materials
**Solution:**
1. Check inventory immediately
2. Assign substitute if available
3. Note in tracker
4. Resolve before student needs item
5. CD coordinates replacement

### Issue: Roommate Conflict at Check-In
**Solution:**
1. Listen to concerns
2. Encourage giving it a try
3. Explain roommate contract process
4. Monitor first 24 hours
5. Be prepared to switch rooms if truly incompatible
6. Document conversation

### Issue: Student Late for Check-Out Deadline
**Solution:**
1. Contact parents immediately
2. Assign mentor to supervise
3. Provide dinner if needed
4. Stay calm and professional
5. May incur late fee per policy
6. Document thoroughly
