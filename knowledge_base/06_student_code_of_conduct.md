# Student Code of Conduct - Summer Springboard 2026

## Purpose and Philosophy

The Summer Springboard Code of Conduct exists to create a safe, respectful, and inclusive learning environment for all participants. This code applies to ALL students during the entirety of their program, including academic time, free time, excursions, and all campus activities.

**WHY THIS EXISTS:**
- Ensures student safety and wellbeing
- Creates an environment conducive to learning
- Protects students from harmful behaviors
- Establishes clear expectations and boundaries
- Provides fair and consistent consequences

## Core Expectations

### Respect for Self, Others, and Property

**Students must:**
- Treat all students, staff, instructors, and campus community members with respect
- Use appropriate language (no profanity, slurs, or derogatory terms)
- Follow instructions from Summer Springboard staff
- Respect personal and university property
- Maintain appropriate noise levels
- Respect dormitory quiet hours

**WHY:** Creating a respectful environment allows everyone to feel safe, valued, and able to focus on learning.

### Academic Integrity

**Students must:**
- Attend all scheduled classes and workshops
- Arrive on time to all activities
- Participate actively in academic programs
- Complete assignments honestly and independently
- Give credit for ideas and work from others

**WHY:** Academic integrity is foundational to learning and prepares students for college and professional environments.

## Prohibited Behaviors and Consequences

### Immediate Dismissal Offenses (Zero Tolerance)

The following behaviors result in **immediate dismissal** from the program with no refund:

#### 1. Alcohol and Drugs

**PROHIBITED:**
- Possession, use, or distribution of alcohol
- Possession, use, or distribution of illegal drugs or controlled substances
- Possession, use, or distribution of marijuana (including in states where legal for adults)
- Possession, use, or distribution of vaping devices, e-cigarettes, or tobacco products
- Being under the influence of any prohibited substance
- Sharing prescription medications

**WHY:** Student safety is paramount. Substance use impairs judgment, violates university policies, creates legal liability, and puts all participants at risk.

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- Immediate dismissal from program
- Parents notified immediately
- Parents responsible for immediate pickup or travel arrangements
- No refund of program fees
- Police may be notified depending on circumstances
- Permanent ban from all future Summer Springboard programs

**EXAMPLE:** Student brings vodka in water bottle, shares with roommates. All students involved are dismissed.

#### 2. Sexual Activity and Inappropriate Behavior

**PROHIBITED:**
- Any sexual activity between students
- Being in another student's room after hours
- Inappropriate touching or physical contact
- Sexual harassment of any kind
- Sharing intimate images or videos
- Sexting or sexual communication

**WHY:** Protects students from exploitation, maintains appropriate boundaries, and complies with legal requirements regarding minors.

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- Immediate dismissal from program
- Parents of all students involved notified immediately
- Full incident report filed
- Police notified if appropriate
- No refund of program fees
- Permanent ban from all future Summer Springboard programs

**EXAMPLE:** Two students found kissing in dorm room after lights out - both dismissed.

#### 3. Violence and Threats

**PROHIBITED:**
- Physical violence or fighting
- Threatening violence against any person
- Possession of weapons of any kind
- Threatening or intimidating behavior
- Bullying (physical, verbal, or cyber)

**WHY:** All participants have the right to feel physically and emotionally safe at all times.

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- Immediate dismissal from program
- Police notified
- Parents notified immediately
- No refund of program fees
- Permanent ban from all future Summer Springboard programs

**EXAMPLE:** Student threatens to "beat up" another student if they don't leave their friend alone - immediate dismissal.

#### 4. Leaving Campus Without Permission

**PROHIBITED:**
- Leaving campus boundaries without approved leave request
- Failing to check out through REACH when leaving campus
- Visiting unapproved locations
- Traveling with unapproved individuals

**WHY:** Staff must know where students are at all times for safety and emergency response. Unauthorized departures create severe liability and safety concerns.

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- First offense: May result in immediate dismissal (assessed case-by-case)
- Parents notified immediately
- If dismissed: No refund, permanent ban
- If not dismissed: Written contract, final warning, restricted privileges

**EXAMPLE:** Commuter student invites residential student to their house without approved leave request - both students face dismissal consideration.

#### 5. Theft

**PROHIBITED:**
- Stealing from any person or location
- Shoplifting
- Taking university or SSB property without permission
- Using another person's belongings without permission

**WHY:** Respect for property is fundamental. Theft is illegal and violates community trust.

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- Immediate dismissal from program
- Police notified
- Restitution required
- Parents notified immediately
- No refund of program fees
- Permanent ban from all future Summer Springboard programs

**EXAMPLE:** Student steals alcohol from grocery store during excursion - immediate dismissal, police called.

#### 6. Vandalism and Property Damage

**PROHIBITED:**
- Intentional damage to university property
- Intentional damage to SSB property
- Intentional damage to other students' property
- Graffiti or defacing property
- Tampering with safety equipment (fire alarms, sprinklers, etc.)

**WHY:** Vandalism creates safety hazards, financial burden, and disrespects the university hosting the program.

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- Immediate dismissal from program (for intentional damage)
- Full cost of repair/replacement charged to family
- Police notified if significant damage
- Parents notified immediately
- No refund of program fees
- Permanent ban from all future Summer Springboard programs

**EXAMPLE:** Students pull fire alarm as "prank" - immediate dismissal, fines from university and fire department.

### Escalating Consequences Offenses

These behaviors follow a progressive discipline system:

#### Verbal Warning → Written Contract → Dismissal

#### 1. Violation of Curfew and Quiet Hours

**CURFEW RULES:**
- Residential students must be in residence hall by specified curfew (typically 9:30pm)
- Students must be in their own rooms by lights out (typically 10:30pm)
- Quiet hours begin at lights out
- Students may not visit other students' rooms after lights out

**VIOLATIONS:**
- Out of room after lights out
- In another student's room after lights out
- Excessive noise during quiet hours
- Violating floor/building access restrictions

**WHY:** Adequate sleep is essential for learning, safety, and wellbeing. Curfew violations also create supervision gaps.

**CONSEQUENCES:**

**First Offense - Unofficial Verbal Warning:**
- Conversation with mentor
- Reminder of policy and expectations
- No parent call
- Documented in staff notes

**Second Offense - Official Verbal Warning:**
- Meeting with Campus Director
- Official warning documented
- Parent phone call home
- Documented in incident report

**Third Offense - Written Behavior Contract:**
- Formal meeting with Campus Director
- Written contract signed by student
- Parent phone call home
- Next violation results in dismissal
- Restricted privileges (determined case-by-case)

**Fourth Offense - Dismissal:**
- Immediate dismissal from program
- Parents notified, must arrange pickup
- No refund

**EXAMPLE:** Student found in hallway at 11:00pm three nights in a row - first time gets unofficial warning, second time gets official warning with parent call, third time gets written contract.

#### 2. Disrespect to Staff or Peers

**VIOLATIONS:**
- Rude or disrespectful language toward staff
- Refusing to follow staff instructions
- Talking back or arguing with staff
- Name-calling or verbal bullying
- Excluding or ostracizing other students
- Making fun of other students

**WHY:** Respectful communication is essential for a positive community. Staff authority must be maintained for safety.

**CONSEQUENCES:**

**First Offense - Official Verbal Warning:**
- Immediate conversation with Campus Director
- Official warning documented
- Parent phone call home
- Apology required

**Second Offense - Written Behavior Contract:**
- Formal meeting with Campus Director
- Written contract signed by student
- Parent phone call home
- Next violation results in dismissal

**Third Offense - Dismissal:**
- Immediate dismissal from program
- Parents notified
- No refund

**EXAMPLE:** Student tells mentor "You can't tell me what to do" when asked to put phone away - immediate official verbal warning and parent call.

#### 3. Technology Misuse

**TECHNOLOGY RULES:**
- Use devices appropriately during designated free time
- No devices during classes unless instructor permits
- No photographing or recording others without consent
- No cyberbullying or harmful online behavior
- No sharing private information about program or participants

**VIOLATIONS:**
- Using phone during class without permission
- Taking photos/videos of students without consent
- Posting about program participants on social media without consent
- Cyberbullying through any platform
- Accessing inappropriate content
- Using devices to violate other policies (e.g., coordinating rule violations)

**WHY:** Technology can enhance learning but also create distractions, privacy violations, and opportunities for harm.

**CONSEQUENCES:**

**First Offense - Unofficial Verbal Warning:**
- Reminder of policy
- Device may be confiscated temporarily
- Returned at end of day

**Second Offense - Official Verbal Warning:**
- Official warning documented
- Parent phone call home
- Device confiscated for 24 hours
- Incident report filed

**Third Offense - Written Behavior Contract:**
- Formal meeting with Campus Director
- Written contract signed
- Device confiscated for remainder of session
- Parent phone call home
- Device only returned at program end

**EXAMPLE:** Student uses phone during class to text friends - first time phone confiscated until end of class with reminder, second time official warning and parent call with 24-hour confiscation.

#### 4. Dress Code Violations

**DRESS CODE REQUIREMENTS:**
- Clothing must be appropriate for educational environment
- No clothing with offensive language or images
- No clothing that promotes drugs, alcohol, or violence
- Clothing must cover torso and undergarments
- Footwear required at all times in public areas
- Follow university dress codes for specific facilities (labs, dining halls, etc.)

**WHY:** Appropriate dress maintains professional environment and respects diverse community members.

**CONSEQUENCES:**

**First Offense - Unofficial Verbal Warning:**
- Asked to change immediately
- Reminder of policy

**Second Offense - Official Verbal Warning:**
- Required to change immediately
- Official warning documented
- Parent phone call home

**Third Offense - Written Behavior Contract:**
- Formal meeting with Campus Director
- Written contract
- Parent phone call home

**EXAMPLE:** Student wears shirt with profanity - asked to change with reminder of policy.

#### 5. Failure to Attend Required Activities

**ATTENDANCE REQUIREMENTS:**
- All academic classes
- All College Readiness Workshops
- All scheduled excursions
- All mandatory meetings and orientations
- All meal times (or approved alternative arrangements)

**VIOLATIONS:**
- Skipping class or workshop
- Arriving late to scheduled activities
- Leaving early without permission
- Not showing up for mandatory activities

**WHY:** Students and families pay for full program experience. Absences disrupt learning and create supervision challenges.

**CONSEQUENCES:**

**First Offense - Unofficial Verbal Warning:**
- Conversation about importance of attendance
- Discussion of reason for absence
- May require makeup work

**Second Offense - Official Verbal Warning:**
- Official warning documented
- Parent phone call home
- Required attendance contract

**Third Offense - Written Behavior Contract:**
- Formal meeting with Campus Director
- Written contract
- Parent phone call home
- Restricted privileges

**Fourth Offense - Dismissal:**
- Dismissal from program
- No refund

**SPECIAL NOTE:** Falsifying attendance (having someone else check in for you, or falsifying records) is immediate dismissal.

**EXAMPLE:** Student sleeps through breakfast and first class - unofficial warning and conversation. Next time, official warning and parent call.

#### 6. Violating COVID-19 or Health Protocols

**HEALTH PROTOCOLS:**
- Follow all current health and safety guidelines
- Report illness to staff immediately
- Attend mandatory health check-ins
- Follow isolation/quarantine requirements if ill
- Use medications only as prescribed

**WHY:** Protecting community health is everyone's responsibility.

**CONSEQUENCES:**

**First Offense - Official Verbal Warning:**
- Immediate compliance required
- Official warning documented
- Parent phone call home

**Second Offense - Written Behavior Contract:**
- Formal meeting
- Written contract
- May require isolation or dismissal depending on severity

**EXAMPLE:** Student fails to report feeling ill before attending class - official warning and parent call.

## Special Circumstances and Considerations

### Romantic Relationships

**POLICY:** While students may have pre-existing friendships or relationships, the following rules apply:

**Prohibited:**
- Public displays of affection beyond brief, appropriate hugs
- Being alone together in private spaces (dorm rooms, etc.)
- Any sexual activity
- Meeting in private after lights out

**WHY:** Romantic relationships can create power dynamics, exclusion of other students, and inappropriate situations. Students are minors in a supervised program.

**CONSEQUENCES:** Same as "Sexual Activity" policy above - immediate dismissal possible.

### Harassment and Bullying

**DEFINITION:**
Bullying is repeated, intentional behavior meant to hurt, intimidate, or exclude someone. This includes:
- Physical bullying (hitting, pushing, taking belongings)
- Verbal bullying (name-calling, insults, threats)
- Social bullying (exclusion, spreading rumors, public embarrassment)
- Cyberbullying (harmful texts, posts, messages)

**POLICY:** Summer Springboard has ZERO TOLERANCE for bullying and harassment.

**CONSEQUENCES:**

**First Incident:**
- Immediate investigation
- Meeting with all students involved
- Parents notified
- Official warning OR immediate dismissal (depending on severity)
- May require separation of students
- Required behavioral contract

**Second Incident:**
- Immediate dismissal from program
- No refund

**FOR SEVERE BULLYING (threats, sustained harassment, coordinated bullying):**
- Immediate dismissal
- No second chance

**REPORTING:** Students who witness or experience bullying MUST report to any staff member immediately. Staff will take all reports seriously.

**EXAMPLE:** Student repeatedly makes fun of another student's accent and excludes them from group activities - immediate parent notification, investigation, behavioral contract, and possible dismissal.

### Mental Health and Wellbeing

**STUDENT RESPONSIBILITY:**

## Discipline Process

### Investigation

**When a code of conduct violation is reported:**

1. **Immediate Response:**
   - Staff ensure all students are safe
   - Separate involved students if needed
   - Initial information gathering

2. **Investigation:**
   - Individual conversations with all involved students
   - Gather witness statements
   - Review evidence (REACH records, photos, etc.)
   - Maintain neutral, fact-finding approach

3. **Determination:**
   - Campus Director reviews all information
   - Consults with Program Director if needed
   - Determines if violation occurred
   - Determines appropriate consequence

4. **Communication:**
   - Meet with student(s) to explain findings
   - Explain consequences
   - Contact parents
   - Document in incident report

**TIMELINE:** Investigations completed within 24-48 hours when possible.

### Levels of Consequences

#### Level 1: Unofficial Verbal Warning
- Conversation with mentor or staff
- Reminder of expectations
- No formal documentation
- No parent contact

#### Level 2: Official Verbal Warning
- Meeting with Campus Director
- Official documentation in incident report
- Parent phone call home
- Explanation of next consequences if behavior continues

#### Level 3: Written Behavior Contract
- Formal meeting with Campus Director
- Written contract outlining:
  - Specific behaviors that must stop
  - Specific behaviors expected going forward
  - Consequences if contract violated
  - Timeline for contract (usually remainder of session)
- Parent phone call home
- Student signs contract
- May include restrictions (no excursions, limited free time, etc.)

#### Level 4: Dismissal from Program
- Immediate removal from program activities
- Meeting with Campus Director and Program Director
- Parents notified immediately
- Parents arrange immediate pickup
- No refund of program fees
- May include permanent ban from future programs
- Full incident report filed

### Appeals Process

**Limited appeals available for dismissal decisions:**

1. Student/family may request appeal within 24 hours of dismissal notification
2. Appeal must be in writing to Program Director
3. Appeal must include new information not available during original investigation
4. Program Director reviews with Risk Management Director
5. Decision on appeal is final
6. Student must leave campus during appeal process

**NOTE:** Immediate safety dismissals (violence, substances, leaving campus) are typically not eligible for appeal.

### Parent Notification

**Parents will be notified:**
- Any official verbal warning
- All written behavior contracts
- All dismissals
- Any injury or medical issue
- Any significant behavioral concern
- Repeated minor concerns that form pattern

**HOW:** Phone call from Campus Director or Program Director, followed by email documentation.

**WHEN:** Within 24 hours of incident (immediately for emergencies or dismissals).
