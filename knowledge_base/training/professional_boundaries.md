# Professional Boundaries and Staff Policies

## Core Principle: Students Are Always Watching

Operating Principle #3: **Students are always watching and following our example.**

Everything you do models behavior for students. Your professionalism, boundaries, and conduct set the standard.

## Banned Practices (Zero Tolerance)

### NEVER Allowed
1. **Sharing a room with a student**
2. **Sharing a private space with a student** (without other students present)
3. **Being alone with a student in a closed room** (without other students present)
4. **Touching a student when no one else is around**
5. **Flirting with students**
6. **Having sex with a student**
7. **Allowing students to kiss**
8. **Engaging in a relationship with fellow mentors or Directors**

### Consequences
Violation of these policies results in immediate dismissal and potential legal consequences.

## Acceptable Practices

### What IS Allowed
- Being in a room with **3 or more students** present
- Accepting a hug from a student **in an open space with other students around**
- Hanging out with another staff member during your free time
- High-fives, fist bumps, side hugs in public settings with witnesses

### The "Two Deep" Rule
Never be alone with a student. Always have:
- Another adult present, OR
- Multiple students (3+) present, OR
- Be in a public, visible space

## Communication Boundaries

### With Students

**Appropriate:**
- Communication through **Reach app only**
- Group messages to mentor groups
- Direct messages on Reach for program-related information
- Face-to-face conversations in public spaces

**NOT Appropriate:**
- Sharing personal phone number
- Adding students on personal social media (Instagram, Snapchat, TikTok, etc.)
- Texting from personal phone
- Creating group chats outside of Reach
- Connecting on social media during or after program

### Social Media Policy

**Strict Rules:**
- **NO photos with students** on your personal social media
- **NO tagging students** in any posts
- **NO adding students** as friends/followers during program
- You can post photos of campus, activities, scenery (without students)

**Why This Matters:**
- Protects students' privacy
- Maintains professional boundaries
- Prevents appearance of favoritism
- Protects you legally

### Post-Program Contact
Even after the program ends:
- Do not connect with students on social media
- Do not maintain personal relationships with students
- If a student reaches out, politely decline and keep it professional
- Remember: they are minors, you are in a position of authority

## Physical Boundaries

### Appropriate Touch
- High-fives
- Fist bumps
- Side hugs (quick, in public, with others present)
- Pats on the back (shoulder area only)
- Handshakes

**Key Rule:** Always in public, always brief, always initiated by student

### Inappropriate Touch
- Full frontal hugs when alone
- Prolonged physical contact
- Touching hair, face, or other personal areas
- Sitting on laps or having students sit on yours
- Holding hands
- Any touch that could be misinterpreted

### If a Student Initiates Inappropriate Contact
- Gently redirect: "Let's keep it to a high-five"
- Step back physically
- Address if it continues: "I need you to respect my personal space"
- Report to CD if it persists

## Emotional Boundaries

### Supporting Without Over-Involvement

**Appropriate Support:**
- Listening to student concerns
- Validating feelings
- Connecting them with resources
- Being consistently present and reliable

**Over-Involvement (Avoid):**
- Becoming the student's therapist
- Sharing your own personal problems with students
- Making yourself the only person they talk to
- Promising to "always be there" after the program
- Creating dependency

### Warning Signs You're Over-Involved
- Student only wants to talk to you
- You feel personally responsible for their happiness
- You're sharing personal information about yourself
- Other staff notice the student seeks you out exclusively
- You feel emotionally drained by one student's needs

### How to Maintain Healthy Distance
- Distribute your attention among all students
- Redirect students to also talk to other staff
- Keep conversations focused on program experience
- Don't share personal phone number or social media
- Remember: you're a mentor, not their best friend

## Staff-to-Staff Boundaries

### Relationships Policy
**NO romantic or sexual relationships between staff members during the program.**

**Rationale:**
- Creates perception of favoritism
- Impacts professionalism
- Distracts from student care
- Creates drama that affects the team
- Students notice everything

**Consequences:**
Both staff members will be sent home immediately.

### Professional Conduct
- Keep personal relationships professional during work hours
- Don't show favoritism to friends on staff
- Don't gossip about other staff members
- Address conflicts directly, not through others
- Maintain united front in front of students

## Privacy and Confidentiality

### Student Information

**Confidential Information:**
- Medical information (allergies, medications, diagnoses)
- Mental health history
- Gender identity (if student keeps private)
- Family situations
- Academic struggles
- Behavioral issues

**Need-to-Know Basis:**
Share information only with:
- Campus Director
- Academic Manager
- Other staff who directly supervise the student
- HQ if escalation is needed

**NEVER Share:**
- With other students
- With parents (unless you're CD and it's appropriate)
- On social media
- In casual conversation with other staff

### Mandated Reporting

**You MUST Report:**
- Suspected abuse (physical, emotional, sexual)
- Student expressing thoughts of self-harm or suicide
- Student harming others
- Illegal activity
- Violations of code of conduct

**How to Report:**
1. Tell CD immediately
2. Document what you heard/saw (exact words if possible)
3. Do not investigate on your own
4. Do not promise the student confidentiality

## Alcohol and Substance Use

### Staff Policy

**During Program:**
- **Dry campus** - NO alcohol or drugs
- Even during time off, no alcohol while you could be called back
- No smoking or vaping on campus or in front of students

**During Designated Outings:**
- Leadership may organize designated times for adult beverages off campus
- Must be completely separate from students
- Must maintain professional behavior
- Must be able to return if emergency occurs

**Between Sessions:**
- Personal time is yours
- Still represent SSB - maintain professionalism
- Must be ready to return if needed

### Consequences for Violations
- Immediate dismissal
- No second chances
- Reference may be affected

## Electronic Devices and Social Media

### Phone Use During Programming

**Minimize Phone Use:**
- Be present during student activities
- Don't scroll social media during programs
- Emergency calls/texts are acceptable
- Step away from students to take personal calls

**Model Good Behavior:**
- Students notice when you're on your phone
- Put phone away during meals, activities, conversations
- Don't take calls during important moments
- Show that people are more important than screens

### Photos and Videos

**For Program Use:**
- Take photos of students during activities (SSB will use these)
- Upload to designated Google Drive daily
- Use for blogs and marketing
- Students signed photo release

**For Personal Use:**
- NO posting photos with students on your personal accounts
- You can take photos of campus, scenery, your food
- Be mindful of what's in the background

## Time Off and Personal Boundaries

### Time Off Policy
- **At least 1 evening (6pm - 8am) off per week**
- One mentor off per night (or two if ratios maintained)
- No time off Saturday/Sunday nights or final night of session
- Cannot travel more than 2 hours from campus during programming

### Between Sessions
- Off from last Friday (once CD dismisses you) until Sunday 9am
- May be needed if students stay for back-to-back sessions
- Can be contacted in emergency - must respond

### Setting Personal Boundaries

**It's Okay To:**
- Need alone time to recharge
- Take care of your own mental health
- Ask for support when you're struggling
- Set limits on emotional labor
- Take your time off seriously

**Not Okay To:**
- Completely check out emotionally
- Refuse to help in emergencies during "off time"
- Complain to students about being tired
- Show resentment about your workload to students

## Avoiding Favoritism

### The Problem with Favorites
- Other students notice and feel excluded
- Creates perception of unfairness
- Can make favorite student uncomfortable
- Other staff lose respect for you
- Undermines team culture

### How to Avoid It
- Distribute your attention equally
- Rotate who you sit with at meals
- Call on different students during discussions
- Celebrate different students' achievements
- Check yourself if you're spending too much time with one student

### If You Notice Connection
It's natural to connect more with some students. That's okay.

**What to Do:**
- Acknowledge it to yourself
- Consciously balance your time
- Don't let it affect your professional judgment
- Don't give special privileges
- Keep it appropriate

## Conflict of Interest

### When You Know a Student Before Program

**Disclose Immediately:**
If you:
- Went to school with a student
- Know their family
- Have any pre-existing relationship

**Why:**
- Leadership can make appropriate group assignments
- Prevents appearance of favoritism
- Protects you and the student

### Former Students or Younger Siblings
- Disclose the connection
- Don't promise special treatment
- Treat them like any other student in front of peers
- Check in with them privately about how to handle it

## Red Flags and Warning Signs

### Student Behavior That Requires Attention
- Student seeks you out constantly
- Student shares information that feels too personal
- Student makes comments about your appearance
- Student tries to be alone with you
- Student gives you gifts
- Student says they "love you" or similar
- Student asks about your personal life repeatedly

### Your Response
- Maintain boundaries firmly
- Redirect to appropriate topics
- Don't be alone with this student
- Document the behavior
- Report to CD

### Staff Behavior That's Problematic
If you see another staff member:
- Spending excessive time with one student
- Being alone with students
- Sharing personal contact information
- Engaging in inappropriate touch
- Showing favoritism
- Violating any policies

**You Must Report It:**
- Tell CD immediately
- Document what you saw
- Don't confront the staff member alone
- This protects students and the program

## Self-Assessment Questions

Ask yourself regularly:

**Am I maintaining appropriate boundaries if:**
- Would I do/say this in front of the CD?
- Would I be comfortable with this being photographed?
- Could this be misinterpreted?
- Am I treating all students equally?
- Would I want someone treating my younger sibling this way?
- Am I putting student needs before my personal wants?

If the answer to any of these makes you uncomfortable, **adjust your behavior**.

## When in Doubt

**Ask yourself:**
1. Is this for the student's benefit or mine?
2. Would I do this in front of my supervisor?
3. Could this be misunderstood?
4. Does this maintain my professional role?

**If you're unsure, ask your CD before proceeding.**

## Resources and Support

### If You're Struggling with Boundaries
- Talk to your CD or PD
- Ask for guidance before situations arise
- Debrief difficult situations with leadership
- Don't try to handle everything alone

### If a Boundary Has Been Crossed
- Report immediately
- Don't hide mistakes
- Better to address early than let it escalate
- You can recover from honesty, not from cover-ups

## Remember

**Professional boundaries protect everyone:**
- They protect students from harm
- They protect you from accusations
- They maintain program integrity
- They allow you to do your job effectively
- They create a safe environment for learning

**When you maintain boundaries, you model healthy relationships for students. This is one of the most important things you do as a mentor.**
