# Staff Wellness and Self-Care

## Operating Principle: We Take Our Work Seriously So We Can Have Fun

Summer Springboard programs are intense, rewarding, and demanding. Taking care of yourself isn't selfish - it's essential for taking care of students.

## The Reality of Summer Program Work

### What to Expect
- Long hours (often 6am breakfast to 10:30pm bed checks)
- Emotionally demanding work
- Limited personal time
- Living where you work
- Constant "on" mode
- Little privacy
- Intense team dynamics
- Unexpected challenges daily

### Why It's Worth It
- Life-changing impact on students
- Professional growth
- Leadership experience
- Lifelong friendships with staff
- Unique summer experience
- Building your resume
- Personal growth and challenge

## Time Off Policy

### During Program

**Minimum Guaranteed:**
- At least 1 evening (6pm - 8am) off per session
- Time off starts once commuter students depart
- Back on duty at breakfast the following morning

**Flexibility:**
- CDs can give more nights off if program permits
- Smaller programs may have more time off
- Cannot have time off Saturday, Sunday, or final night of session

**Important Rules:**
- Can't travel more than 2 hours from campus (for emergencies)
- Must respond if contacted during time off
- Must be back on time

### Between Sessions
- Off from last Friday (once CD dismisses) until Sunday 9am
- May be needed if students stay back-to-back
- Can be contacted for emergencies
- Exact times based on program needs - CD has final discretion

### Making the Most of Time Off

**Use It Wisely:**
- Actually rest - don't just run errands
- Get off campus if possible
- Connect with friends/family
- Do something enjoyable
- Catch up on sleep
- Eat a meal that's not dining hall food

**Don't:**
- Feel guilty for taking your time off
- Stay on campus scrolling social media
- Use it to do program work
- Complain about not having enough time

## Preventing Burnout

### Signs of Burnout

**Physical:**
- Exhaustion that sleep doesn't fix
- Getting sick frequently
- Headaches
- Stomach issues
- Tension in neck/shoulders

**Emotional:**
- Irritability with students or staff
- Crying easily
- Feeling detached or numb
- Loss of enthusiasm
- Dread going to work

**Mental:**
- Difficulty concentrating
- Forgetting things
- Making mistakes you normally wouldn't
- Negative self-talk
- Feeling overwhelmed by small tasks

**Behavioral:**
- Isolating from team
- Snapping at people
- Not sleeping well
- Overeating or not eating
- Increased phone scrolling
- Avoiding responsibilities

### Prevention Strategies

**Daily:**
- Eat three meals (don't skip breakfast)
- Drink water throughout the day
- Move your body (even 10 minutes)
- Get outside
- Take 5 deep breaths between activities
- Laugh
- Connect with at least one other staff member

**Weekly:**
- Take your time off seriously
- Check in with yourself honestly
- Sleep in on your day off
- Do something fun
- Call a friend or family member
- Process your week with someone

**Throughout Program:**
- Maintain boundaries with students and staff
- Ask for help when you need it
- Celebrate small wins
- Remember your "why"
- Keep perspective (it's temporary)

## Self-Care Strategies

### Physical Self-Care

**Sleep:**
- Aim for 7-8 hours
- Go to bed at a consistent time
- Avoid screens before bed
- Use earplugs if needed
- Take a nap on your day off

**Nutrition:**
- Eat regular meals
- Keep healthy snacks in your room
- Drink water (not just coffee)
- Don't live on dining hall french fries
- Treat yourself to a meal out occasionally

**Movement:**
- Use campus gym
- Walk between activities
- Stretch during breaks
- Join students in sports club sometimes
- Take stairs instead of elevator

**Basic Needs:**
- Shower daily (sounds obvious, but you'll be tired)
- Clean your room periodically
- Do laundry before you're desperate
- Take care of minor health issues promptly

### Emotional Self-Care

**Connection:**
- Build relationships with other staff
- Call family/friends during time off
- Don't isolate when you're struggling
- Laugh with your team
- Create inside jokes

**Expression:**
- Journal about your experience
- Talk to someone you trust
- Cry if you need to (in private)
- Celebrate victories (even small ones)
- Acknowledge hard days

**Boundaries:**
- It's okay to say "I need a minute"
- Don't take student struggles home emotionally
- Separate work from rest time
- Let go of what you can't control

### Mental Self-Care

**Perspective:**
- This is temporary (2 weeks)
- Not everything is an emergency
- You can't control everything
- Mistakes are learning opportunities
- You don't have to be perfect

**Processing:**
- Debrief difficult situations with leadership
- Talk through challenges with fellow staff
- Learn from each day
- Notice your growth
- Give yourself credit

**Boundaries:**
- You can't fix every student's problems
- You're a mentor, not a therapist
- It's okay to not have all the answers
- Ask for help when you need it

## Working with Your Team

### Building Supportive Culture

**For Each Other:**
- Check in on teammates who seem stressed
- Offer to help when someone's overwhelmed
- Cover for someone who needs a break
- Celebrate each other's wins
- Give encouragement freely

**Creating Team Wellness:**
- Team meals together
- Shout-outs at meetings
- Inside jokes and laughter
- Shared challenges
- Supporting each other through hard days

### When a Teammate is Struggling

**Notice the Signs:**
- Withdrawn or isolated
- Snapping at students or staff
- Not sleeping or eating
- Making unusual mistakes
- Expressing hopelessness

**What to Do:**
1. Check in privately: "Hey, I've noticed you seem [observation]. Are you okay?"
2. Listen without judgment
3. Encourage them to talk to CD
4. Offer practical help: "Can I cover room checks so you can rest?"
5. Follow up: "How are you doing today?"

**What Not to Do:**
- Gossip about their struggles
- Try to be their therapist
- Enable unhealthy behavior
- Ignore warning signs
- Take on all their responsibilities

### When YOU'RE Struggling

**Ask for Help:**
- Talk to your CD or PD
- "I'm feeling overwhelmed. Can we problem-solve together?"
- Don't wait until you're in crisis
- Asking for help is strength, not weakness

**Be Honest:**
- "I need to adjust my schedule"
- "I'm not okay right now"
- "I need support"
- "This is harder than I expected"

**Create a Plan:**
- What specifically would help?
- What can you do yourself?
- What do you need from leadership?
- What's the timeline?

## Managing Stress in the Moment

### Quick Reset Techniques

**When You Have 30 Seconds:**
- Take 5 deep breaths
- Step outside for fresh air
- Drink cold water
- Shake out your body
- Close your eyes and reset

**When You Have 5 Minutes:**
- Walk around the building
- Call someone supportive
- Journal quick thoughts
- Stretch
- Listen to a favorite song

**When You Have 30 Minutes:**
- Take a real break
- Eat a proper snack/meal
- Talk to another staff member
- Do something fun
- Move your body intentionally

### Difficult Moment Strategies

**After a Hard Conversation:**
- Debrief with CD or another staff
- Don't process alone
- Learn from it
- Let it go
- Don't replay it all night

**After a Student Crisis:**
- Make sure student is safe
- Report to appropriate people
- Process your emotions separately
- Don't take it home
- Seek support if needed

**After Team Conflict:**
- Address it directly, don't let it fester
- Focus on solution, not blame
- Involve CD if needed
- Move forward
- Don't hold grudges

## Common Challenges and Solutions

### "I'm So Tired"
- **Cause:** Not enough sleep, too much emotional labor
- **Solution:** Prioritize sleep. Go to bed earlier. Use your day off to catch up.

### "I Miss Home"
- **Cause:** Being away is hard
- **Solution:** Call family/friends during time off. Remember it's temporary. Focus on the positive aspects.

### "The Students Are Driving Me Crazy"
- **Cause:** Normal fatigue, boundary issues
- **Solution:** Take a break. Remember they're teenagers. Maintain boundaries. Process with team.

### "My Team is Dysfunctional"
- **Cause:** Different work styles, stress, lack of communication
- **Solution:** Address conflicts directly. Talk to CD. Focus on students. Don't gossip.

### "I Don't Know If I Can Make It"
- **Cause:** Burnout, unrealistic expectations, lack of support
- **Solution:** Talk to CD immediately. Problem-solve together. Adjust what you can. Remember your why.

### "I Feel Like I'm Failing"
- **Cause:** Perfectionism, comparing to others, mistakes
- **Solution:** Nobody's perfect. Learn from mistakes. Ask for feedback. Celebrate what you're doing well.

## Red Flags: When to Escalate Your Own Wellness

### Talk to Leadership Immediately If:
- You're having thoughts of harming yourself
- You're experiencing panic attacks
- You can't sleep for multiple days
- You can't eat or can't stop eating
- You're having angry outbursts
- You feel completely hopeless
- You're unable to function in your role

### These Are Emergencies
Don't wait. Don't tough it out. Talk to your CD or PD right away.

**Resources:**
- Campus Director
- Program Director
- Crisis Text Line: Text HOME to 741741
- National Suicide Prevention Lifeline: 988

## Creating Your Wellness Plan

### Before Program Starts

**Set Intentions:**
- What do you want from this experience?
- What are your personal wellness non-negotiables?
- What are your warning signs of stress?
- Who will you reach out to if you're struggling?

**Prepare:**
- Bring comfort items from home
- Pack healthy snacks
- Bring exercise clothes
- Create a playlist that calms you
- Identify your coping strategies

### During Program

**Daily Check-In:**
- How am I feeling physically?
- How am I feeling emotionally?
- What do I need today?
- What can I celebrate?

**Weekly Review:**
- What went well this week?
- What was challenging?
- How am I taking care of myself?
- What needs to change?
- What support do I need?

### After Program

**Decompress:**
- Give yourself time to adjust back
- Process the experience
- Rest and recover
- Reconnect with regular life
- Celebrate what you accomplished

**Reflect:**
- What did you learn?
- What are you proud of?
- What would you do differently?
- How did you grow?
