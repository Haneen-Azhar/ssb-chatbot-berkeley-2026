## Staff Policies and Prohibited Practices

### Banned Practices (Immediate Termination)

These practices are strictly prohibited and violate Summer Springboard policy:

1. **Sharing a room with a student**
2. **Sharing a private space with a student** (unless 3+ other students present)
3. **Being alone with a student in a closed room** (unless 3+ other students present)
4. **Touching a student when no one else is around**
5. **Flirting with students**
6. **Having sex with a student**
7. **Allowing students to kiss or engage in sexual acts**
8. **Engaging in a romantic or sexual relationship with fellow mentors or Directors during the program**

**WHY:** These policies exist to protect both students and staff from allegations of misconduct, maintain appropriate boundaries, and ensure student safety at all times.

**CONSEQUENCES:** Immediate dismissal from program, potential legal consequences, and permanent ban from SSB employment.

### Acceptable Practices

Staff MAY:
- Be in a room with 3 or more students present
- Accept a hug from a student in an open space with other students around
- Hang out with another staff member during free time (as friends/colleagues only)

### Social Media Policy

**POLICY:** No photos with students posted on personal social media accounts.

**WHY:** Protects student privacy, prevents boundary violations, and maintains professional relationships.

**WHAT YOU CAN DO:**
- Take photos for SSB official use only
- Share photos through official SSB channels only
- Tag SSB official accounts when appropriate

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- First violation: Written warning
- Second violation: Dismissal from program

### Alcohol and Substance Policy

**POLICY:** No alcohol or substance use during program time, including nights and weekends while on duty.

**SPECIFIC RULES:**
- No drinking on campus at any time
- No drinking during designated staff outings if students are present
- May drink responsibly during approved staff-only time off, off-campus
- Must not be under the influence when returning to campus

**WHY:** Staff must be ready to respond to emergencies 24/7, model appropriate behavior, and maintain professional standards.

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- Any violation: Immediate dismissal from program

### No Visitors Policy

**POLICY:** Staff may not have visitors on campus during program sessions.

**WHY:** Maintains focus on students, prevents distractions, and ensures proper supervision ratios.

**CONSEQUENCES:**
- First violation: Written warning
- Second violation: Dismissal from program

### Staff-to-Student Ratio Requirements

**REQUIRED RATIOS:**
- High School programs: 10:1 (10 students per 1 mentor)
- Middle School programs: 8:1 (8 students per 1 mentor)

**ENFORCEMENT:**
- Ratios must be maintained at ALL times during program activities
- When staff take time off, remaining staff must still meet ratio requirements
- Campus Director responsible for ensuring compliance

**WHY:** Adequate supervision ensures student safety, allows for individualized attention, and meets insurance and legal requirements.

### Electronic Device Usage Around Students

**POLICY:** Staff should be present and engaged when interacting with students.

**GUIDELINES:**
- Put phones away during meals, activities, and conversations with students

## Staff Coaching and Performance Management

### Performance Classification Levels (Stoplight System)

#### GREEN LEVEL - Coaching and Pattern Monitoring

**Definition:** Minor infractions, learning moments, or first-time mistakes that don't impact student safety.

**Examples:**
- Late to morning check-in (first few times)
- Not wearing staff lanyard
- Unprepared for evening activity
- Providing students with incorrect information
- Requesting materials last minute

**Response:**
- Have coaching conversation immediately
- Complete Green-Level Staff Incident & Performance Report
- Document in Staff Performance Tracker
- No formal warning issued

**Timeline:** Week 1 is considered adjustment period - expect mistakes and prioritize coaching.

#### YELLOW LEVEL - Verbal Warning and Coaching Tracking

**Definition:** Repeated green-level issues, or single incidents that show poor judgment but don't immediately endanger students.

**Examples:**
- 4+ green-level reports for same issue
- Being late causes student to be left unsupervised
- Minor policy violation (e.g., phone use during student time)
- Unprofessional communication with parent

**Response:**
- Formal conversation with 2 staff members present
- Issue verbal warning
- Complete Yellow-Level Staff Incident & Performance Report
- Document specific behavior, expectations, and consequences
- Set clear improvement timeline
- PD notified

**Documentation:** Must include what happened, what was said, staff member's response, and action plan.

#### ORANGE LEVEL - Written Warning and Probationary Period

**Definition:** Continued pattern after yellow warning, or serious policy violations that impact program integrity or student experience.

**Examples:**
- No improvement after yellow-level warning
- Repeated boundary violations
- Falsifying attendance or other records
- Disrespectful to students, parents, or team members
- Missing assigned duties repeatedly

**Response:**
- Formal meeting with Campus Director and one other staff member
- Written warning placed in file
- Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) created
- Complete Orange-Level Staff Incident & Performance Report
- Daily check-ins required
- PD and HR notified
- Probationary period (typically 3-5 days)

**PIP Must Include:**
- Specific behaviors that must change
- Measurable expectations
- Timeline for improvement
- Consequences if no improvement
- Support being provided

**Timeline:** If no improvement within probationary period, escalate to dismissal.

#### CRITICAL/RED LEVEL - Immediate Termination

**Definition:** Severe policy violations, illegal activity, or actions that endanger students.

**Examples:**
- Any banned practice (alone with student, inappropriate touching, etc.)
- Substance use during program
- Falsifying records related to student safety
- Sexual harassment or assault
- Threatening or abusive behavior
- Abandoning assigned duties leaving students unsupervised
- Any action that poses immediate danger to students

**Response:**
- Immediate removal from duties
- Meeting with Campus Director and Program Director
- Complete Critical-Level Staff Incident & Performance Report
- HQ and HR immediately notified
- Dismissal from program
- May face legal consequences

**Documentation:** Extremely detailed report required including witnesses, exact quotes, timeline, and any evidence.

### Documentation = Protection

**KEY PRINCIPLE:** If it isn't written down, it didn't happen.

**Requirements:**
- Keep all documentation OBJECTIVE and FACTUAL
- NO opinions - only observable behaviors and direct quotes
- Include dates, times, locations, witnesses
- Document immediately after incident

**When in Doubt:** Over-communicate upward to your Program Director.

### Staff Incident & Performance Report System

**Four Reporting Levels:**
1. **Green** → Observation Report (coaching level)
2. **Yellow** → Incident Report (formal warning)
3. **Orange** → Incident Report (written warning + PIP)
4. **Critical** → Incident Report (immediate dismissal)

**Who Completes:**
- Campus Director for mentor/staff issues
- Program Director for Campus Director issues
- Any leadership who witnesses concerning behavior

**Timeline:** Must be completed within 24 hours of incident.

**Where to Submit:** Staff Performance Tracker + Salesforce case system

## Sexual Harassment Policy

### SSB Commitment

Summer Springboard is committed to maintaining safe, respectful, and inclusive environments for all students, staff, and partners. Sexual harassment will not be tolerated.

### Staff Responsibilities

**All staff must:**
1. Familiarize themselves with SSB's Sexual Harassment Policy
2. Understand the reporting process and structure
3. Know their role in upholding the policy at all times
4. Complete required training before program starts

### Sexual Abuse Prevention Policy Components

**Student Orientation (Day 1):**
- Sexual Abuse Prevention Policy explained verbally
- Clear reporting structures outlined
- Cultural norms discussion
- Policy highlighted in common areas (dorms, lounges)

**Staff Training:**
- All staff/instructors sign policy outlining appropriate interactions
- Training on potential misinterpretations
- Reporting procedures

### How to Report Sexual Harassment Incidents

**Students can report to:**
- Any mentor or Campus Director (verbal)
- Sexual Harassment Claim phone number (Maria's Google Voice)
- Claim form via QR code
- safety@summerspringboard.com

**Staff can report to:**
- Campus Director or Assistant Director (verbal)
- SSB HQ staff directly
- Sexual Harassment Claim phone number
- safety@summerspringboard.com

**Instructors can report to:**
- Program Directors (verbal)

### Response Protocol

**When a report is made:**
1. Take ALL reports seriously
2. Immediate support to impacted student
3. Separate involved parties if needed
4. Document everything immediately
5. Notify Program Director and HQ immediately
6. Begin investigation within 24 hours
7. Clear follow-up with both parties
8. Incident Report completed
9. Parents notified as appropriate

**Timeline:** Investigation must begin immediately. HQ must be notified within 1 hour of report.
