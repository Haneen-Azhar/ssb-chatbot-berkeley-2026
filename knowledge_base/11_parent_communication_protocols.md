# Parent Communication Protocols - Summer Springboard 2026

## Communication Philosophy

### Core Principles

**Transparency:**
- Parents deserve timely, honest information about their students
- Share both challenges and successes
- Never hide issues hoping they'll resolve themselves

**Professionalism:**
- All communications represent Summer Springboard
- Use appropriate language and tone
- Respond promptly
- Follow established procedures

**Partnership:**
- Parents are partners in student success
- Value parent input and concerns
- Work collaboratively to support students

**Clarity:**
- Be specific and factual
- Avoid educational jargon
- Provide clear next steps
- Confirm understanding

## When to Contact Parents

### MUST Contact Parents Immediately

**Emergency Situations:**
- Any life-threatening medical emergency
- Student missing
- Serious injury
- Student dismissal from program
- Sexual harassment or assault allegation
- Any police involvement
- Natural disaster or campus emergency
- Student expressing suicidal thoughts

**Timeline:** Within minutes of incident, before or as soon as emergency services contacted.

**Who calls:** Campus Director (or Program Director if CD unavailable).

### MUST Contact Parents Within 24 Hours

**Medical Issues:**
- Visit to urgent care, ER, or doctor
- Injury requiring more than basic first aid
- Illness causing missed classes or activities
- Student taking prescription medication from campus
- Any ongoing medical monitoring

**Behavioral Issues:**
- Official verbal warning (second Code of Conduct offense)
- Written behavior contract
- Significant conflict with roommate or peers
- Concerning behavioral pattern
- Student expressing significant unhappiness with program

**Academic Concerns:**
- Student struggling significantly in class
- Student missing multiple classes
- Instructor reports concerning disengagement
- Student requesting to change courses

**Timeline:** Within 24 hours of incident or discovery of pattern.

**Who calls:** Campus Director.

### MAY Contact Parents (Staff Discretion)

**Positive Updates:**
- Student doing exceptionally well
- Notable achievement or growth
- Student particularly happy
- Positive feedback from instructors

**Minor Medical:**
- Student has cold but managing fine
- Minor injury that's been treated and student is fine
- FYI about minor issue that's resolved

**Minor Homesickness:**
- Student missing home but adjusting
- FYI that student mentioned family but is engaging in program

**Administrative Updates:**
- Schedule changes
- Excursion reminders
- Weather-related changes

**Timeline:** As appropriate or during regular check-in calls.

**Who calls:** Campus Director or assigned mentor.

### Do NOT Contact Parents Without Campus Director Approval

**Staff should NOT independently call parents about:**
- Disciplinary issues
- Medical issues beyond basic first aid
- Roommate conflicts requiring intervention
- Any sensitive or potentially escalating situation

**Why:** Campus Director needs to coordinate communication, ensure consistency, involve HQ if needed, and prevent mixed messages.

**Process:** Tell Campus Director about concern → CD determines appropriate response → CD makes call or delegates with specific talking points.

## How to Contact Parents

### Phone Calls (Preferred for Urgent/Sensitive Matters)

**Use phone calls for:**
- All emergencies
- Medical issues requiring doctor visit
- Behavioral issues (verbal warnings, behavior contracts)
- Any situation requiring conversation
- When you need immediate parent input
- Follow-up to serious situations

**Best practices:**
- Use campus Google Voice number (never personal cell phone)
- Choose quiet, private location
- Have all relevant information in front of you
- Have another staff member present if disciplinary issue
- Take notes during call
- Document call afterward

**Structure:**
1. Introduction: "Hi, this is [name] calling from Summer Springboard. I'm [student name]'s [role]. Do you have a few minutes to talk?"
2. Purpose: "I'm calling because [specific reason]."
3. Facts: Objective description of situation
4. Action taken: What's been done so far
5. Input: "What questions do you have?" or "What are your thoughts?"
6. Next steps: Clear plan going forward
7. Follow-up: "I'll call you again [when] to update you."
8. Closing: "Please feel free to call me at this number if you have any concerns."

### Emails (Appropriate for Non-Urgent Matters)

**Use emails for:**
- Administrative updates
- Schedule changes
- Positive updates
- Non-urgent FYIs
- Follow-up documentation after phone calls

**Best practices:**
- Use campus email address (never personal email)
- Professional subject line: "Update on [Student Name] - [Brief topic]"
- Clear, concise message
- Proofread before sending
- CC Campus Director on all parent emails
- Respond within 24 hours

**Structure:**
1. Greeting: "Dear Mr./Ms./Dr. [Last Name],"
2. Purpose: First sentence states why you're writing
3. Details: Factual, specific information
4. Next steps: What will happen and when
5. Closing: Invitation for questions/concerns
6. Signature: Full name, role, contact information

### Text Messages (Rarely Appropriate)

**Only use text messages for:**
- Parent-initiated text conversations (reply via campus Google Voice)
- Quick confirmations of receipt of information
- Time-sensitive logistics when parent prefers texts

**Never use text messages for:**
- Initial contact about incidents
- Disciplinary issues
- Medical emergencies
- Complex situations
- Sensitive information

## Communication Scenarios and Scripts

### Scenario 1: Medical Emergency (Life-Threatening)

**IMMEDIATE PHONE CALL**

**Script:**
"Mr./Ms. [Name], this is [Your Name] calling from Summer Springboard. [Student Name] is having a medical emergency. We have called 9-1-1 and paramedics are on their way. Here's what happened: [brief factual description]. [Student name] is [current status]. I am going to stay on the phone with you and keep you updated. One of our staff will accompany [student name] to [hospital name]. Do you have any immediate questions?"

**During call:**
- Stay on line with parent
- Provide updates as they happen
- Give parent hospital information
- Ask if parent is coming to hospital
- Provide staff contact who will be at hospital
- Remain calm and factual

**After paramedics arrive:**
"The paramedics are here now and evaluating [student name]. They are [what paramedics are doing]. [Staff name] is going to accompany [student name] to the hospital and will call you as soon as there's any update. Here is [staff name]'s number: [number]. Do you have that? And you can also call me anytime at this number."

**Follow-up:**
- Staff at hospital calls parent with updates every 30 minutes or when new information
- Campus Director follows up after student stabilized
- Daily updates until resolved

### Scenario 2: Non-Emergency Medical (Urgent Care Visit Needed)

**PHONE CALL BEFORE GOING TO URGENT CARE**

**Script:**
"Hi Mr./Ms. [Name], this is [Your Name] from Summer Springboard. I'm calling about [Student Name]. [He/She/They] came to me today with [specific symptoms - be factual]. [Student name] is safe and comfortable right now, but I think [he/she/they] should be evaluated by a doctor to be safe. I'd like to take [him/her/them] to [urgent care or ER] to get checked out. Do you have any questions about what's going on?"

**Gather information:**
- "Is there anything in [student name]'s medical history I should know related to this?"
- "Does [he/she/they] have any allergies I should tell the medical staff?"
- "Do you have any specific concerns or requests?"
- "Can you confirm [his/her/their] insurance information?"

**Next steps:**
"I'm going to take [student name] to [facility name] right now. [Staff name] will stay with [him/her/them] the whole time. I'll call you as soon as we know anything from the doctor. You can also call me at any time on this number. Do you have any other questions?"

**After medical evaluation:**
"Hi Mr./Ms. [Name], this is [Your Name] calling back about [Student Name]. We saw the doctor and here's what we learned: [diagnosis]. The doctor [treatment plan]. [Student name] is [current condition]. [He/She/They] [will return to activities / needs to rest / requires follow-up]. Do you have any questions?"

### Scenario 3: Code of Conduct Violation - Official Verbal Warning

**PHONE CALL SAME DAY AS INCIDENT**

**Script:**
"Hi Mr./Ms. [Name], this is [Your Name] from Summer Springboard. I'm calling about a situation that happened today with [Student Name]. Do you have a few minutes to talk?"

[Wait for confirmation]

"Today at [time], [factual description of what happened]. This is a violation of our Code of Conduct regarding [specific rule]. I've met with [Student Name] and given [him/her/them] an official verbal warning. I want to make sure you're aware and we can work together to make sure this doesn't happen again."

**Share student's perspective:**
"I spoke with [Student Name] about this, and [his/her/their] perspective was [student's explanation - factual]. I've explained to [him/her/them] that [expectations going forward]."

**Explain consequences:**
"This is [his/her/their] [first/second] official warning. Our policy is that [explain progression]. I'm confident that [Student Name] understands the expectations now, and we'll be monitoring closely."

**Partner with parent:**
"Is there anything you think would be helpful for me to know about [Student Name] or anything you'd suggest in how we work with [him/her/them]?"

**Next steps:**
"I'm going to check in with [Student Name] daily to see how things are going. If there are any other concerns, I'll call you right away. If things are going well, I'll touch base with you in a few days. Please feel free to call me any time if you have questions or concerns."

### Scenario 4: Code of Conduct Violation - Behavior Contract

**PHONE CALL SAME DAY AS INCIDENT, BEFORE STUDENT SIGNS CONTRACT**

**Script:**
"Hi Mr./Ms. [Name], this is [Your Name] from Summer Springboard. I need to talk with you about a serious situation with [Student Name]. Do you have time to talk now?"

[Wait for confirmation - if not available, schedule specific callback time within 2 hours]

"Today at [time], [factual description]. This is [his/her/their] [number] violation of our Code of Conduct. As I mentioned when we spoke about [previous violation], our next step is a written behavior contract. I'm going to go over what that means."

**Explain contract:**
"A behavior contract outlines specific behaviors that must stop and specific expectations going forward. It also states that if [Student Name] violates the contract, [he/she/they] will be dismissed from the program. I want to review the contract with you before I review it with [Student Name]."

[Read key points of contract]

**Gather parent input:**
"Before I meet with [Student Name], I want to hear your perspective. What questions or concerns do you have? Is there anything you want me to know about [Student Name] or this situation?"

**Explain next steps:**
"I'm going to meet with [Student Name] today at [time] to review and sign this contract. After we meet, I'd like [Student Name] to call you to talk about it. I'll also follow up with you [tomorrow/in 2 days] to let you know how things are going. It's important you understand that we want [Student Name] to be successful here, but [he/she/they] must follow our rules. We're going to support [him/her/them] in being successful, but this is [his/her/their] final chance."

### Scenario 5: Student Dismissal

**PHONE CALL IMMEDIATELY AFTER DECISION MADE**

**Campus Director makes call with Program Director present**

**Script:**
"Mr./Ms. [Name], this is [Your Name] from Summer Springboard. I'm calling with [Program Director Name]. We need to talk with you about a very serious situation with [Student Name]."

[Pause]

"Today at [time], [factual description of incident]. This is a violation of our Code of Conduct regarding [specific rule]. Based on the severity of this violation and our policy, we have made the decision to dismiss [Student Name] from the program effective immediately."

**Explain decision:**
"Our Code of Conduct, which you and [Student Name] both signed, states that [specific policy]. Because [Student Name] violated this policy, [he/she/they] cannot continue in the program. This decision is final."

**Logistical next steps:**
"Here's what needs to happen next:
- [Student Name] is currently [location/supervision status]
- [He/She/They] will pack belongings under staff supervision
- We need you to arrange to pick up [Student Name] or arrange [his/her/their] travel home
- We need [Student Name] picked up by [time] today/tomorrow morning
- Can you commit to that timeline?"

**Answer questions:**
- Be prepared for parent anger or pushback
- Remain calm and factual
- Restate policy and decision
- "I understand you're upset. This is a difficult situation. However, the decision is final based on our Code of Conduct."
- Redirect to logistics: "Let's focus on making sure [Student Name] gets home safely. What is your plan for pickup?"

**Document everything:**
- Take detailed notes
- Email summary to parent after call
- Complete incident report immediately
- Notify Program Director of any concerns parent raised

### Scenario 6: Positive Update

**PHONE CALL OR EMAIL**

**Script (phone):**
"Hi Mr./Ms. [Name], this is [Your Name] from Summer Springboard. I wanted to call with some good news about [Student Name]. Do you have a minute?"

"I wanted you to know that [Student Name] is doing really well. [Specific examples - instructor feedback, participation, friendships, growth, achievement]. I'm really enjoying getting to know [him/her/them] and I can tell [he/she/they] is getting a lot out of the program."

"I just wanted to share that with you. Please let me know if you ever have any questions or if there's anything I can do to make [Student Name]'s experience even better."

**Email version:**
```
Subject: Great Update on [Student Name]

Dear Mr./Ms. [Last Name],

I wanted to share some positive news about [Student Name]'s experience here at Summer Springboard.

[Specific examples of success, growth, or positive feedback]

It's been wonderful working with [him/her/them]. Please feel free to reach out any time if you have questions.

Best regards,
[Your Name]
[Title]
[Contact Information]
```

### Scenario 7: Student Requesting to Call Home (Homesick)

**If student having normal homesickness:**
- Encourage student to call parent from their own phone/device
- Give student privacy for call
- Check in with student afterward
- No need to contact parent separately unless student very upset

**If student very upset, crying, wanting to go home:**
- Sit with student and listen
- Ask if student wants to call parent now
- Offer to speak with parent briefly first
- "Mr./Ms. [Name], [Student Name] is missing home today and wanted to call you. [He/She/They] is safe and being supported. [He/She/They] is going to talk with you now, and I'm going to check in afterward."
- After student call, check in with student
- Follow up with parent later: "I wanted to check in about [Student Name]. How did you feel the conversation went? Is there anything I can do to support [him/her/them] in adjusting?"

### Scenario 8: Parent Complaint or Concern

**PHONE CALL (if parent calls or emails with concern)**

**If parent calls you directly:**
"Thank you for calling, Mr./Ms. [Name]. I want to make sure I understand your concern and address it appropriately. Can you tell me what's going on?"

**Listen actively:**
- Don't interrupt
- Take notes
- Ask clarifying questions
- Validate feelings: "I understand why you're concerned about this."
- Don't get defensive

**Gather information:**
"So if I'm understanding correctly, [summarize concern]. Is that right?"
"When did [Student Name] tell you about this?"
"What would you like to see happen?"

**Respond appropriately:**

**If you can address immediately:**
"I appreciate you bringing this to my attention. Here's what I'm going to do: [action plan]. I'll follow up with you by [time] to let you know what I've learned. Does that work for you?"

**If you need to investigate:**
"This is important and I want to make sure I address it properly. I'm going to [investigate/talk to people involved/review documentation]. I will call you back by [specific time] with an update. Is this number the best one to reach you?"

**If you need Campus Director involvement:**
"I want to make sure this is addressed properly. Our Campus Director, [Name], is the best person to help with this. I'm going to have [him/her] call you within [timeframe]. Can you confirm the best number to reach you?"

**Always:**
- Thank parent for bringing concern to attention
- Commit to specific follow-up timeframe
- Actually follow up as promised
- Document conversation
- Inform Campus Director

## Difficult Parent Conversations

### Angry or Upset Parents

**Stay calm:**
- Don't take it personally
- Keep your voice calm and even
- Take deep breaths
- Remember parent is scared/worried about their child

**Use the 3 C's:**
1. **CARE:** "I hear how concerned you are. [Student Name]'s wellbeing is my top priority."
2. **COMPASSION:** "I understand this is really difficult. If my child was there, I would feel the same way."
3. **CONTROL:** "Here's what we're doing to address this. [specific actions]"

**Validate feelings:**
- "I understand why you're upset."
- "You have every right to feel frustrated."
- "I would be concerned too if I were in your position."

**Avoid:**
- Getting defensive
- Making excuses
- Blaming student or parent
- Saying "calm down"
- Arguing

**Redirect to solutions:**
- "I hear your concern. Let's talk about how we can address this."
- "What would help you feel more comfortable with the situation?"
- "Here's what I propose we do. What do you think?"

**Know when to escalate:**
- If parent is abusive or threatening, politely end call: "I want to help resolve this, but I'm not able to continue the conversation in this tone. I'm going to have [Campus Director/Program Director] call you within the hour."
- If parent demanding things beyond your authority, bring in Campus Director or Program Director
- If legal threats or media threats, immediately involve Program Director and HQ

### Parents Who Disagree with Decisions

**Dismissal or disciplinary decisions:**

Parent: "This isn't fair. Other students were doing it too."

Response: "I understand you feel this isn't fair. I can only speak to [Student Name]'s situation. Based on [specific policy violation], this is the consequence outlined in our Code of Conduct that you and [Student Name] signed. The decision is final. Let's focus on [Student Name]'s next steps."

**Don't:**
- Discuss other students
- Change policy for one family
- Apologize for enforcing policy

**Do:**
- Restate policy
- Express empathy for difficulty
- Focus on next steps
- Remain firm

### Parents Who Want Special Treatment

Parent: "Can't you just let him leave campus with his friend's family?"

Response: "I understand you want [Student Name] to have that opportunity. Our policy is that all off-campus leave must be pre-approved with authorized adults only. This policy is for all students' safety. I'm not able to make an exception, but [Student Name] can submit a leave request for future approved outings."

**Guidelines:**
- Policies exist for all students' safety
- Exceptions create inequity and safety gaps
- Be empathetic but firm
- Offer alternatives when possible
- Loop in Campus Director if parent pushes back

## Apologies - Legal Considerations

### When to Apologize

**Protected apology (validating feelings):**
- Cannot be used in legal claim as admission of guilt
- Expresses empathy without admitting fault

**Examples:**
- "I am very sorry that there has been so much worry and upset. This has been very rough."
- "I'm sorry this experience hasn't been what you hoped for."
- "I'm sorry [Student Name] is going through this difficult time."

**Admissible apology (admitting error):**
- Can be used in legal claim
- Only use when investigation concludes we made a mistake
- Requires consultation with HQ/leadership

**Example:**
- "I am so sorry I did not get back to you immediately. The error was mine. Thankfully we are addressing the issue right now to come to a resolution as quickly as possible."

### When to Apologize for Organizational Mistake

**Only apologize when:**
- Investigation confirms we made a mistake
- HQ/leadership approves apology
- Honest disclosure of error

**Why apologize for errors:**
- Reduces bitterness and mistrust
- Apology is expected when we're clearly at fault
- Absence of apology when we're wrong can be more detrimental

**What to say:**
"After reviewing what happened, it's clear that [specific error]. I apologize for [specific impact]. Here's what we're doing to address this: [action plan]. And here's what we're doing to make sure this doesn't happen again: [prevention]."

**Requires consultation:**
- Always consult with Campus Director
- Campus Director consults with Program Director
- Program Director may consult with HQ/legal

**Never:**
- Apologize on behalf of organization without approval
- Admit fault when facts are unclear
- Promise specific remedies (refunds, etc.) without approval

## Documentation Requirements

### Document All Parent Communication

**For phone calls, document:**
- Date and time of call
- Who initiated call
- Summary of conversation
- Parent concerns or questions
- Information provided to parent
- Agreements or next steps
- Parent's response/satisfaction level

**For emails:**
- Save all email correspondence
- Forward to Campus Director
- Add to student file

**For in-person conversations at drop-off/pick-up:**
- Brief notes after conversation
- Any commitments made
- Any concerns raised

**Why:**
- Creates record if parent later claims they weren't informed
- Tracks patterns of parent concerns
- Shows duty of care was met
- Protects staff and organization

**Where to document:**
- Incident reports for significant issues
- Student notes in REACH or tracking system
- Email to Campus Director summarizing call
- Salesforce case if ongoing issue

## Special Communication Situations

### Separated or Divorced Parents

**Before program:**
- Clarify communication preferences
- Identify custodial parent if applicable
- Understand any court orders about information sharing
- Get both parents' contact information if appropriate

**During program:**
- Follow documented preferences
- Don't get caught in middle of parental conflict
- If parents disagree, defer to Campus Director
- May need to communicate with both parents separately
- Never share one parent's communication with other parent without permission

**For emergencies:**
- Contact custodial parent first OR both parents simultaneously depending on circumstances
- Document who was contacted and when
- Stay neutral in any disagreements

### Language Barriers

**If parent's first language is not English:**
- Speak slowly and clearly
- Avoid idioms and complex language
- Ask if they need interpreter (Google Translate for email, phone interpreter for calls)
- Confirm understanding: "Can you tell me what you understood so I can make sure I was clear?"
- Follow up in writing when possible
- Be patient and respectful
- Use translation services if needed

**Resources:**
- Google Translate for written communication
- Phone interpreter services (HQ can arrange)
- Student as interpreter only for non-sensitive topics

### Different Time Zones

**Be mindful:**
- Check parent's time zone before calling
- Call at reasonable hours in their time zone (9am-8pm generally)
- For emergencies, call regardless of time
- Apologize if calling at inconvenient time: "I apologize for calling at [time your time], I know it's [time their time] there, but I wanted to reach you as soon as possible about [situation]."

### Cultural Differences

**Be aware:**
- Different cultures have different expectations about authority, communication style, physical contact, etc.
- Some cultures expect formal communication, others prefer casual
- Some cultures expect extensive detail, others prefer brevity
- Never assume - ask about preferences
- Be respectful of all approaches
- When in doubt, err on side of formality

## Mass Communication to All Parents

**When to send mass communication:**
- Schedule changes affecting all students
- Weather-related changes
- Campus emergencies that are resolved
- Important reminders
- Positive updates about program

**Who sends:**
- Campus Director drafts
- Program Director approves
- Sent via email to all parents

**What NOT to send in mass communication:**
- Individual student information
- Information about specific incidents (unless campus-wide impact)
- Anything that would violate student privacy

**Template:**
```
Subject: [Topic] - Summer Springboard at [Campus]

Dear Summer Springboard Families,

[Purpose of communication]

[Details/information]

[Action needed from parents, if any]

[How to contact us with questions]

Best regards,
[Name]
Campus Director
[Contact Information]
```

## Response Time Expectations

**Emergency:**
- Answer phone immediately or return call within 15 minutes

**Urgent (non-emergency):**
- Return call within 2 hours during business hours
- Return call by next morning if after hours

**Non-urgent:**
- Return call or email within 24 hours

**Follow-up on promises:**
- Always follow up exactly when you said you would
- If you can't, call to update on delay and new timeline

## Quick Reference

### Always:
- Be factual and objective
- Document the communication
- Follow up as promised
- Loop in Campus Director on significant communications
- Return calls/emails promptly
- Validate parent feelings
- Focus on student wellbeing

### Never:
- Share information about other students
- Make promises beyond your authority
- Apologize on behalf of organization without approval
- Get defensive
- Use personal phone or email
- Discuss one parent with another parent
- Hide or minimize issues
- Make assumptions
