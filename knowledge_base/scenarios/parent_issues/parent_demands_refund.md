# Parent Demands Refund

**Tags:** refund, money back, tuition refund, chargeback, non-refundable, financial policy, parent demands refund, unhappy parent, early departure, dismissal, escalation to HQ

## Recognition Triggers
- "I want my money back"
- "This program isn't what we paid for"
- "My student is leaving early, I expect a refund"
- "You should refund us because [complaint]"
- Parent demanding partial or full tuition refund
- "I'm calling my credit card company to dispute the charge"
- Student dismissed and parent demands refund
- Student leaving voluntarily and parent expects refund

## Assessment

### IMMEDIATE ESCALATION TO PD/HQ
- **ALL refund requests escalate immediately to PD/HQ**
- Financial decisions are NOT made at campus level
- CD does NOT have authority to promise, discuss, or negotiate refunds
- Legal threats related to refunds
- Chargeback threats

### CD HANDLES INITIAL CALL, ESCALATES IMMEDIATELY
- CD listens to parent's concern and reason for refund request
- CD acknowledges concern without discussing refund
- CD escalates to PD/HQ for any financial discussion
- PD coordinates with HQ on response

### NEVER HANDLE AT MENTOR/STAFF LEVEL
- Any refund mention goes straight to CD
- Staff should not discuss refund policy except to say "CD will need to discuss that with you"

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Listen to Parent's Concern (First 3-5 minutes)
**Do NOT discuss refund yet - understand the underlying issue first**

**Listen for:**
- Why parent is requesting refund
- Is student still on program or leaving?
- What specifically didn't meet expectations?
- Is this about quality, safety, or something else?
- Is student being dismissed or leaving voluntarily?

**Script:**
"I want to make sure I understand your concern. Can you tell me what's happened that's leading you to request a refund?"

**Take detailed notes on:**
- Specific complaints about program
- Timeline of issues
- Whether parent spoke to staff before about concerns
- Student's experience vs. parent's expectation
- Any safety or quality issues raised

### Step 2: Address Underlying Concern (If Applicable)
**Before jumping to refund discussion, address the actual problem**

**If program quality issue:**
- "I hear that [student name] has been disappointed with [aspect]. Let me tell you what we can do to address that..."
- Attempt to resolve the actual issue
- May prevent refund request if problem can be fixed

**If safety issue:**
- Address safety concern immediately
- Explain actions being taken
- May retain student if safety can be ensured

**If student leaving early (non-dismissal):**
- Understand reason for departure
- See if situation can be salvaged
- If not, explain that early departure doesn't change tuition policy

**If student being dismissed:**
- Explain that dismissal policy explicitly states non-refundable tuition
- This was in parent/student contract

### Step 3: State Refund Policy Clearly
**Do NOT negotiate, promise, or make exceptions - state policy only**

**Policy Statement:**
"I understand you're requesting a refund. Our tuition policy, which was outlined in the Terms & Conditions you signed, states that program tuition and fees are non-refundable once the program has started. This policy applies in all cases, including early departure and dismissal."

**If student dismissed for COC violation:**
"As stated in our Code of Conduct and parent contract, program tuition and fees are non-refundable in cases of dismissal. [Student name] violated the Code of Conduct [specific violation], and the dismissal decision was made in accordance with our policy."

**If student leaving voluntarily:**
"I understand [student name] has decided to leave the program early. While I'm sorry it didn't work out, our tuition policy is that fees are non-refundable once the program has started, regardless of the reason for departure."

**If student hasn't started yet (rare):**
- Different policy may apply - escalate to HQ
- Do NOT make assumptions about pre-program cancellation refunds

### Step 4: Acknowledge Concern, Escalate Financial Discussion
**Do NOT argue about policy - acknowledge and escalate**

**Script:**
"I understand this is disappointing and you'd like to discuss the financial aspect. Financial decisions are made by our headquarters office, not at the campus level. I'm going to have [Program Director name] or someone from our headquarters team reach out to you within [24 hours] to discuss this further. They'll review your situation and our policy with you.

In the meantime, is there anything else I can address regarding [student name]'s experience or situation?"

**Document and escalate:**
- Email PD + HQ immediately with subject: "Refund Request - [Campus] - [Student Name]"
- Include all details: parent's reason, student's situation, timeline
- Note parent's emotional state and any threats (chargeback, legal, etc.)
- Create or update Incident Report
- PD/HQ will respond to parent directly

### Step 5: Set Expectations for Follow-Up
**Parent needs to know:**
- Who will contact them (PD or HQ)
- When they'll be contacted (within 24-48 hours)
- That campus cannot make financial decisions
- Process for any appeals or exceptions (if HQ has one)

**Script:**
"Here's what will happen next:
1. I'm going to document your request and send it to our headquarters team today
2. Someone from headquarters will contact you within [24 hours] to discuss the financial policy
3. They'll review your specific situation and explain our policy in detail
4. If you have questions about [student name]'s experience on the program, I'm here to answer those"

## Specific Refund Scenarios

### Scenario A: Student Dismissed for COC Violation
**Parent: "My student made one mistake and now you're keeping our $7,000?"**

**Response:**
"I understand this is a difficult situation. When [student name] enrolled, you signed our Terms & Conditions which clearly state that program tuition and fees are non-refundable in cases of dismissal. This policy protects the integrity of our program and ensures all students understand the seriousness of our Code of Conduct.

[Student name] was dismissed because [specific violation]. This decision was made in consultation with our Program Director and headquarters, following our established disciplinary process.

Financial matters are handled by our headquarters office. I'll have them contact you within 24 hours to discuss the policy, but I want to be clear that our standard policy is that tuition is non-refundable in dismissal cases."

**Do NOT:**
- Apologize for dismissal if it was warranted
- Suggest refund might be possible
- Blame student harshly
- Argue about fairness of keeping tuition

---

### Scenario B: Student Leaving Voluntarily (Homesick, Not a Fit, etc.)
**Parent: "My daughter is miserable and wants to come home. We've only been there 3 days - you should refund at least part of the tuition."**

**Response:**
"I'm sorry to hear [student name] is struggling. Before we discuss the financial aspect, can we talk about what's making this difficult for her? We may be able to address the issue and help her have a better experience."

[If parent insists on leaving despite intervention attempts:]

"I understand you've decided [student name] should leave the program. Our tuition policy states that fees are non-refundable once the program has started, regardless of the reason for departure. This policy was outlined in the Terms & Conditions you agreed to during enrollment.

I know this isn't the answer you were hoping for. I'm going to have our headquarters team contact you within 24 hours to discuss the policy further. In the meantime, let's coordinate logistics for [student name]'s departure."

---

### Scenario C: Program Quality Complaint
**Parent: "This program is not what was advertised. The activities are boring, my student isn't learning anything, and we want our money back."**

**Response:**
"I want to understand your concerns better. Can you tell me specifically what aspects of the program aren't meeting your expectations?"

[Listen to specifics, take notes]

"Thank you for sharing that feedback. Let me address each of those concerns:
[Address each specific complaint - explain what's actually happening, correct misunderstandings, acknowledge any valid issues and what's being done]

I hear that [student name]'s experience isn't matching what you expected. We want to make the rest of the program better for [student name]. Here's what we can do: [specific actions to improve student experience].

Regarding a refund, our tuition policy is that fees are non-refundable once the program has started. If you'd like to discuss the financial policy or file a formal complaint, I can have our headquarters team contact you. However, I'd really like to focus on improving [student name]'s experience for the remainder of the program. Are you open to that?"

---

### Scenario D: Safety Concern Leading to Departure
**Parent: "My child doesn't feel safe because of [incident]. I'm removing them and I expect a refund since you couldn't keep them safe."**

**Response:**
"I take safety concerns very seriously. Tell me exactly what happened."

[Investigate immediately - see "Parent Says Child Doesn't Feel Safe" protocol]

[If legitimate safety issue:]
"I'm very sorry this happened. Here's what we're doing to address it: [specific actions]. We want [student name] to feel safe and continue in the program if possible. Are you open to giving us the opportunity to make this right, or have you decided [student name] needs to leave?"

[If parent insists on leaving:]
"I understand your decision. I'm going to have our Program Director and headquarters team contact you within a few hours to discuss both the safety concern and the financial aspect. Given that this involves a serious concern, they'll want to review the situation directly with you."

**Do NOT:**
- Promise refund even if safety issue was real
- Admit liability ("Yes, we failed to keep your child safe")
- Discuss refund before PD/HQ reviews safety incident

**Immediate escalation to PD/HQ for:**
- Safety incident + refund request = high-risk situation
- Potential liability claim
- May require legal review

---

### Scenario E: Parent Threatening Chargeback
**Parent: "Fine, I'll just dispute the charge with my credit card company."**

**Response:**
"I understand you're frustrated. I want you to know that if you dispute the charge, it will be reviewed by our headquarters team who will provide the signed Terms & Conditions and your agreement to the non-refundable policy. We're required to report fraudulent chargebacks.

However, if you have a legitimate concern about the program or [student name]'s experience, I'd encourage you to let our headquarters team review your situation through our proper channels first. They'll contact you within 24 hours. That's a better path forward than a chargeback dispute."

**Document immediately:**
- Email PD/HQ with "URGENT - Chargeback Threat"
- Provide all documentation
- HQ may need to flag account and prepare documentation

---

### Scenario F: Student Hasn't Started Yet (Pre-Program Cancellation)
**Parent: "We need to cancel before the program starts. What's the refund policy?"**

**Response:**
"I'll need to connect you with our admissions or headquarters team to discuss pre-program cancellation policy, as that's different from the policy once the program has started. Let me get you their contact information."

**Do NOT:**
- State what the pre-program cancellation policy is (may vary, may have changed)
- Promise any refund
- Handle pre-program cancellations at campus level

**Escalate to:**
- Admissions team or HQ
- Check current cancellation policy documentation

## What NOT to Say/Promise

**NEVER Promise or Discuss Refunds:**
- ❌ "I'll see what we can do about a partial refund"
- ❌ "Maybe we can refund part of it"
- ❌ "Let me talk to my supervisor about getting you some money back"
- ❌ "I think you might be eligible for a refund"
- ❌ "We've given refunds in situations like this before"

**NEVER Agree Refund is Deserved:**
- ❌ "You're right, you should get your money back"
- ❌ "I'd want a refund too if I were you"
- ❌ "It's not fair that we keep your tuition"

**NEVER Admit Liability:**
- ❌ "Yes, we failed to provide what we promised"
- ❌ "You're right, this program isn't worth what you paid"
- ❌ "We made mistakes that justify a refund"

**NEVER Undermine Policy:**
- ❌ "I know it's a strict policy, but between you and me..."
- ❌ "I disagree with the no-refund policy too"
- ❌ "I wish I could give you your money back"

**NEVER Make Exceptions:**
- ❌ "I'll make an exception this time"
- ❌ "Don't worry, I'll get you a refund" (you can't)

## When to Involve CD/PD/HQ

### CD Involvement (ALL refund requests):
- CD takes initial call
- CD listens to concern and states policy
- CD escalates to PD/HQ immediately
- CD does NOT negotiate or discuss refund terms

### PD Involvement (ALL refund requests):
- PD reviews situation
- PD coordinates with HQ on financial response
- PD may handle parent callback or HQ may handle directly
- PD has NO authority to grant refunds without HQ approval

### HQ Involvement (ALL refund requests):
- HQ makes ALL financial decisions
- HQ reviews contract and Terms & Conditions
- HQ determines if any exception warranted (extremely rare)
- HQ handles any chargeback disputes
- HQ legal team involved if liability questions

**Process:**
1. CD takes call → escalates to PD/HQ same day
2. PD/HQ reviews within 24 hours
3. PD or HQ contacts parent directly with financial response
4. CD stays informed but does NOT lead financial discussion

## Follow-Up Procedures

### After Escalating Refund Request:
**CD's ongoing role:**
- Document refund request in Incident Report
- Provide all context to PD/HQ
- Continue addressing student's program experience if student staying
- Coordinate departure logistics if student leaving
- Do NOT follow up on financial discussion unless PD asks

**PD/HQ role:**
- Review contract and Terms & Conditions
- Assess any exceptions or special circumstances
- Prepare response to parent (almost always: no refund per policy)
- Document decision and rationale
- Handle any escalation (legal threats, chargebacks, etc.)

### If Student Staying Despite Refund Request:
- Address underlying program issues
- Improve student experience
- Document improvements in IR
- May help avoid further escalation

### If Student Leaving:
- Complete departure procedures
- Final parent communication from PD/HQ
- Document departure as voluntary or dismissal
- Close case with financial decision documented

### If Chargeback Occurs:
- HQ handles entirely
- Provides documentation to credit card company
- Signed Terms & Conditions
- Student/Parent Contract
- Evidence of services provided
- Usually resolved in favor of organization due to clear contract

## Common Mistakes

1. **Discussing refund at campus level**: "Maybe we can work something out" - NO, escalate to HQ
2. **Promising to "try"**: "I'll see what I can do" raises false hope
3. **Admitting program failures**: "You're right, we didn't deliver" creates liability
4. **Not documenting**: Failing to record refund request in IR
5. **Apologizing inappropriately**: "I'm sorry we can't refund you" sounds like you want to but can't
6. **Arguing about fairness**: Getting into debate about whether policy is fair
7. **Comparing to other situations**: "We gave someone a refund once" (probably not true, creates expectation)
8. **Not escalating quickly**: Waiting days to involve PD/HQ
9. **Making exceptions**: No one at campus level can authorize refund
10. **Poor handoff**: Not clearly telling parent that HQ will contact them

## Key Principles from Training

**Refund Policy:**
- "Program tuition and fees are non-refundable in cases of dismissal"
- Non-refundable once program has started (standard policy)
- Stated in Terms & Conditions signed by student and parent
- Parent & Student Contract and Waiver includes this language

**Financial Authority:**
- Campus Director has NO authority to grant refunds
- Program Director has NO authority to grant refunds without HQ
- ALL financial decisions made by HQ
- Policy exists to ensure consistency and protect organization

**Communication Approach:**
- State policy clearly and factually
- Don't apologize for policy (it's not an error)
- Acknowledge parent's frustration without agreeing refund is warranted
- Escalate quickly and clearly
- Focus on addressing program issues separately from financial discussion

**Documentation:**
- Every refund request documented in Incident Report
- Include: parent's reason, student's situation, policy stated, escalation to HQ
- Protect organization by having clear record
- HQ may need documentation for chargeback defense

**Parent Partnership:**
- Separate program experience issues from financial policy
- Try to resolve program issues even if refund not possible
- "I can't change our refund policy, but I can make the rest of the program better for your student"
- Some parents will be satisfied with improved experience even without refund

**When Rare Exceptions Might Occur:**
- Medical emergency requiring student to leave (family situation)
- Natural disaster or program cancellation (not student choice)
- Fraud or misrepresentation by organization (extremely rare)
- **Even these require HQ review and approval**
- CD/PD never assumes exception will be made
