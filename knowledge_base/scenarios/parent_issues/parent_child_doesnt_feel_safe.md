# Parent Says Child "Doesn't Feel Safe"

**Tags:** doesnt feel safe, feels unsafe, uncomfortable, threatened, safety concern, bullying, harassment, identity-based concern, diversity, parent concern, investigation, escalation

## Recognition Triggers
- Parent calls saying student "doesn't feel safe"
- Student told parent they feel unsafe due to another student
- Parent reports student feeling unsafe because of another student's identity (religion, race, sexuality, gender, politics, etc.)
- Parent concerned about safety after hearing about incident involving other students
- "My child doesn't feel comfortable" or "My child feels threatened"
- Parent wants student moved away from specific student(s)
- Parent demands action against another student

## Assessment

### IMMEDIATE ESCALATION TO CD/PD
- Student reporting threats of violence
- Allegations of assault, harassment, or bullying
- Safety concern based on actual COC violations
- Parent saying student's identity is being targeted
- Multiple students reporting same safety concerns
- Parent threatening to remove student due to safety fears

### HANDLE WITH CD OVERSIGHT
- **All "doesn't feel safe" calls require CD involvement**
- Safety concerns based on discomfort vs. actual threat
- Parent uncomfortable with diversity/inclusion of program
- Identity-based discomfort (parent uncomfortable with other student's identity)
- Roommate conflicts creating discomfort
- CD leads assessment with PD consultation

### STAFF CAN HANDLE (INITIALLY)
- Student sharing general discomfort with mentor
- Roommate personality conflicts (not safety-based)
- Must escalate to CD immediately if "safety" language used

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Gather Specific Information (First 5 minutes)
**Critical to determine: ACTUAL THREAT vs. DISCOMFORT**

**Ask parent:**
- "Can you tell me specifically what [student name] told you?"
- "What did [student name] say happened?"
- "Who is involved in this situation?"
- "When did this occur?"
- "Has [student name] reported this to any staff members?"
- "What specifically makes [student name] feel unsafe?"

**Listening for:**
- **Actual safety concern**: Threats, harassment, assault, bullying, COC violations
- **Identity-based concern**: Student uncomfortable because another student is LGBTQ+, different religion, different race, etc.
- **Conflict-based concern**: Disagreement or personality clash escalated to "feeling unsafe"
- **Environmental concern**: General anxiety or homesickness framed as safety

### Step 2: Immediate Assessment - Safety or Discomfort?

**ACTUAL SAFETY THREAT - Immediate Action Required:**

**Examples:**
- "Another student threatened to hurt my child"
- "My child is being bullied by roommate"
- "Someone touched my child inappropriately"
- "A student said they're going to spread rumors/hurt my child's reputation"
- "My child is being harassed because of their [identity]"

**Response:**
"Thank you for telling me this immediately. This is very serious and I'm going to investigate right away. I'm going to speak with [student name] and the other students involved within the next hour and call you back by [specific time] to update you on what I've learned and what action we're taking."

**Actions:**
1. Notify CD/PD immediately
2. Interview student immediately in private
3. Interview other students involved separately
4. Assess credibility and severity
5. Take immediate protective action if needed (separate students, remove aggressor from activities)
6. Follow COC violation and discipline protocols
7. Call parent back within 2-4 hours with findings and action plan

---

**IDENTITY-BASED DISCOMFORT - Different Approach:**

**Examples:**
- "My child doesn't feel safe because another student is gay/trans/Muslim/etc."
- "I don't want my child around students who believe in [different political/religious views]"
- "This program is exposing my child to things we don't agree with"
- "My child feels uncomfortable because students are discussing [race/gender/politics]"

**Response - Use "Conversations with Parents" Framework:**
- **Professional Neutrality**: Keep your voice calm and steady to de-escalate high emotions
- **Shared Interest**: "We both want your student to have a successful, safe summer"
- **Policy as Shield**: Lean on pre-established program guidelines to maintain boundaries

**Script:**
"I want to make sure I understand. Is [student name] feeling unsafe because another student has threatened them or harassed them, or is this about [student name] feeling uncomfortable with another student's identity or background?"

**If parent confirms it's about identity/diversity:**
"I understand that [student name] may feel uncomfortable in new situations or around people with different backgrounds. That's actually a really common feeling when students are exposed to diverse perspectives for the first time.

Our program brings together students from many different backgrounds, identities, religions, and viewpoints. This diversity is intentional - we're preparing students for college and careers where they'll work with people from all walks of life.

What I want to assure you is:
1. All students must treat each other with respect - that's our Code of Conduct
2. No student should be harassed or targeted because of their identity
3. Students are not required to agree with each other, but they are required to be respectful

Has another student been disrespectful to [student name]? If so, I'll address that immediately. But if this is about [student name] simply being around students who are different from them, that's part of our program's learning experience."

### Step 3: Investigation (If Actual Threat)

**Interview student privately:**
- "Your parent called and said you don't feel safe. Tell me what's going on."
- "What happened? When did it happen?"
- "Who was involved?"
- "Have you told any staff members about this?"
- "What would make you feel safe?"

**Interview other students separately:**
- Get all sides of story
- Separate facts from perceptions
- Identify any COC violations
- Determine if pattern of behavior

**Review evidence:**
- Reach messages if relevant
- Other student reports
- Staff observations
- Prior incidents involving same students

**Determine:**
- Did actual threat or harassment occur?
- Is this conflict/disagreement vs. safety threat?
- What action is warranted based on COC?

### Step 4: Response Strategy Based on Findings

**SCENARIO A: Actual Threat Confirmed (COC Violation)**

**Actions:**
- Address COC violation immediately (warning, behavior contract, or dismissal depending on severity)
- Separate students if needed
- Increased supervision and monitoring
- Support victim student
- Call parent back with specific actions taken

**Parent callback script:**
"I've investigated what happened. Here's what I found: [factual summary]. This violated our Code of Conduct. Here's the action we've taken: [consequence for offending student]. To ensure [your student]'s safety going forward: [protective measures - separate rooms, no contact, staff monitoring, etc.]. I'll be checking in with [your student] daily to make sure they continue to feel safe. Please have [your student] come to me immediately if anything else happens."

---

**SCENARIO B: Conflict/Disagreement, No Actual Threat**

**Actions:**
- Mediate conflict if appropriate
- Clarify expectations for respectful behavior
- Coach both students on communication
- Monitor situation
- May separate if tension is high, but not framing as "safety threat"

**Parent callback script:**
"I've spoken with [your student] and the other students involved. Here's what I learned: [factual summary]. While I understand [your student] is uncomfortable, there wasn't a threat or harassment - this appears to be [personality conflict / disagreement / misunderstanding]. Here's what we're doing: [mediation / separation / coaching]. I'll continue monitoring and checking in with [your student] to ensure the situation improves."

---

**SCENARIO C: Identity-Based Discomfort, No Violation**

**Actions:**
- Validate student's feelings of discomfort
- Explain program's diversity and inclusion mission
- Support student in navigating diverse environment
- Stand firm on program values
- Offer student tools for engaging with differences

**Parent callback script:**
"I've spoken with [your student] and here's what I've learned: [your student] is feeling uncomfortable because [situation]. I want to assure you that:

1. No student has violated our Code of Conduct or been disrespectful to [your student]
2. Our program intentionally brings together students from diverse backgrounds - this prepares them for college and career success
3. [Your student] is not required to agree with other students' beliefs or identities, but we do expect all students to treat each other with respect

What I'm hearing is that [your student] is experiencing the normal discomfort that comes with encountering new perspectives. This is actually part of the growth experience we provide. We'll support [your student] in navigating this, and I'll check in with them regularly. However, we won't be separating students or taking action against another student based on their identity alone.

How can we partner to help [your student] develop skills for engaging respectfully with people who are different from them?"

### Step 5: Pivot to Shared Values (Engagement Strategy)

**From training - Four-Phase Engagement:**

1. **Listen**: Understand the root emotion before reacting
   - Let parent fully express concern
   - Don't interrupt or defend

2. **Bridge**: Acknowledge how parent feels without conceding policy
   - "I understand this is concerning for you"
   - "I hear that [student name] is uncomfortable"
   - "We both want [student name] to have a successful summer"

3. **Pivot**: Redirect to values and program mission
   - "Our program prepares students for diverse college environments"
   - "Part of our mission is helping students engage with multiple perspectives"
   - "We're building skills students will need in global workforce"

4. **Repair**: Define exactly what happens next to restore safety
   - "Here's what we're going to do to support [student name]..."
   - "I'll check in with [student name] daily"
   - "If there's any actual harassment or disrespect, we'll address it immediately"

## Conversation Scripts

### When Parent Says Child Doesn't Feel Safe (Opening):
"I take this very seriously. Can you tell me specifically what happened that makes [student name] feel unsafe? Has someone threatened them or harassed them?"

### Distinguishing Threat from Discomfort:
"I want to make sure I address this appropriately. Is [student name] feeling unsafe because someone has done something to them - like threatened them, harassed them, or been disrespectful? Or is [student name] uncomfortable being around someone who is different from them in terms of background, identity, or beliefs?"

### If Identity-Based (Training Scenario: "Parent says child doesn't feel safe because of another student's identity"):
**Strategy: Use the Pivot to Shared Values technique**

"I understand [student name] is uncomfortable. Let me ask: has another student been disrespectful or inappropriate toward [student name]? If so, I'll address that immediately - we have zero tolerance for harassment.

[If no specific incident:]
Our program brings together students from many backgrounds - different religions, races, gender identities, political views, and life experiences. This diversity is intentional. We're preparing students for college and professional environments where they'll work with people very different from themselves.

The goal isn't for everyone to agree - it's for students to learn to engage respectfully with people who have different perspectives. That's a critical skill for the diverse global workforce they'll enter.

What we require is that all students treat each other with respect, regardless of their differences. We don't require agreement, but we do require respect.

How can we support [student name] in learning to navigate diverse environments while maintaining their own values and beliefs?"

### If Parent Insists on Student Being Moved/Removed:
"I understand you want [student name] to feel comfortable. If another student has violated our Code of Conduct by harassing or threatening [student name], we'll absolutely address that with consequences up to dismissal.

However, if [student name] is uncomfortable simply because another student exists and has a different [identity/belief/background], we can't and won't remove a student for that reason. That would contradict our program's educational mission and wouldn't be fair to the other student.

What we can do is:
- Support [student name] in developing skills to engage respectfully with people who are different
- Ensure [student name] is never harassed or disrespected
- Check in with [student name] regularly
- Address any actual Code of Conduct violations immediately

Would you like to give [student name] some time to adjust with this support, or would you prefer to pick [student name] up?"

### If Parent Threatens to Remove Student:
"I understand this is a difficult decision. Our program's diversity is core to who we are and the education we provide. If that doesn't align with what you want for [student name], I respect your decision to remove them from the program. Please know that our tuition policy is that fees are non-refundable once the program has started. Would you like some time to think about this, or shall we discuss logistics for pickup?"

## What NOT to Say/Promise

**DON'T Agree to Remove Other Student Based on Identity:**
- ❌ "We'll move that student to a different mentor group"
- ❌ "We'll make sure your student doesn't have to interact with them"
- ❌ "I'll talk to that student about keeping their identity/beliefs to themselves"

**DON'T Dismiss Actual Safety Concerns:**
- ❌ "Your student is overreacting"
- ❌ "Kids will be kids"
- ❌ "This is just typical teenage drama"

**DON'T Compromise Program Values:**
- ❌ "I agree, we shouldn't expose students to [diverse perspectives]"
- ❌ "You're right, that's inappropriate for this age"
- ❌ "I'll make sure staff don't discuss [diversity topics]"

**DON'T Make It Personal:**
- ❌ "You're being intolerant"
- ❌ "This is 2026, you need to accept diversity"
- ❌ "Your values are outdated"

**DON'T Violate Other Student's Privacy:**
- ❌ "That student is [identity details]"
- ❌ "We've had problems with that student before"

## When to Involve CD/PD

### CD Involvement (ALL "doesn't feel safe" calls):
- Any parent reporting safety concerns = CD
- CD assesses, investigates, determines response
- CD leads parent communication

### PD Involvement:
- Parent threatening to remove student
- Parent threatening legal action
- Potential COC violation requiring dismissal
- Parent demanding action that violates program values
- CD needs support in navigating identity-based concerns
- Parent escalating to headquarters

### HQ Involvement:
- Legal threats
- Discrimination claims
- Parent demanding refund
- Media involvement
- Multiple families raising similar concerns

## Follow-Up Procedures

### After Safety Concern Resolved:
**If actual threat:**
- Daily check-ins with victim student for 3-5 days
- Monitor for retaliation or ongoing issues
- Parent update after 24-48 hours
- Document resolution in IR
- Consequences enforced for offending student

**If identity-based discomfort:**
- Check in with student to support adjustment
- Provide tools for navigating differences
- Monitor for any actual harassment
- Parent update if parent was cooperative
- Document conversation and outcome

**If parent removed student:**
- Complete departure procedures
- Document as voluntary departure
- No refund per policy
- Debrief with staff if needed
- Close IR with outcome

### Documentation:
- Incident Report for all "doesn't feel safe" calls
- Parent conversation summary
- Investigation findings
- Actions taken
- Student response to interventions
- Case closure when resolved

## Common Mistakes

1. **Not distinguishing threat from discomfort**: Treating all "doesn't feel safe" the same
2. **Compromising program values**: Agreeing to remove students based on identity to appease parent
3. **Dismissing real threats**: Assuming it's just discomfort when actual harassment occurred
4. **Getting into debates**: Arguing with parent about diversity/inclusion values
5. **Not involving CD**: Trying to handle "safety" concern at mentor level
6. **Violating student privacy**: Sharing details about other students
7. **Overpromising**: "We'll make sure they never interact"
8. **Poor documentation**: Not recording what parent said vs. what investigation found
9. **Not supporting student**: Taking action without helping student develop skills
10. **Delayed investigation**: Waiting to investigate when actual threat may exist

## Key Principles from Training

**From Scenario 3: "Parent says their child 'doesn't feel safe' because of another student's identity"**

**Conversations with Parents Framework:**
- **Professional Neutrality**: Keep your voice calm and steady to de-escalate high emotions
- **Shared Interest**: "We both want your student to have a successful, safe summer"
- **Policy as Shield**: Lean on pre-established program guidelines to maintain boundaries

**The Strategy:**
- **Use the Pivot to Shared Values technique**
- Focus on goal of preparing students for diverse global workforce
- Requires exposure to multiple perspectives
- Education over exclusion

**Safety Philosophy:**
- "True safety isn't the absence of conflict, but the presence of skills to navigate it"
- "High 'risk appetite' means we lean into tough topics to prevent them from becoming toxic undercurrents"
- "Risk tolerance is low for exclusion or harassment, but high for learning moments"

**Handling Difficult Conversations:**
- Listen → Bridge → Pivot → Repair
- Acknowledge impact without conceding policy
- Redirect to program values
- Define specific next steps

**When Students Bring Up Divisive Topics:**
- Encourage curiosity but avoid making others feel "othered"
- "That comment isn't appropriate here" (if truly inappropriate)
- "Let's create space where everyone feels safe" (from actual harassment)
- "That's not something we joke about" (if targeting identity)

**Key Principle:**
- "Impact vs. Intent: Impact trumps intent. A student might not intend to be exclusionary, but the impact on the peer group creates an unsafe environment."
- BUT: Existing with different identity ≠ creating unsafe environment
- Harassment/targeting based on identity = unsafe environment that must be addressed
