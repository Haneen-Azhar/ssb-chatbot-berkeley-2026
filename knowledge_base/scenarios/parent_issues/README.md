## Extracted Scenarios

### 1. Angry Parent Phone Call - Demanding Immediate Answers
**File**: `angry_parent_demanding_answers.md`

**Situation**: Parent is yelling, demanding immediate answers after hearing student is upset

**Key Protocols**:
- Professional neutrality and de-escalation
- Never speculate or overpromise
- Emotional parents need calm leadership
- Active listening → Validation → Investigation → Follow-up
- Listen-Bridge-Pivot-Repair engagement strategy

**Key Scripts**:
- "I understand you're concerned about [student name]. Student safety is our top priority."
- Protected apology: "I am very sorry that there has been so much worry and upset."
- Setting expectations: "I need to gather all the facts before I can give you complete information. I will call you back by [specific time]."

### 2. Parent Threatens Legal Action
**File**: `parent_threatens_legal_action.md`

**Situation**: Parent says "I'm going to sue" or "You'll be hearing from my lawyer," has screenshot evidence, threatens legal action

**Key Protocols**:
- **IMMEDIATE escalation to PD/HQ** - ALL legal threats
- Do NOT engage with legal claims
- Do NOT admit fault or discuss legal merits
- Do NOT make promises about outcomes
- Document everything verbatim
- Transfer to PD within 1-2 hours

**Key Scripts**:
- "Given that you've mentioned legal action, I need to involve our Program Director immediately to ensure this is handled appropriately."
- "I want to make sure you get the proper response from the people authorized to address legal matters."

---

### 3. Student Wants to Leave / Go Home - Homesickness
**File**: `student_wants_to_leave_homesick.md`

**Situation**: Student says "I want to go home," parent calls saying student is bored and might leave early, homesickness, adjustment struggles

**Key Protocols**:
- Assess: Safety concern vs. normal homesickness vs. specific problem
- Private conversation with student to understand root cause
- Validation and support strategies
- Parent partnership approach
- Retention vs. Safety vs. Voluntary Departure decision framework
- **Tuition is non-refundable** - must communicate clearly

**Key Scripts**:
- To student: "It's really normal to feel homesick. A lot of students feel this way in the first few days."
- To parent: "Let's work together on this. What if we give it until [specific time] to see if [student name] adjusts? I'll check in with them and call you tomorrow at [time] to update you."
- Setting boundaries: "If [student name] leaves voluntarily, please understand that our tuition policy is that fees are non-refundable once the program has started."

### 4. Parent Reports Staff Misconduct
**File**: `parent_reports_staff_misconduct.md`

**Situation**: Parent reports mentor "behaving inappropriately," discussing politics/divisive topics, "radicalizing" students, boundary violations, professionalism concerns

**Key Protocols**:
- Listen and document without defending staff
- Validate concern without admitting fault
- Immediate investigation protocol
- Interview students and staff separately
- Determine: Green (coaching) → Yellow (warning) → Orange (serious) → Red (termination)
- Appropriate vs. inappropriate staff discussions framework
- Terra's Diversity and Inclusivity Ideology

**Key Scripts**:
- "Thank you for bringing this to my attention. We take all concerns about staff conduct seriously."
- Investigation process: "Here's what's going to happen next: [clear steps]. I will get back to you by [specific timeframe] to update you on our findings."
- If substantiated: "We've completed our investigation and confirmed that [general description]. This is not acceptable, and we have taken the following action..."

**Training Example**: Parent upset about mentor discussing Trump's trial and "radicalizing students to Pro-Palestine" - has screenshots, threatening to sue, en route to pick up student.

### 5. Parent Says Child "Doesn't Feel Safe"
**File**: `parent_child_doesnt_feel_safe.md`

**Situation**: Parent calls saying student "doesn't feel safe" due to another student, identity-based discomfort, conflict escalated to safety concern

**Key Protocols**:
- **Critical distinction**: Actual threat vs. discomfort vs. identity-based concern
- Immediate investigation if actual threat
- Different response for identity-based discomfort
- Conversations with Parents framework: Professional Neutrality, Shared Interest, Policy as Shield
- Pivot to Shared Values technique
- Stand firm on program diversity and inclusion mission

**Key Scripts**:
- Assessing: "Is [student name] feeling unsafe because someone has threatened them or harassed them, or is [student name] uncomfortable being around someone who is different from them?"
- Identity-based response: "Our program brings together students from many backgrounds. This diversity is intentional - we're preparing students for college and professional environments."
- Setting boundaries: "If another student has harassed [student name], we'll address that immediately. However, if [student name] is uncomfortable simply because another student exists and has a different [identity], we can't remove a student for that reason."

**Training Scenario**: "A parent says their child 'doesn't feel safe' because of another student's identity."

---

### 6. Parent Questions Discipline Decision
**File**: `parent_questions_discipline.md`

**Situation**: Parent disputes warning or behavior contract, argues consequence is too harsh, claims unfairness, believes student being targeted

**Key Protocols**:
- CD handles all discipline parent communications
- Present facts clearly and objectively
- Explain three-step discipline process (Warning → Behavior Contract → Dismissal)
- Acknowledge parent perspective without conceding policy
- Policy as shield - lean on Code of Conduct
- Stand firm on decision unless PD approves change
- Partner with parent to support student moving forward

**Key Scripts**:
- "Here's what happened: [specific behavior, date, time, location]. This violated our Code of Conduct section on [policy]."
- "I understand this feels [harsh/unfair]. The reason we take [behavior] seriously is [safety/respect/community impact]."
- Standing firm: "The decision has been made based on our policy and what occurred. What we can do is support [student name] in moving forward successfully."

**Discipline Steps Explained**:
- Step 1: Official Warning (verbal or written, documented)
- Step 2: Behavior Contract (written agreement, parent notified, final warning)
- Step 3: Dismissal (parent pays for transportation home, tuition non-refundable)

### 7. Parent Demands Refund
**File**: `parent_demands_refund.md`

**Situation**: Parent wants money back due to early departure, dismissal, quality complaints, safety concerns, or dissatisfaction

**Key Protocols**:
- **ALL refund requests escalate immediately to PD/HQ**
- CD has NO authority to promise, discuss, or negotiate refunds
- State policy clearly: "Non-refundable once program has started"
- Address underlying concern separately from financial discussion
- Distinguish: Dismissal, Voluntary Departure, Quality Complaint, Safety Concern, Pre-Program Cancellation
- Chargeback threats escalate immediately

**Key Scripts**:
- Policy statement: "Our tuition policy, which was outlined in the Terms & Conditions you signed, states that program tuition and fees are non-refundable once the program has started."
- Escalation: "Financial decisions are made by our headquarters office, not at the campus level. I'm going to have someone from headquarters contact you within 24 hours to discuss this further."
- Dismissal case: "When [student name] enrolled, you signed our Terms & Conditions which clearly state that tuition is non-refundable in cases of dismissal."

## Core Communication Frameworks

### Engagement Strategy (Four-Phase)
From training - applies to all difficult parent conversations:

1. **Listen**: Understand the root emotion before reacting (Active Silence)
2. **Bridge**: Acknowledge how student/parent feels without conceding policy (Validate Impact)
3. **Pivot**: Redirect to values and program mission (Redirect to Values)
4. **Repair**: Define exactly what happens next to restore safety (Actionable Steps)

### Conversations with Parents Framework
**Professional Neutrality**: Keep your voice calm and steady to de-escalate high emotions

**Shared Interest**: "We both want your student to have a successful, safe summer"

**Policy as Shield**: Lean on pre-established program guidelines to maintain boundaries

### Protected vs. Admissible Apologies
**Protected (empathy, no admission of guilt)**:
- ✅ "I am very sorry that there has been so much worry and upset. This has been very rough."
- Cannot be used in legal claim

**Admissible (admits fault)**:
- ❌ "I am so sorry I did not get back to you immediately. The error was mine."
- Can be used against organization

**When in doubt**: Use protected apologies only, or get PD/HQ approval

## Escalation Decision Framework

### STAFF CAN HANDLE:
- Routine parent questions
- Positive updates
- Minor concerns with clear answers
- Daily communication about student experience
- **Must escalate immediately if**: Safety concern, complaint, legal language, or demanding tone

### HANDLE WITH CD OVERSIGHT:
- Student behavioral issues
- Homesickness or adjustment problems
- Roommate conflicts
- Parent questions about program
- Discipline at warning level
- General complaints
- **CD coordinates with PD if**: Escalation likely, serious concern, pattern issue

### IMMEDIATE ESCALATION TO PD/HQ:
- Legal threats (sue, lawyer, legal action)
- Refund demands (ANY financial discussion)
- Dismissal situations
- Serious safety allegations
- Staff misconduct allegations (serious)
- Parent threatening to come remove student immediately
- Media involvement
- Discrimination or civil rights claims
- Chargeback threats
- Parent bypassing CD to call headquarters directly

## Documentation Requirements

### Every Parent Issue Requires:
- Incident Report created within 24 hours
- Verbatim quotes when relevant (especially legal threats)
- Timeline of events
- Parent's specific complaint or concern
- Investigation findings (if applicable)
- Actions taken
- Follow-up plan
- Case status (open/closed)

### Incident Report Triggers:
- Any programmatic challenge
- Expressed dissatisfaction from student, parent, or other
- Early departure (any reason)
- COC violation
- Medical clinic visits
- Police reports
- Staff policy violations
- Safety concerns
- **Parental concerns** requiring formal follow-up

## Common Themes Across All Scenarios

### Do's:
✅ Listen actively before responding
✅ Validate feelings without conceding policy
✅ State facts clearly and objectively
✅ Set specific expectations and timelines
✅ Document everything immediately
✅ Escalate when appropriate
✅ Partner with parent when possible
✅ Follow up as promised
✅ Use protected apologies only (unless investigation confirms fault + HQ approval)
✅ Keep voice calm and professional

### Don'ts:
❌ Speculate or make assumptions
❌ Promise outcomes before investigation
❌ Admit fault without investigation
❌ Make financial promises
❌ Engage with legal claims
❌ Dismiss parent concerns
❌ Violate student/staff privacy
❌ Undermine policies
❌ Get defensive or argue
❌ Miss promised callback time
❌ Forget to document

## Key Principles from Training

**"Emotional parents need calm leadership"**
- Professional neutrality de-escalates
- Your emotional regulation models appropriate behavior
- Stay calm even when parent is not

**"Never speculate or overpromise"**
- Stick to facts you know
- "I will investigate and call you back by [time]"
- Don't promise outcomes before you know them

**"Staff professionalism helps de-escalate situations"**
- How you handle call matters as much as what you say
- Calm, clear, organized response builds trust
- Document everything for accountability

**"Documentation matters!"**
- Protects student, staff, and organization
- Ensures consistency and follow-through
- Required for legal/liability protection
- Helps identify patterns

**"If in doubt, escalate it! We can always de-escalate!"**
- Better to involve PD and not need them than opposite
- Escalation is not weakness - it's appropriate protocol
- Some situations require higher authority

**Impact vs. Intent**
- "Impact trumps intent"
- Staff/student might not intend harm, but impact on others matters
- Address impact while considering intent

**Policy as Shield**
- Use policies to maintain boundaries
- "Our Code of Conduct states..."
- "Our Terms & Conditions are clear that..."
- Not personal decision - organizational standard

## How to Use These Scenarios

### For Training:
- Role-play scenarios with staff before program starts
- Practice de-escalation scripts
- Discuss decision points (when to escalate, how to respond)
- Review common mistakes

### During Program:
- Quick reference when parent call comes in
- Script templates for difficult conversations
- Escalation decision trees
- Documentation requirements

### After Incidents:
- Debrief using scenario protocols
- Identify what went well and what could improve
- Update procedures based on real experiences
- Share learnings across campus teams
