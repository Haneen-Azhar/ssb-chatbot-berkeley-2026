# Parent Threatens Legal Action

**Tags:** lawsuit, lawyer, attorney, legal action, legal threat, sue, litigation, parent threatening, angry parent, escalation, legal counsel, immediate escalation

## Recognition Triggers
- Parent explicitly says "I'm going to sue" or "You'll be hearing from my lawyer"
- Mentions having "screenshot evidence" or documentation
- Threatens to "take this to court" or "file a complaint"
- Says "this is a legal matter now"
- Parent mentions consulting attorney
- Threats related to staff misconduct, safety violations, or discrimination

## Assessment

### IMMEDIATE ESCALATION TO PD/HQ
- **ALL legal threats escalate immediately**
- Do not attempt to resolve at campus level
- Do not engage with legal claims
- Parent threatening lawsuit = immediate PD + HQ notification
- Screenshot evidence of staff misconduct = escalate to PD/HQ
- Any discrimination or civil rights claims = escalate immediately

### NEVER HANDLE AT STAFF LEVEL
- Legal threats bypass normal escalation
- Campus Director does NOT resolve alone
- Must involve Program Director and HQ

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Stay Calm, Do Not Engage Legal Claims (First 30 seconds)
- Remain professionally neutral
- DO NOT defend against legal claims
- DO NOT admit fault or wrongdoing
- DO NOT argue about legal merits
- Keep voice calm and steady

**Script**: "I understand this is a serious concern. I need to involve our Program Director and headquarters team immediately to address this properly."

### Step 2: Document Everything Parent Says
- Exact words used in threat
- What specific incident prompted threat
- What evidence parent claims to have
- Whether parent has already contacted attorney
- Whether parent is removing student from program
- Timeline parent mentions

### Step 3: Immediate Escalation
**Do NOT say**: "Let me investigate and get back to you"
**DO say**: "Given the nature of this concern, I need to connect you with our Program Director immediately. Can you hold while I get them on the line, or would you prefer they call you back within the hour?"

**Immediate notifications**:
- Text/call Program Director immediately
- Email PD + HQ with "URGENT - LEGAL THREAT" in subject
- Do not delay escalation

### Step 4: Transfer or Coordinate Response
**If PD available**: Three-way call or transfer
**If PD unavailable**:
- Get parent's preferred callback number
- Confirm parent email
- Get best time for PD to call within next 2 hours
- "Our Program Director will call you back within [1-2 hours]. Is [phone number] the best number to reach you?"

### Step 5: Document Immediately
- Create Incident Report within 30 minutes
- Include verbatim quotes of legal threats
- Document parent's specific complaints
- Note if student is being removed from program
- Copy all communications to PD/HQ

## Conversation Scripts

### Initial Response to Legal Threat
"I understand you have serious concerns. Given that you've mentioned legal action, I need to involve our Program Director and headquarters team to ensure this is handled appropriately. Let me get them on the line for you."

### If Parent Demands Immediate Answer
"I want to make sure you get the proper response from the people authorized to address legal matters. Our Program Director [Name] will call you within the next hour to discuss this."

### If Parent Is Coming to Remove Student
"I understand you're coming to pick up [student name]. For everyone's safety and to ensure proper procedures, let me notify our Program Director. When do you expect to arrive?"

### Validating Without Engaging Legally
"I understand this is very serious and concerning. Our organization takes these matters seriously, which is why I need to immediately involve our leadership team who handles these situations."

## What NOT to Say/Promise

### NEVER Engage Legal Claims:
- ❌ "You don't have grounds to sue"
- ❌ "That's not a legal issue"
- ❌ "We haven't done anything wrong legally"
- ❌ "Our lawyers will handle this"
- ❌ "You're overreacting"

### NEVER Admit Liability:
- ❌ "You're right, we made a mistake"
- ❌ "I apologize for what happened" (admissible apology)
- ❌ "Yes, that staff member shouldn't have done that"
- ❌ "We'll fix this immediately"

### NEVER Make Promises:
- ❌ "We'll give you a refund"
- ❌ "That staff member will be fired"
- ❌ "This won't go any further"
- ❌ "We'll make this right"

### NEVER Delay Escalation:
- ❌ "Let me look into this and call you back"
- ❌ "I'll talk to the staff member first"
- ❌ "Can we resolve this without involving lawyers?"

## When to Involve CD/PD/HQ

### IMMEDIATE PD + HQ Involvement (ALL cases):
- Any mention of lawsuit or legal action
- Parent has consulted or will consult attorney
- Parent has "evidence" for legal claim
- Threats to file complaints with regulatory bodies
- Discrimination or civil rights allegations
- Claims of negligence or duty of care violations

### Contact Protocol:
1. **Immediate** (within 5 minutes):
   - Text PD: "URGENT - Legal threat from parent of [student name]. Need immediate call back."
   - Email PD + HQ with subject: "URGENT LEGAL THREAT - [Campus] - [Student Name]"

2. **Within 30 minutes**:
   - Create Incident Report
   - Include all documentation
   - Note parent's timeline (e.g., "parent arriving in 2 hours")

3. **Within 1 hour**:
   - PD must speak with parent
   - HQ must be briefed
   - Unified response strategy determined

## Follow-Up Procedures

### After Escalation to PD/HQ:
- **CD's role becomes supportive**, not primary
- PD leads all parent communications
- CD coordinates on-campus logistics (if student being removed)
- CD provides information to PD as requested
- CD does NOT contact parent directly unless instructed by PD

### If Student Being Removed:
- Coordinate with PD on timeline
- Arrange student supervision until parent arrives
- Remove student from programming activities
- Keep student safe, fed, supervised
- Have student pack belongings with staff supervision
- Document departure time and condition
- Complete checkout procedures

### Documentation Requirements:
- Incident Report within 30 minutes of legal threat
- Document every interaction with parent (time, content, outcome)
- Save any emails, voicemails, screenshots parent provides
- Note any witnesses to conversations
- Update case as situation develops
- Final report after student departure (if applicable)

### Response Timeline:
- **Immediate**: Escalate to PD/HQ
- **Within 1-2 hours**: PD contacts parent
- **Within 24 hours**: HQ legal review if needed
- **Ongoing**: PD leads all communications; CD supports

## Common Mistakes

1. **Trying to resolve at campus level**: Legal threats ALWAYS go to PD/HQ
2. **Defending the organization**: Creates legal liability
3. **Admitting fault**: Can be used against organization
4. **Promising outcomes**: "We'll fire that person" creates legal problems
5. **Delaying escalation**: "Let me investigate first" - NO, escalate NOW
6. **Arguing legal merits**: Not your role to debate legal issues
7. **Making refund promises**: Financial decisions are HQ only
8. **Not documenting**: Critical for legal protection
9. **Continuing to engage parent**: After escalation, PD takes over
10. **Discussing with staff member involved before PD guidance**: May compromise investigation

## Key Principles from Training

**From Training Document**:
- "If a parent threatens legal action, escalate immediately"
- "Do not engage with legal claims"
- "PD or HQ will ALWAYS be involved"
- Parent threatens to sue SSB = immediate escalation
- Screenshot evidence = serious concern requiring HQ involvement

**Communication Strategy**:
- Professional, brief, neutral
- Acknowledge seriousness without admitting fault
- Escalate quickly and clearly
- Document everything
- Let PD/HQ lead response

**Protected vs. Admissible Apology**:
- ✅ Protected: "I am very sorry that there has been so much worry and upset. This has been very rough." (Empathy, no admission)
- ❌ Admissible: "I am so sorry I did not get back to you immediately. The error was mine." (Admits fault)

When legal threats are made, use only protected apologies or no apology until PD/HQ guidance.
