# Parent Questions Discipline Decision

**Tags:** discipline, warning, behavior contract, parent disputes, unfair, parent questions, code of conduct, consequence, dismissal, parent pushback, parent communication

## Recognition Triggers
- Parent calls after student received warning or behavior contract
- Parent disputes staff's disciplinary decision
- "Why is my student being punished for this?"
- Parent argues consequence is too harsh or unfair
- Parent claims other students did the same thing without consequence
- Parent wants discipline removed or reduced
- Student told parent their version, parent is challenging staff account
- Parent believes their student is being unfairly targeted

## Assessment

### IMMEDIATE ESCALATION TO CD/PD
- Parent threatens legal action over discipline
- Discipline involves potential dismissal from program
- Parent alleging discrimination or unfair treatment
- Multiple parents complaining about same discipline situation
- Parent demanding refund due to discipline

### HANDLE WITH CD OVERSIGHT
- **All discipline conversations with parents should involve CD**
- Parent questioning verbal warning
- Parent upset about behavior contract
- Parent wants to understand what happened
- Parent believes consequence is too harsh
- CD leads call, PD consulted if escalation likely

### STAFF CAN HANDLE
- Informing parent of minor COC reminder (not formal warning)
- Routine parent communication about behavior expectations
- Must escalate to CD if parent challenges or disputes

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Listen to Parent's Concern (First 3-5 minutes)
- Let parent explain their understanding of situation
- Take notes on what student told them vs. what actually happened
- Listen for specific objections:
  - "My student says they didn't do it"
  - "Other students were doing the same thing"
  - "This seems too harsh"
  - "Why wasn't I called first?"
  - "This is unfair"

**Don't:**
- Interrupt or argue
- Get defensive
- Blame the student
- Dismiss parent's concern

**Do:**
- "I want to make sure I understand your concern. Can you tell me what [student name] shared with you?"
- "Thank you for calling. Let me walk you through what happened from our perspective."

### Step 2: Present the Facts Clearly
**Use objective, factual language:**
- "Here's what happened: [specific behavior, date, time, location]"
- "This violated our Code of Conduct section on [policy]"
- "[Number] students/staff members witnessed this"
- "We spoke with [student name] about it on [date]"

**Provide context:**
- "All students signed the Code of Conduct on check-in day"
- "We reviewed these expectations during orientation"
- "This is the [first/second/third] time this has occurred"

**Explain the disciplinary process:**
- "Our discipline process has three steps: official warning, behavior contract, dismissal"
- "[Student name] is currently at step [1/2]"
- "This is consistent with how we handle all Code of Conduct violations"

### Step 3: Acknowledge Parent's Perspective Without Conceding Policy
**Validation:**
- "I understand you're concerned about [student name]. We are too"
- "I hear that this feels [harsh/unfair/surprising] from your perspective"
- "I know you want what's best for [student name]"

**Bridge to shared values:**
- "We both want [student name] to have a successful summer"
- "Our goal is not to punish but to ensure a safe, respectful environment for all students"
- "We want [student name] to learn from this and move forward positively"

**Engagement Strategy (from training):**
- **Listen**: Understand the root emotion before reacting
- **Bridge**: Acknowledge how parent feels without conceding policy
- **Pivot**: Redirect to values and program mission
- **Repair**: Define exactly what happens next

### Step 4: Explain Why Consequence Is Appropriate
**Policy as shield:**
- "Our Code of Conduct is clear that [behavior] results in [consequence]"
- "We apply this policy consistently with all students"
- "This ensures fairness and safety for the entire community"

**If parent says "other students did the same thing":**
- "I can't speak to other students' situations due to privacy"
- "What I can tell you is that we address all Code of Conduct violations consistently"
- "If you're aware of other violations, please share that information with me so we can address it"

**If parent says consequence is too harsh:**
- "I understand this feels significant. The reason we take [behavior] seriously is [safety/respect/community impact]"
- "A verbal warning/behavior contract gives [student name] a clear opportunity to correct course"
- "We want [student name] to succeed, and this is how we help students understand expectations"

**If parent wants discipline removed:**
- "The decision has been made based on our policy and what occurred"
- "What we can do is support [student name] in moving forward successfully"
- "If [student name] follows expectations going forward, this won't affect their program experience"

### Step 5: Set Expectations Moving Forward
**What happens next:**
- "Here's what [student name] needs to do: [specific expectations]"
- "If [student name] follows these expectations, they will complete the program successfully"
- "If there's another violation, the next step would be [behavior contract/dismissal]"

**Parent's role:**
- "It would be really helpful if you could reinforce these expectations with [student name]"
- "Can you support [student name] in understanding why this is important?"
- "We'll check in with [student name] regularly to make sure they're on track"

**Ongoing communication:**
- "I'll email you a summary of our conversation and the expectations"
- "If there are any concerns, I'll reach out to you immediately"
- "Please feel free to call me if you have questions or if [student name] shares concerns with you"

## Conversation Scripts

### Opening (When You're Calling Parent About Discipline):
"Hi [Parent name], this is [Name], Campus Director at Summer Springboard. I'm calling about a situation that occurred with [student name] today. [Student name] is safe and okay, but I need to update you on a Code of Conduct violation. Do you have a few minutes to talk?"

**Then explain:**
"Today at [time], [student name] [specific behavior]. This violates our Code of Conduct policy on [policy area]. We've spoken with [student name] and issued a [verbal warning/behavior contract]. Here's what that means..."

### Response (When Parent Calls You Upset About Discipline):
"I'm glad you called. Let me make sure I understand what [student name] shared with you, and then I'll walk you through what happened from our perspective."

**After listening:**
"Thank you for sharing that. Here's what we observed: [facts]. I want to explain why we took this action..."

### When Parent Argues Consequence Is Unfair:
"I understand why you feel that way. Our Code of Conduct is designed to keep all students safe and to maintain a respectful community. When [behavior] occurs, we need to address it consistently. The verbal warning/behavior contract isn't meant to punish [student name], but to make clear what our expectations are going forward. [Student name] has a clean path forward to complete the program successfully by following these expectations."

### When Parent Says "My Student Didn't Do It":
"I appreciate you sharing [student name]'s perspective. Let me tell you what we know: [specific facts, witnesses, timeline]. We don't make these decisions lightly. We spoke with [number] students who were present, and this is what we determined based on multiple accounts. I understand [student name] may remember it differently, but based on our investigation, this is what occurred."

### When Parent Wants to Argue or Debate:
"I understand you disagree with this decision. The decision has been made based on our policy and the facts as we understand them. What I'd like to focus on now is helping [student name] move forward successfully. Are you willing to partner with us on that?"

## Explaining Each Discipline Step to Parents

### Step 1: Official Warning
**What it is:**
- Formal notification that behavior violated Code of Conduct
- May be verbal or written
- Two staff members present when issued
- Documented in student file

**What it means:**
- "[Student name] can continue in program"
- "This is a clear notice that the behavior must stop"
- "If there's another violation, the next step is a behavior contract"
- "We want [student name] to learn from this and move forward"

**Parent's role:**
- "Please reinforce with [student name] that this is serious"
- "Help [student name] understand why the behavior was inappropriate"
- "Encourage [student name] to follow expectations going forward"

### Step 2: Behavior Contract
**What it is:**
- Written agreement signed by student, CD, and witness
- Clearly states: behaviors violated, expectations moving forward, consequences for additional violations
- Student must call parent to inform them (with staff oversight)
- Parent contacted by CD
- Uploaded to Incident Report

**What it means:**
- "This is the final warning before dismissal"
- "[Student name] is one violation away from being sent home"
- "We're giving [student name] one more chance to correct course"
- "Parent is responsible for arranging and covering cost of transportation home if student is dismissed"

**Parent's role:**
- "This is very serious. Please have a conversation with [student name] about the importance of following expectations"
- "The next violation will result in [student name] being sent home at your expense"
- "We need your support in helping [student name] understand the gravity of this situation"

### Step 3: Dismissal from Program
**What it is:**
- Student removed from program due to violation of behavior contract or severe misconduct
- PD or HQ ALWAYS involved in dismissal decision
- Student required to return home on next available flight/transportation
- Parent/guardian pays for transportation
- **Program tuition and fees are non-refundable in cases of dismissal**
- Dismissal is final; student may not return once removed

**Process:**
1. Before informing student, CD confirms with PD
2. Student informed of removal and reason
3. Student calls parent to inform them and arrange transportation
4. CD/Mentor speaks with parent to confirm SSB will follow up with details
5. Student supervised and kept safe until departure
6. If dropped at airport, staff stays until student passes through security
7. HQ notified
8. Debrief with remaining students and staff
9. Incident Report completed

**Parent conversation:**
"I need to inform you that [student name] has been dismissed from the program due to [violation]. This decision has been made in consultation with our Program Director and headquarters. [Student name] will need to return home on the next available flight, which you will need to arrange and pay for. Please understand that program tuition and fees are non-refundable in cases of dismissal. I know this is difficult news. Let's discuss the logistics of [student name]'s departure."

## Standing Firm on Discipline

### When Parent Pushes Back:
**Maintain professional boundaries:**
- "I understand this is disappointing/frustrating/upsetting"
- "The decision stands based on our policy"
- "What I can offer is support for [student name] to move forward successfully"

**Don't:**
- Argue or debate
- Get pulled into "what ifs" or hypotheticals
- Agree that consequence is unfair (undermines policy)
- Change discipline decision to appease parent (unless PD approves)

**Do:**
- Listen to parent's full concern
- Restate the facts and policy
- Redirect to moving forward
- Offer partnership in supporting student

### If Parent Escalates:
**Parent threatens to call headquarters:**
- "You're welcome to contact our main office. I'm confident that our approach is consistent with our policies. Here's the number: [SSB main line]"
- Document that parent plans to escalate
- Notify PD immediately

**Parent threatens legal action:**
- See separate protocol: "Parent Threatens Legal Action"
- Immediately escalate to PD/HQ
- Do not engage with legal claims
- "Given that you've mentioned legal action, I need to involve our Program Director. They will contact you within [timeframe]"

**Parent demands refund:**
- "I understand you're upset. Our tuition policy is that fees are non-refundable once the program has started"
- "If you'd like to discuss financial matters, I can have our headquarters team contact you"
- Do NOT promise or discuss refunds - that's HQ decision only

## What NOT to Say/Promise

**DON'T Undermine the Discipline:**
- ❌ "I agree it seems harsh, but it's policy"
- ❌ "Between you and me, I think it's too much too"
- ❌ "Other staff members didn't want to do this"

**DON'T Blame the Student Harshly:**
- ❌ "Your student was out of control"
- ❌ "Your student is the worst we've had"
- ❌ "Your student is a troublemaker"

**DON'T Promise to Reduce or Remove Discipline:**
- ❌ "I'll see if we can reduce it to just a verbal warning"
- ❌ "Maybe we can just forget about this"
- ❌ "I'll talk to my supervisor about removing this"
(Only PD can approve changing discipline decision)

**DON'T Violate Other Students' Privacy:**
- ❌ "Well, [other student name] did it too and got the same consequence"
- ❌ "Three other students were involved and they all got warnings"

**DON'T Admit Uncertainty or Inconsistency:**
- ❌ "I'm not sure this was the right call"
- ❌ "We might have handled this differently with another student"
- ❌ "I was overruled by my supervisor"

## When to Involve CD/PD

### CD Involvement (ALL discipline parent calls):
- CD should make the call to parent about discipline
- If mentor issued warning, CD still calls parent
- CD explains policy, answers questions, manages pushback

### PD Involvement:
- Parent threatens legal action
- Parent demands refund
- Parent plans to escalate to headquarters
- Parent very upset and CD needs backup
- Discipline decision being questioned and CD wants support
- Parent requesting policy exception
- Potential dismissal situation

### HQ Involvement:
- Dismissal decisions (always)
- Refund requests
- Legal threats
- Formal complaints
- Parent bypassing CD/PD and calling main office directly

## Follow-Up Procedures

### After Discipline Parent Call:
**Documentation:**
- Create or update Incident Report within 24 hours
- Document parent's response (supportive, upset, neutral)
- Note any concerns parent raised
- Record next steps and agreements

**Email Summary to Parent:**
Within 24 hours, send email summarizing:
- What happened (brief, factual)
- Policy violated
- Consequence issued
- Expectations moving forward
- Your contact information
- Appreciation for parent partnership

**Student Check-In:**
- Talk with student after parent call
- "I spoke with your parent about [situation]. How are you feeling?"
- Ensure student understands expectations
- Offer support for moving forward
- Monitor student's attitude and compliance

**Staff Check-In:**
- Brief staff about parent conversation
- Ensure consistent messaging if parent calls again
- Alert staff to monitor student's behavior
- Support staff if parent was difficult

### Monitoring After Discipline:
**If verbal warning issued:**
- Increased check-ins with student for 2-3 days
- Monitor for compliance and attitude
- Positive reinforcement when student follows expectations
- Document improvement or ongoing concerns

**If behavior contract issued:**
- Daily check-ins with student
- Close monitoring of all activities
- Any violation escalated to PD immediately
- Regular communication with parent (daily or every other day)
- Clear documentation of student's conduct

**If dismissal occurred:**
- Debrief with remaining students
- Debrief with staff
- Monitor program climate
- Parent communication through PD/HQ
- Complete all documentation

### Case Closure:
- When student successfully completes program after discipline, note resolution in file
- Final positive communication to parent if appropriate
- Close Incident Report with outcome

## Common Mistakes

1. **Getting defensive**: "We didn't do anything wrong" instead of validating parent concern
2. **Undermining discipline**: Agreeing with parent that it's too harsh
3. **Not having facts ready**: Can't clearly explain what happened and when
4. **Making excuses**: "We were short-staffed" instead of focusing on policy
5. **Comparing students**: "Well, your student isn't the only one" violates privacy
6. **Promising to reconsider**: Raising false hope about changing decision
7. **Not involving PD early enough**: Handling escalated parent alone
8. **Poor documentation**: Not recording parent conversation in IR
9. **Not following up**: Promising to send email and not doing it
10. **Leaving student in the dark**: Not talking to student after parent conversation

## Key Principles from Training

**Discipline Philosophy:**
- "Consistency and documentation matter"
- "Behavior issues should never be handled informally once patterns emerge"
- Three-step process ensures fairness and escalation
- Goal is education and behavior change, not punishment
- All students held to same standard

**Parent Communication:**
- Professional neutrality keeps emotions from escalating
- Policy as shield: lean on pre-established guidelines
- Acknowledge feelings without conceding policy
- Pivot to shared values: student's success and safety
- Partner with parent to support student moving forward

**Documentation:**
- "Documentation matters!"
- Every discipline action documented in Incident Report
- Parent conversations logged
- Pattern tracking over time
- Protects organization and ensures consistency

**Standing Firm:**
- Discipline decisions are not negotiable with parents unless PD approves change
- Explain clearly, listen to concerns, but hold boundary
- If parent escalates, involve PD/HQ
- Never undermine discipline to appease parent
- Trust the process and the policy
