# Student Wants to Leave / Go Home - Homesickness

**Tags:** wants to leave, wants to go home, homesick, parent picking up, early departure, withdrawal, miserable, unhappy, crying, bored, parent concern, voluntary departure, retention

## Recognition Triggers
- Student says "I want to go home" or "I don't want to be here anymore"
- Student is crying, withdrawn, not participating
- Student calls parent asking to be picked up
- Parent calls saying student is "bored" and might "fly home early"
- Student reports not feeling safe or comfortable
- Emotional distress, isolation from peers
- Student fakes illness to avoid activities
- Refusing to leave dorm room or engage with group

## Assessment

### IMMEDIATE ESCALATION TO CD/PD
- Student expresses suicidal ideation or self-harm thoughts
- Student has safety concerns (threats, harassment, assault)
- Medical or mental health emergency underlying request
- Student has already called parent and parent is en route

### HANDLE WITH CD OVERSIGHT
- General homesickness or adjustment issues
- Student unhappy with program but no safety concerns
- Parent calling to discuss student's desire to leave
- Student wants to leave after conflict with peers
- CD coordinates with PD before any decision to send student home

### STAFF CAN HANDLE (INITIALLY)
- Mentor check-in with homesick student
- Initial emotional support and listening
- Connecting student with peers or activities
- Must escalate to CD if student insists on leaving or situation doesn't improve

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Private Conversation with Student (First 10-15 minutes)
- Pull student aside to quiet, private space
- Use active listening without judgment
- Ask open-ended questions:
  - "Tell me what's making you want to leave"
  - "What's been going on?"
  - "When did you start feeling this way?"
  - "Has something specific happened?"

**Key Assessment Questions:**
- Is this homesickness or a safety concern?
- Has student been bullied, harassed, or threatened?
- Is there a conflict with roommate or peers?
- Is student physically ill or in pain?
- Has student made friends or connected with anyone?
- What did student expect vs. reality?

### Step 2: Assess Safety and Severity
**Immediate escalation required if:**
- Student mentions wanting to hurt themselves
- Student reports harassment, assault, or threats
- Student has been victim of COC violation
- Student has medical or mental health emergency
- Student is in crisis

**Continue support if:**
- Normal adjustment/homesickness
- Missing family/friends
- Program not meeting expectations
- Conflict with peers (non-safety issue)
- Feeling left out or isolated

### Step 3: Validation and Support
- Acknowledge feelings without immediately problem-solving
- "It's normal to feel homesick. A lot of students feel this way in the first few days"
- "I understand this is hard. Tell me more about what would help"
- "You're not alone in feeling this way"

**Explore what's behind the feeling:**
- "What would be different at home?"
- "What's the hardest part of being here?"
- "Is there anything specific that happened today?"

### Step 4: Problem-Solving and Engagement
**If homesickness/adjustment:**
- Connect student with peer mentor or buddy
- Identify one positive thing student has experienced
- Set small, achievable goals (attend next meal, one activity)
- Increase mentor check-ins
- Facilitate call home with parent for reassurance (WITH staff guidance)

**If specific problem (roommate, schedule, activity):**
- Problem-solve the specific issue
- "What would make this better for you?"
- Consider room change, schedule adjustment if appropriate
- Connect student to resources

**If boredom/unmet expectations:**
- Review upcoming exciting activities
- Connect student with club or interest group
- Pair with student who shares interests

### Step 5: Involve Campus Director
**CD notification required when:**
- Student still wants to leave after initial support
- Parent has been contacted by student
- Situation not improving after 24 hours
- You're unsure how to proceed
- Student has specific ongoing complaint

**CD will:**
- Meet with student one-on-one
- Assess whether departure is necessary
- Contact PD for guidance if needed
- Coordinate parent communication
- Make final decision on retention vs. departure

## Parent Communication Protocol

### If Student Has Already Called Parent:
**Student says parent is coming to get them:**
1. Immediately notify CD
2. CD calls parent to understand situation
3. CD explains what staff have observed
4. CD and PD decide on response
5. If parent insists on pickup, coordinate logistics

### If CD Needs to Call Parent (Student Wants to Leave):
**Call objectives:**
1. Inform parent of student's emotional state
2. Explain what staff are doing to support
3. Get parent's perspective and support
4. Decide together on best path forward
5. Set timeline for reassessment

**Opening script:**
"Hi [Parent name], this is [Name], Campus Director at Summer Springboard. [Student name] is physically fine and safe, but I wanted to call you because they've been feeling homesick and have expressed wanting to go home. I want to update you on what we're seeing and get your input."

**Collaborative approach:**
- "Here's what we're observing..."
- "Has [student name] mentioned this to you?"
- "What has helped in the past when [student name] has felt this way?"
- "We'd like to try [interventions] over the next 24-48 hours and see if things improve"
- "How do you think we should approach this?"

**Setting expectations:**
- "Let's give it until [specific time, e.g., end of tomorrow] to see if [student name] adjusts"
- "I'll check in with [student name] after [activity] and call you tomorrow at [time] to update you"
- "If things don't improve or if [student name] is really struggling, we can discuss next steps"

### Parent Calls You (Student Told Parent They're Miserable):
**Parent: "My kid called me crying and wants to come home"**

1. **Gather information:**
   - "When did [student name] call you?"
   - "What specifically did they say?"
   - "How did they sound?"

2. **Provide your observations:**
   - "Let me check in with our staff and [student name] right away"
   - "I'll call you back within [1-2 hours] after I've spoken with them"
   - OR "I just saw [student name] at [activity] and they seemed [observation]. Let me talk with them privately about what's going on"

3. **Collaborative plan:**
   - "Let's work together on this"
   - "What do you think would help [student name]?"
   - "Would it help for [student name] to call you tonight after [activity]?"

4. **Avoid:**
   - Dismissing parent concern: "They're fine, all kids feel this way"
   - Blaming student: "They just need to try harder to make friends"
   - Promising immediate solution: "We'll fix this right away"

## Retention vs. Safety Decision Framework

### RETAIN (Continue Program with Support):
- Homesickness without safety concerns
- Normal adjustment period struggles (first 2-3 days)
- Student willing to try support strategies
- Parent supportive of student staying
- No ongoing bullying or harassment
- Student has moments of positive engagement
- Specific problem can be addressed (room change, schedule adjustment)

**Support Plan:**
- Increased mentor check-ins (2-3x daily)
- Buddy system with compatible peer
- Daily call home at scheduled time
- Small achievable goals
- CD check-in after 24 hours
- Parent update after 24-48 hours

### SAFETY DEPARTURE (Student Should Leave):
- Student in immediate danger (threats, assault)
- Severe mental health crisis
- Student has been victim of serious COC violation
- Medical emergency requiring home care
- Student safety cannot be ensured on campus
- Mutual agreement student cannot continue

**Departure Process:**
1. CD consults with PD immediately
2. Parent called to arrange pickup/flight
3. Student supervised and kept safe until departure
4. Remove from activities, provide safe space
5. Incident Report completed
6. Debrief with staff

### VOLUNTARY DEPARTURE (Student Chooses to Leave):
- Student and parent mutually decide to leave after consultation
- No safety issue, but student unable to adjust
- Trial period did not result in improvement
- Student's wellbeing better served at home
- **Tuition is non-refundable** (must communicate this clearly)

**Process:**
1. CD and PD approve departure
2. Parent arranges and pays for transportation
3. Early departure documented as "voluntary"
4. Complete checkout procedures
5. Incident Report filed
6. No refund provided (per policy)

## Conversation Scripts

### With Student:
**Initial check-in:**
"I've noticed you seem a bit down. Do you have a few minutes to talk? I want to make sure you're okay."

**When student says they want to go home:**
"I hear that you're having a hard time. Tell me more about what's going on. What's making you want to leave?"

**Validation:**
"It's really normal to feel homesick. You're not the first person to feel this way, and you won't be the last. A lot of students feel like this in the first few days."

**Exploring solutions:**
"What would make things better for you here? Is there something specific that would help?"

**Setting small goals:**
"I know it feels overwhelming right now. What if we take it one step at a time? Let's just get through dinner tonight, and we can check in after that. How does that sound?"

### With Parent:
**When parent is supportive of student staying:**
"Thank you for partnering with us on this. Here's what we're going to do to support [student name]... I'll call you tomorrow at [time] to update you on how they're doing."

**When parent wants to pick up student:**
"I understand this is hard to hear [student name] is struggling. Before making a decision, would you be open to giving us 24 hours to work with [student name] on some strategies? I'll call you tomorrow at this time, and if things haven't improved, we can discuss next steps."

**When parent is angry at program:**
"I hear your concern. Our goal is the same as yours - we want [student name] to have a positive, safe experience. Let's talk about what's been happening and how we can address this together."

## What NOT to Say/Promise

### To Student:
- ❌ "You're fine, stop being dramatic"
- ❌ "Everyone feels this way, just get over it"
- ❌ "You're the only one having this problem"
- ❌ "Your parents paid a lot of money for this"
- ❌ "Just try harder to make friends"
- ❌ "You can go home if you want" (without CD approval)

### To Parent:
- ❌ "Your student is overreacting"
- ❌ "All kids feel homesick, they'll be fine"
- ❌ "There's nothing wrong with the program"
- ❌ "We can refund your tuition" (tuition is non-refundable)
- ❌ "Your student will get in trouble if they leave"
- ❌ "You're being overprotective"

## Follow-Up Procedures

### First 24 Hours:
- Mentor checks in with student 3x daily minimum
- CD checks in with student once
- Monitor meal attendance, activity participation
- Document student's mood and engagement
- Facilitate scheduled call home if agreed with parent

### 24-48 Hour Reassessment:
- CD meets with student again
- Evaluate improvement or decline
- CD calls parent with update
- Decide: continue with support, escalate intervention, or arrange departure

### If Student Stays:
- Continue increased support for 3-5 days
- Gradually reduce check-ins as student stabilizes
- Document improvement in case notes
- Close case when student fully integrated
- Final parent update when student is stable

### If Student Leaves:
- Complete Incident Report within 24 hours
- Document reason for departure (safety vs. voluntary)
- Note that tuition is non-refundable
- File all parent communications
- Debrief with remaining students if needed
- Debrief with staff about how to better support future students

## Common Mistakes

1. **Dismissing feelings**: "You're fine" instead of validating
2. **Forcing participation**: Making student go to activities when in crisis
3. **Not involving CD early enough**: Trying to handle alone when escalation needed
4. **Promising student they can leave**: Only CD/PD can make that decision
5. **Not documenting**: Failing to track interventions and outcomes
6. **Comparing to other students**: "Everyone else is fine"
7. **Blaming student**: "You just need to try harder"
8. **Not coordinating with parent**: Parent hears from student only, not staff
9. **Refund promises**: Tuition is non-refundable per policy
10. **Waiting too long**: Not addressing until crisis point

## Key Principles

**From Training:**
- Homesickness is normal, especially first 2-3 days
- Early intervention prevents escalation
- Parent partnership is critical
- Safety always comes first
- Retention should be goal when safe and appropriate
- Non-refundable tuition policy must be communicated
- Documentation protects student and organization
- CD makes final decision with PD support

**Support Philosophy:**
- Meet student where they are emotionally
- Small steps, achievable goals
- Connect to peers and community
- Address specific problems (don't just say "stick it out")
- Parent communication and partnership
- Know when departure is the right answer
