# Substance Violations - Behavioral Issue

**Tags:** alcohol, vape, vaping, drug, substance, weed, marijuana, drinking, smoking, possession, intoxication, dismissal, code of conduct violation, drunk, high, juul, e-cigarette, behavioral issue

## Recognition Triggers
- Smell of vape, smoke, or alcohol in dorm rooms or on students
- Students appearing intoxicated (slurred speech, unsteady, glassy eyes)
- Hangover symptoms (pale, sweaty, nauseous, headache, hand sanitizer smell + body spray)
- Students unusually nervous or defensive during room checks
- Witness reports from other students
- Items knocked over or hidden quickly when staff approaches
- Students refusing to open door fully during checks
- Discovery of paraphernalia (vape pens, bottles, packaging)
- Students asking to skip class due to feeling ill after late-night activities
- Confession from roommates or other students

## Severity Assessment

### ZERO TOLERANCE (Immediate Dismissal)
- Possession of alcohol, vaping devices, tobacco products, illegal drugs
- Consumption or use of prohibited substances
- Buying or selling illegal substances
- Attempting to purchase prohibited substances
- Assisting others in obtaining prohibited substances
- Sharing substances with other students
- Possession with intent to distribute

**IMPORTANT:** ALL substance violations are zero tolerance. There is no "first warning" for possession or use.

### ESCALATING CONSEQUENCE (Warning → Contract → Dismissal)
- Remaining in a situation where prohibited substances are being used WITHOUT removing oneself and notifying staff
  - First offense: verbal warning
  - Second offense: written contract
  - Third offense: dismissal

### RESTORATIVE (Mediation/Education)
- NOT APPLICABLE for substance violations
- Being present is only situation that allows for progressive discipline
- Any possession or use = immediate dismissal track

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Immediate Intervention

**When Substance Use is Suspected:**

1. **Do Not Enter Room Alone:**
   - Always have second staff member present
   - Knock and identify yourself
   - If smell detected, note it immediately

2. **Request Entry:**
   - "We need to come in and talk with you"
   - If students refuse or seem nervous: "We're coming in. Please open the door all the way"
   - Enter with second staff, scan room visually

3. **Assess Student Condition:**
   - Are students showing signs of intoxication?
   - Does anyone need medical attention?
   - Are students coherent enough to have conversation?
   - Check for safety concerns (extreme intoxication requiring medical care)

4. **Secure Any Visible Substances:**
   - If alcohol/vape/drugs visible: confiscate immediately
   - Do NOT return to student
   - Document exactly what was found (brand, amount, location)
   - Take photos if possible

5. **Separate Students:**
   - Each student involved goes with different staff member
   - No communication between students before interviews
   - Supervision at all times

### Step 2: Investigation

**Room Search Protocol:**

**IMPORTANT:** Staff reserves the right to search student belongings when drug, tobacco, or alcohol possession is suspected. However:
- Room searches must be approved by HQ (call PD/HQ before conducting)
- Searches should only be conducted when safety risk exists
- Two staff members must be present
- Document everything found
- Take photos of evidence

**Search Procedure:**
1. Call HQ to get approval FIRST
2. Inform student: "We have reason to believe there may be prohibited substances in this room. We need to conduct a search"
3. Two staff conduct search together
4. Document what's found, where it was found
5. Photograph evidence
6. Confiscate all prohibited items
7. Items will NOT be returned to student

**Investigation Interviews:**

**Interview Each Student Separately:**

1. **Start with Facts:**
   - "Tell me what happened tonight"
   - "What were you doing when [staff] came to your room?"
   - "Was anyone drinking/vaping/using substances?"

2. **Direct Questions:**
   - "Did you drink alcohol tonight?"
   - "Did you use a vape or smoke?"
   - "Where did the [substance] come from?"
   - "Who brought it?"
   - "Who consumed it?"
   - "How did you get it?" (stealing vs. brought from home vs. purchased)

3. **Assess Involvement:**
   - Who possessed the substance?
   - Who consumed it?
   - Who was present but didn't participate?
   - Who purchased or stole it?
   - Who knew about it but didn't report?

4. **Document Everything:**
   - Write down exact quotes
   - Note student's demeanor (defensive, remorseful, confused)
   - Document any admissions
   - Note any inconsistencies between accounts

**Interview Witnesses/Roommates:**
- "What did you see tonight?"
- "Were you aware of any alcohol/drugs/vaping?"
- "Did you participate?"
- "Why didn't you report this or remove yourself from the situation?"

**Determine Each Student's Level of Involvement:**
- **Active Participant:** Possessed, consumed, or purchased = DISMISSAL
- **Facilitator:** Helped obtain, covered for others, assisted = DISMISSAL
- **Bystander:** Was present but didn't use, didn't report = PROGRESSIVE DISCIPLINE (warning/contract)
- **Reporter/Separated:** Left situation and reported = NO DISCIPLINE

### Step 3: Discipline Decision

**Call PD/HQ IMMEDIATELY for all substance violations:**
- Do NOT make dismissal decision alone
- PD/HQ must be involved in all substance cases
- Document who you spoke with and their guidance

**Decision Matrix:**

**IMMEDIATE DISMISSAL (Must consult PD/HQ first):**
- Possessed alcohol, drugs, tobacco, or vaping devices
- Consumed any prohibited substance
- Purchased or attempted to purchase
- Stole substances from store or elsewhere
- Shared substances with other students
- Bought/sold substances
- Brought substances from home
- Assisted others in obtaining substances

**WRITTEN CONTRACT:**
- Second offense of remaining in situation where substances were present
- Student was aware of substance use and didn't report

**VERBAL WARNING:**
- First offense of being present where substances were used but:
  - Student did not use
  - Student was unaware when they entered situation
  - Student shows understanding of responsibility to remove self and report

**Special Considerations:**

**If Student Says "My Parents Let Me Drink at Home":**
- Irrelevant. Program policy prohibits it even with parental consent
- Note this in incident report but doesn't change consequence

**If Substance Was Brought From Home:**
- Parents may have unknowingly packed it
- Still results in dismissal
- Document in incident report

**If Student Stole Substance:**
- Double violation: theft + substance
- Dismissal for both
- May need to involve local police/store
- Restitution required

### Step 4: Implementation

**Dismissal Process for Substance Violations:**

**BEFORE Informing Student - REQUIRED STEPS:**
1. Call PD/HQ to confirm dismissal decision
2. Complete investigation with all students involved
3. Secure all evidence (substances, photos)
4. Identify all students being dismissed
5. Arrange supervision plan for overnight (if late night)
6. Prepare timeline for parents

**Informing Student(s) of Dismissal:**

**Two staff must be present:**

1. **Private Location** (office or separate room)

2. **Clear, Direct Statement:**
   "Based on our investigation tonight, we've determined that you [possessed/consumed/purchased] [substance]. This is a violation of our Code of Conduct policy on drugs and alcohol."

   "We've been in touch with our headquarters, and the decision has been made that you will need to return home from the program."

3. **Explain Why:**
   "Our policy on alcohol, drugs, tobacco, and vaping is zero tolerance. This means any possession or use results in dismissal from the program."

4. **Next Steps:**
   "What needs to happen now is you'll call your parent/guardian and let them know what occurred and that you need to be picked up or have a flight arranged."

   "You'll remain supervised until you depart. You will not participate in any more program activities."

**If Student Says "But My Mom Lets Me Drink at Home":**
"That may be true at home, but our program policy is clear: no alcohol, even with parental permission. The Code of Conduct you signed at enrollment outlined this policy."

**If Student Blames Others:**
"I've heard from everyone involved. Regardless of who brought it or whose idea it was, you chose to [possess/consume] it, which violates our policy."

**Student Calls Parent/Guardian:**
- Staff member must be present to overhear call
- Ensure student is actually calling parent (not friend)
- Student explains what happened
- If student minimizes or lies: staff interjects with truth
- CD gets on phone after student explains

**CD Conversation with Parent:**

"Hi [parent name], this is [CD name], Campus Director at [location]. I'm here with [student]. As [he/she] just explained, we discovered that [student] [possessed/consumed] [substance] tonight."

"Our policy on alcohol, drugs, tobacco, and vaping is zero tolerance. This was outlined in the Code of Conduct signed at enrollment. The consequence for possession or use is immediate dismissal from the program."

"What we need from you now is to arrange for [student] to return home by [timeframe - usually 24 hours]. Can you drive to pick [him/her] up, or do you need to arrange a flight?"

"[Student] will remain supervised and safe until departure. You'll receive a full incident report within 24 hours detailing our investigation."

"Tuition and fees are non-refundable in cases of dismissal, which was outlined in the enrollment agreement."

**Coordinate Transportation:**
- Parents arrange and pay for transportation home
- Student must depart within 24 hours (sooner if possible)
- If flying: book flight for next available departure
- Parents provide flight details to CD
- Confirm pickup time if driving

**Supervision Until Departure:**
- Student removed from all programming activities immediately
- Supervised at all times
- Kept fed and safe
- Separated from other students to prevent additional conflicts
- No access to phone/social media unsupervised (prevent warning other students)

**Departure Day:**
- Pack belongings with staff supervision
- No goodbyes with other students
- If flying: staff drives to airport and stays until student passes through security
- Call HQ/PD when student has departed
- Confirm with parent that student arrived home safely

### Step 5: Documentation

**Incident Report (REQUIRED within 24 hours):**

Include in Salesforce Incident Report:
1. **Timeline:**
   - When suspicion arose (time, who noticed)
   - When investigation began
   - Who was interviewed and when
   - When substances were confiscated
   - When PD/HQ was called
   - When parent was notified
   - When student departed

2. **Evidence:**
   - Photos of substances found
   - Description of items (brand, amount, type)
   - Where items were found
   - Photos of room/location

3. **Student Accounts:**
   - What each student said (exact quotes when possible)
   - Who admitted to what
   - Any denials or inconsistencies

4. **Witness Statements:**
   - What staff observed (smells, behavior)
   - What other students reported

5. **Discipline Actions:**
   - Who was dismissed
   - Who received warnings/contracts
   - Rationale for each decision

6. **Parent Communication:**
   - Time of call
   - Parent's response
   - Transportation arrangements
   - Flight details or pickup time

7. **PD/HQ Consultation:**
   - Who you spoke with
   - Their guidance/approval
   - Any special instructions

**Additional Documentation:**
- Written statements from witnesses
- Screenshots if any text/social media evidence
- Photos of confiscated items
- Copy of student's original Code of Conduct signature

**If Theft Involved:**
- Store incident report (if applicable)
- Police report number (if filed)
- Restitution agreement
- Parent acknowledgment of theft

## Conversation Scripts

### Initial Confrontation When Substance Suspected

**Knock on Door:**
"[Student names], this is [Staff Name]. We need to talk with you. Please open the door."

**If Door Opens Partially:**
"We need to come in. Please open the door all the way."

**Upon Entering (if smell detected):**
"We're smelling [vape/alcohol/smoke]. We need to talk about what's been going on in here tonight."

**If Substances Visible:**
"I can see [bottle/vape pen/etc]. We need to confiscate that. Please hand it over."

**If Students Say "We Weren't Doing Anything":**
"I need each of you to come with a different staff member so we can talk about what happened tonight. [Student A], you come with me. [Student B], go with [other staff]."

### Investigation Interview - Suspected User

**Opening:**
"I want to hear from you what happened tonight. Tell me in your own words what was going on in your room."

**Direct Questions:**
"Did you drink any alcohol tonight?"
"Did you use a vape pen or any tobacco product?"
"Where did the [substance] come from?"

**If Student Denies:**
"We found [substance] in your room. Other students have told us [information]. I need you to be honest with me about what happened."

**If Student Admits:**
"I appreciate you being honest. I need to ask you some more questions about how this happened."

"How did you get the [substance]?"
"Who else was involved?"
"Did anyone else consume it?"

### Investigation Interview - Bystander Who Was Present

**Opening:**
"I know you were in the room tonight when [incident] happened. I need to understand your involvement."

**Key Questions:**
"What did you see/hear/smell?"
"Did you use any [substance]?"
"When did you realize [substance] was being used?"
"Why didn't you leave the situation or find a staff member?"

**Educational Moment:**
"Our policy says that if you're in a situation where prohibited substances are being used, you need to remove yourself and notify staff. By staying, you're in violation of the Code of Conduct even if you didn't use anything."

### Dismissal Conversation - Possession/Use

**Two Staff Present:**

"[Student], we need to talk about what happened last night. Our investigation has confirmed that you [possessed/consumed] [alcohol/vape/drugs]."

"This is a serious violation of our Code of Conduct. The policy on substances is zero tolerance, which means any possession or use results in dismissal from the program."

"We've consulted with our headquarters, and the decision has been made that you will need to return home."

"I know this is very disappointing. Our policy is clear and applies to everyone equally."

"What needs to happen now is you need to call your parents and tell them what happened and that they need to arrange for you to go home."

**If Student Protests:**
"The decision is final. I understand you're upset, but this policy was in the Code of Conduct you signed at enrollment. Let's focus on getting you home safely."

**If Student Says "It Wasn't Mine":**
"It was in your possession / you admitted to using it. The policy is clear regardless of whose it originally was."

### Dismissal Conversation - Theft + Substance

**Two Staff Present:**

"[Student], we need to talk about what happened at [store] yesterday and what we found in your room last night."

"Our investigation has determined that you stole alcohol from [store] and then consumed it with other students."

"This is actually two serious violations: theft and substance use. Either one alone results in dismissal from the program."

"We take both of these extremely seriously. You'll need to return home from the program."

"Additionally, we may need to address the theft with the store and potentially with local police. Your parents will need to handle any legal consequences and restitution."

### Parent Notification - Possession/Use

"Hi [Parent], this is [CD Name] from Summer Springboard at [campus]. I'm calling about a serious situation involving [student]."

"Last night, our staff discovered that [student] had [possessed/consumed] [alcohol/vape/marijuana] [in the dorm/during the program]."

"We conducted a thorough investigation, speaking with [student] and witnesses. [Student] [admitted to/was found with] the substance."

"Our policy on alcohol, drugs, tobacco, and vaping is zero tolerance. This policy was outlined in the Code of Conduct signed at enrollment. The consequence is immediate dismissal from the program."

"[Student] needs to return home. We need you to arrange transportation - either by driving to pick [him/her] up or arranging a flight. [Student] should depart by [timeframe]."

"[Student] will be supervised and safe until departure. We'll send you a detailed incident report within 24 hours."

"I know this is very difficult news. Do you have questions about what happened or the next steps?"

**If Parent Says "Are You Sure?":**
"Yes. We have [physical evidence/admission/multiple witness accounts]. Our investigation was thorough."

**If Parent Says "It Was Just One Mistake":**
"I understand this may be out of character for [student]. Unfortunately, our safety policy doesn't allow for exceptions. Substance use creates risk for all students in the program."

**If Parent Says "My Child Has Never Done This Before":**
"I hear that, and this may be the first time. However, our policy is designed to keep all students safe and must be applied consistently."

### Parent Notification - Present But Didn't Use (Warning)

"Hi [Parent], this is [CD Name] from Summer Springboard. I need to talk with you about a situation involving [student]."

"Last night, we discovered that some students in [student's] room were using [substance]. Our investigation confirmed that [student] was present but did not use any substances."

"However, our policy requires students to remove themselves from situations where prohibited substances are being used and notify staff immediately. [Student] chose to stay in the room, which is a violation of expectations."

"This is serving as a verbal warning. The other students involved have been dismissed from the program. [Student] is receiving a warning because [he/she] didn't participate, but [he/she] did fail to remove [himself/herself] or report it."

"I've had a serious conversation with [student] about the responsibility to remove yourself from these situations and find staff immediately."

"If [student] is ever in a similar situation again and doesn't report it or remove [himself/herself], [he/she] will be placed on a written behavioral contract or dismissed."

"I need your help reinforcing this expectation with [student]. Do you have any questions?"

## What NOT to Say/Do

### DO NOT:
- Search rooms without HQ approval
- Make dismissal decision without consulting PD/HQ
- Return confiscated substances to students
- Allow students to "sleep it off" without medical assessment if intoxicated
- Fail to separate students before interviews
- Accept "it wasn't mine" without investigation
- Make exceptions based on "good kid" or "first offense"
- Delay parent notification
- Allow dismissed student unsupervised access to other students
- Skip documentation
- Let student keep phone unsupervised (may warn others)

### NEVER SAY:
- "Everyone makes mistakes"
- "I did this when I was your age"
- "It's just vaping, not that serious"
- "I won't tell if it doesn't happen again"
- "Maybe we can make an exception"
- "Let's keep this between us"
- "I'll give you one more chance"
- "This will blow over"

### Legal/Policy Pitfalls:
- Substance violations may require reporting to local authorities (especially marijuana in states where it's illegal for minors)
- Theft requires restitution and may involve police report
- Never allow student to "dispose of" substances (you need evidence)
- Document parent consent for any searches if possible
- If student is severely intoxicated: medical assessment required before discipline conversation

## Parent Notification

### WHEN to Call:
- IMMEDIATELY after dismissal decision is made (after consulting PD/HQ)
- Within 2-4 hours of discovering substance violation
- Same night if possible (even if late) for serious violations
- Next morning at latest

### WHO Calls:
- Campus Director makes the call
- PD may join call for complex cases
- Have second staff member present to witness call

### WHAT to Say - Structure:

1. **Identify self and program**
2. **State the situation clearly**: "[Student] possessed/consumed [substance]"
3. **Explain policy**: "Zero tolerance policy on substances"
4. **State consequence**: "Dismissal from program"
5. **Request transportation arrangements**: "Need pickup/flight by [time]"
6. **Offer to answer questions**
7. **Confirm contact method for follow-up**

### How to Handle Pushback:

**"Can't you give them another chance?"**
"I understand you want that. Our zero tolerance policy on substances doesn't allow for exceptions. This policy exists to keep all students safe and must be applied consistently."

**"How do you know it was theirs?"**
"[It was found in their possession / They admitted to it / Multiple witnesses confirmed]. Our investigation was thorough."

**"Other kids do this all the time"**
"We investigate every situation where we suspect substance use. If we find violations, consequences are applied consistently."

**"This will ruin their college applications"**
"This is a program dismissal, not a school record issue. However, violating substance policies has natural consequences, which is why we take it seriously."

**"My kid says someone else brought it"**
"We've investigated who brought it. However, choosing to possess or use it is still a violation regardless of whose it originally was."

**"We can't get there until [several days later]"**
"[Student] needs to depart within 24 hours. If you can't drive here, you'll need to arrange a flight. We can provide you with airport information."

**"I give them permission to drink at home"**
"I understand that may be your family's choice. Our program policy prohibits it even with parental consent, and this was outlined in the Code of Conduct."

**"This is going to cost us thousands in flights"**
"I understand the financial impact. The enrollment agreement outlined that dismissal results in non-refundable tuition and that families are responsible for transportation costs home."

**"I want to speak to someone above you"**
"You're welcome to request a call with [PD Name]. I want to be clear that the dismissal decision is final, but they can answer questions about our policies."

## Follow-Up Procedures

### Immediate Follow-Up (Same Day/Night):
- Secure all confiscated substances (lock in office)
- Ensure all involved students are safely supervised overnight
- Alert all staff to monitor for additional substance use
- Document everything while details are fresh
- Call PD/HQ with full update

### Within 24 Hours:
- Complete incident report in Salesforce
- Upload all photos and evidence
- Send incident report to parents
- Confirm dismissed student has departed
- If student flew: confirm they passed through security
- Email parent confirming student arrived home safely

### Within 48 Hours:
- Debrief with remaining students (age-appropriate, privacy maintained)
  - "You may have noticed that some students left the program. We can't share details about why, but I want to remind everyone of our substance policy..."
- Check in with roommates of dismissed students
- Reallocate room if needed
- Debrief with staff team

### If Theft Involved:
- Contact store to report theft
- Arrange restitution payment from parents
- File police report if store requires
- Document all legal/financial follow-up
- Parent signs acknowledgment of theft and agrees to restitution

### Ongoing Monitoring:
- Increased vigilance during room checks for remainder of session
- Monitor for copycat behavior
- Check in with students who were bystanders (ensure learning happened)
- Support staff emotionally (substance cases are stressful)

### Case Closure:
- All documentation complete
- Dismissed student safely home
- Parent communication concluded
- Restitution arranged (if theft involved)
- Staff debrief completed
- Program climate stable
- No outstanding safety concerns
