# Curfew/Room Violations - Behavioral Issue

**Tags:** curfew, room violation, out after hours, not in room, lights out, sneaking out, wrong room, opposite gender room, room check, code of conduct violation, behavioral issue, warning, contract

## Recognition Triggers
- Students not in rooms during room check/lights out
- Noise from student rooms after lights out
- Students found in rooms other than their assigned room
- Door not opening fully or quickly during checks
- Students appearing to have just gotten into bed (sleepy act)
- Items knocked over when staff approaches
- Nervous behavior during room checks
- Repeated giggling or noise after warnings
- Students in opposite-gender rooms
- Male students in female dorm areas (or vice versa) after hours
- Students leaving rooms after lights out
- Room doors propped open after curfew

## Severity Assessment

### ZERO TOLERANCE (Immediate Dismissal)
- Being in opposite-gender room for sexual activity
- Leaving campus after curfew
- Sneaking out of dorms to meet others off-campus
- Violating curfew to engage in other prohibited behavior (drugs, alcohol)

### ESCALATING CONSEQUENCE (Warning → Contract → Dismissal)
- First offense: Verbal warning (awake after lights out, noise)
- Second offense: Written contract (repeated curfew violations)
- Third offense or contract violation: Dismissal

### RESTORATIVE (Mediation/Education)
- First-time minor curfew issue with immediate compliance
- Students helping distressed roommate (compassionate violation)

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Immediate Intervention

**During Room Checks - When Violations Discovered:**

1. **Knock and Identify:**
   - "This is [Staff Name]. Room check."
   - Listen for sounds inside (whispers, movement, things being moved)

2. **Request Full Door Opening:**
   - If door opens partially: "Please open the door all the way"
   - If delays or nervousness: stay present until full view of room
   - If door doesn't open: "I need you to open the door now"

3. **Visual Scan:**
   - Are all assigned students in room?
   - Are there extra students (visiting from other rooms)?
   - Are students in bed or up and active?
   - Is there evidence of prohibited behavior?
   - Do students appear to have just jumped into bed?

4. **Initial Response Based on What You Find:**

**Scenario A: Students Still Awake/Making Noise**
- "It's after lights out. You need to be quiet and in bed now."
- Note time and room number
- Return in 5 minutes to ensure compliance
- If still noisy: pull students aside for conversation

**Scenario B: Extra Students in Room**
- "Who is assigned to this room?"
- Identify who shouldn't be there
- "[Student name], you need to return to your own room now"
- Escort student back to correct room
- Note who was where for documentation

**Scenario C: Students Missing**
- "Where is [student name]?"
- Search room, bathroom, common areas
- Alert other staff immediately
- Document time student was last seen

5. **Escalate if Needed:**
   - Second noise violation same night: "This is your second warning. If I have to come back again, there will be consequences"
   - Students in wrong rooms: "This is a violation of our rooming policy. We need to talk about this tomorrow"
   - Missing students: Immediate alert to all staff, contact PD

### Step 2: Investigation

**For Minor Curfew Violations (Noise/Awake After Lights Out):**

**Investigation:**
- Document which room, which students
- Note time of violation(s)
- Record how many warnings given
- Check if this is first offense or pattern
- Review previous incident reports for these students

**No Formal Investigation Needed Unless:**
- This is repeat offense (check previous reports)
- There's suspicion of additional prohibited behavior
- Students were in wrong rooms
- Students were missing from assigned rooms

**For Room Violations (Students in Wrong Rooms):**

**Document:**
1. Which students were where?
2. Why were they in that room?
3. How long had they been there?
4. What were they doing?
5. Any prohibited behavior occurring?

**Interview Each Student Separately:**
- "Why were you in [other student's] room?"
- "How long were you there?"
- "Did you ask staff permission to visit?"
- "What were you doing?"
- "Do you understand the policy about being in assigned rooms only?"

**Check for Pattern:**
- Has this student been found in other rooms before?
- Have these students been violating curfew together repeatedly?
- Is there a romantic relationship component?

**For Missing Students:**

**Immediate Search:**
- Check all common areas, bathrooms, other student rooms
- Alert all staff to search
- Check campus grounds (within boundaries)
- Contact PD/HQ immediately

**When Found:**
- Document where they were
- Who they were with
- What they were doing
- Why they left room after curfew

### Step 3: Discipline Decision

**Decision Matrix:**

**DISMISSAL:**
- Being in opposite-gender room for sexual activity or romantic reasons
- Leaving campus after curfew
- Third curfew violation (after warning and contract)
- Violating written behavioral contract related to curfew
- Curfew violation combined with other prohibited behavior (substances, etc.)

**WRITTEN CONTRACT:**
- Second formal curfew violation after verbal warning
- Being in another student's room after lights out (first offense if no other violations)
- Pattern of noise/awake violations despite multiple warnings

**VERBAL WARNING:**
- First offense of being awake/noisy after lights out
- First time in another student's room (compassionate reason: helping upset friend)
- Minor curfew violation with immediate compliance

**NO DISCIPLINE (Educational Conversation):**
- Student was helping distressed roommate
- Genuine emergency (student felt ill, needed help)
- First-time, minimal violation with full compliance

**Factors to Consider:**
- Is this first offense or pattern?
- Was there other prohibited behavior occurring?
- Did student comply when redirected?
- What was the reason for violation?
- Previous disciplinary history?
- Time - how late after curfew?

### Step 4: Implementation

**For VERBAL WARNING:**

**Next Day Conversation (Two staff present):**

"[Student name], we need to talk about last night. During room checks, you were [still awake and making noise / in another student's room / not in your assigned room]."

"This is against our curfew and rooming policies. After lights out, you need to be in your assigned room, in bed, and quiet."

"This is a verbal warning. If this happens again, you'll be placed on a written behavioral contract, which could lead to dismissal from the program."

"Do you understand the expectations?"

**Call Parent:**
- "I'm calling about a curfew violation last night"
- Explain what happened
- Explain warning given
- Explain next steps if it happens again

**Document:**
- Incident report in Salesforce
- Note warning given and parent call made

**For WRITTEN CONTRACT:**

**Contract Development with PD:**

Contract must include:
- Specific violations that occurred (dates, what happened)
- Clear expectations:
  - In assigned room by curfew
  - In bed by lights out
  - No visiting other rooms without staff permission
  - Quiet after lights out
- Consequences: Any further violation = dismissal
- Daily check-in schedule with CD

**Contract Review Meeting (Two staff, student):**
- Read through contract together
- "This is serious. Any additional violation means you go home"
- Student, CD, witness all sign
- Give student a copy

**Call Parent:**
- More serious tone than warning
- "This is a formal behavioral contract"
- "Any further curfew or rooming violations will result in dismissal"
- "If dismissed, you'll need to arrange and pay for transportation home"

**Upload contract to incident report**

**For DISMISSAL:**

**Consult PD/HQ First** (REQUIRED)

**Follow standard dismissal protocol:**
1. Confirm with PD/HQ
2. Two staff present for conversation
3. Explain violation and consequence
4. Student calls parents
5. CD speaks with parents
6. Arrange transportation home
7. Supervise until departure
8. Complete incident report

### Step 5: Documentation

**For ALL Curfew/Room Violations - Document:**

1. **Incident Details:**
   - Date and time
   - Which room
   - Which students involved
   - What was observed (noise, wrong room, missing, etc.)
   - How many times staff intervened that night
   - Student explanation

2. **Previous Pattern:**
   - Link to previous incident reports if repeat offense
   - Note if this is 1st, 2nd, or 3rd violation

3. **Discipline Action:**
   - Warning/contract/dismissal given
   - Parent call made
   - Student's response to discipline

4. **Follow-Up Plan:**
   - Monitoring plan
   - Check-in schedule (if on contract)

**Timeline for Documentation:**
- Minor violations (noise after curfew): document by next day
- Room violations: incident report within 24 hours
- Dismissals: incident report within 24 hours
- Missing student incidents: immediate documentation even if found quickly

## Conversation Scripts

### Initial Redirection - During Room Check

**First Noise Violation:**
"It's after lights out. You need to be quiet and get into bed now. I'll be back in a few minutes to make sure you're settled."

**Second Noise Violation Same Night:**
"This is the second time I've had to come back here. It's [time] and you need to be asleep. If I have to come back again, we'll be having a serious conversation tomorrow about consequences."

### Student in Wrong Room

**During Discovery:**
"[Student name], you're not assigned to this room. You need to return to your room now. Come with me."

**While Escorting Back:**
"You know the policy is you need to be in your assigned room, especially after lights out. We'll talk about this tomorrow."

### Verbal Warning Conversation - Next Day

**Two Staff Present:**

"[Student], we need to talk about what happened last night. During room checks, I had to come back to your room [number] of times because you were still awake and making noise."

"Our policy is that after lights out at [time], students need to be in bed and quiet. This is for everyone's safety and rest."

"This is a verbal warning. I'm documenting this conversation. If there are more curfew violations, the next step is a written behavioral contract, which puts you at risk of being sent home."

"I'm going to call your parents today to let them know about this warning."

"Do you understand what the expectations are going forward?"

### Written Contract Conversation

**Two Staff Present:**

"[Student], you're being placed on a written behavioral contract today. Let me explain why."

"On [date], you received a verbal warning for being in another student's room after lights out. Last night, you were found in [another student's] room again after curfew."

"Despite the warning and our conversation, this behavior has continued."

[Review contract]

"This contract makes clear: if you violate curfew or the rooming policy again in any way, you will be dismissed from the program immediately. That means you go home, and your parents have to arrange and pay for your transportation."

"Do you understand what's expected of you?"

### Parent Notification - Verbal Warning

"Hi [Parent], this is [CD Name] from Summer Springboard. I'm calling about a curfew violation last night involving [student]."

"During our room checks after lights out, [student] was [still awake and making noise / in another student's room]. Our staff had to redirect [him/her] [number] times to get into bed and be quiet."

"I had a conversation with [student] today, and this is serving as a verbal warning. Our curfew and rooming policies are in place for everyone's safety and rest."

"The expectation going forward is that [student] is in [his/her] assigned room by curfew, in bed by lights out, and quiet for the night."

"If this happens again, [student] will be placed on a written behavioral contract, which could lead to dismissal. I wanted you to be aware so you can reinforce these expectations."

### Parent Notification - Written Contract

"Hi [Parent], this is [CD Name]. I need to talk with you about a serious situation with [student]."

"[Student] is being placed on a written behavioral contract for repeated curfew and rooming violations."

"On [date], [student] received a verbal warning for [violation]. Last night, [student] was found [in another student's room / violating curfew] again."

"The contract makes clear that any further violations will result in immediate dismissal from the program. If that happens, you'll need to arrange and cover the cost of transportation home."

"I want to emphasize how serious this is. This is [student's] final chance. One more violation and [he/she] will be sent home."

"Do you have questions about the contract or expectations?"

## What NOT to Say/Do

### DO NOT:
- Ignore noise violations ("kids will be kids")
- Skip room checks because you're tired
- Open door yourself - always have students open it
- Enter room without second staff member
- Accept "we were just talking" as excuse for being in wrong room after repeated warnings
- Make exceptions for "good students"
- Delay parent notification
- Give "one more chance" beyond contract
- Allow students to visit other rooms after hours "as long as they're quiet"

### NEVER SAY:
- "It's not a big deal"
- "Just keep it down"
- "As long as I don't hear you..."
- "I was young once too"
- "Don't let me catch you again" (implies it's okay if you don't get caught)

### Common Mistakes:
- Not documenting first violations (pattern emerges later with no paper trail)
- Giving too many "unofficial" warnings
- Not following through on stated consequences
- Letting students talk you out of appropriate discipline
- Assuming noise is harmless (may be sign of other violations)

## Parent Notification

### WHEN to Call:

**Verbal Warning:** Same day or next day

**Written Contract:** Same day contract is signed

**Dismissal:** Immediately (follow standard dismissal protocol)

**Minor First-Time Violation:** May not require parent call, use judgment
- If just needed one reminder and students complied immediately
- Document in notes, but may not need formal parent notification
- However, if there's ANY doubt, call

### WHO Calls:
- Campus Director for all formal warnings, contracts, dismissals
- May designate AM for minor first violations

### WHAT to Say:

**Key Points for All Calls:**
- Specific behavior (exact policy violated)
- When it occurred
- How many times it happened
- Discipline action taken
- What happens if it continues

### How to Handle Pushback:

**"They were just having fun"**
"I understand that. However, our curfew policy exists for everyone's safety and rest. When students are up late and noisy, it affects everyone on the floor."

**"Why do they need to be in bed so early?"**
"Our schedule is full and demanding. Students need adequate rest to participate fully. Curfew and lights-out times are designed to ensure everyone gets enough sleep."

**"Can't you just give them one more warning?"**
"They've already received a warning on [date]. The progressive discipline process is: warning, contract, dismissal. We're now at the contract stage because the behavior continued after the warning."

**"Other kids were probably doing it too"**
"We enforce policies when we observe violations. If other students violate curfew, they'll receive the same consequences. I can only speak to what happened with your child."

**"They said they were helping a friend who was upset"**
"I appreciate that [student] cares about their friends. If a friend needs help, the expectation is that they find a staff member who can provide appropriate support. Being in another student's room after hours isn't the appropriate response."

## Follow-Up Procedures

### Immediate Follow-Up:

**Same Night - If Multiple Violations:**
- Debrief with staff team during nightly meeting
- Discuss patterns
- Plan for increased monitoring
- Ensure all staff know which students to watch

**Next Day:**
- Have warning/contract conversation
- Call parents
- Alert staff to monitor these students during next night's checks

### For Students on Contract:

**Daily Check-Ins:**
- Morning: "How did last night go? Any issues?"
- Brief before evening: "Remember the contract expectations tonight"
- Morning after: "How were room checks last night?"

**Staff Briefing:**
- Alert all staff doing room checks to pay special attention to contracted student
- Ask staff to report immediately if ANY violation
- Document each night: compliant or not

### Weekly Monitoring:

**If on Contract:**
- Weekly call/email to parent with update
- "How is [student] doing with curfew expectations?"
- Document progress in case notes

**Pattern Tracking:**
- Monitor program-wide curfew compliance
- If multiple students having issues: review curfew procedures with whole group
- Adjust room check protocols if needed

### End of Contract Period:

**If Successful:**
- "[Student], you've done a great job following curfew expectations. Your contract period is complete. Keep up the good work."
- Email parent with positive update
- Document successful completion

**If Violation During Contract:**
- Immediate dismissal (as per contract terms)
- Follow dismissal protocol
- Call PD/HQ first

### Case Closure:
- Contract completed successfully OR student dismissed
- All documentation in Salesforce
- Parent communication concluded
- Pattern tracking noted for program improvement
