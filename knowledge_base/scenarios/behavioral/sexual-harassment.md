# Sexual Harassment - Behavioral Issue

**Tags:** sexual harassment, unwanted advances, inappropriate touching, sexual comments, uncomfortable, feels unsafe, harassment, assault, code of conduct violation, dismissal, title ix, behavioral issue

## Recognition Triggers
- Student reports unwelcome behavior of sexual nature
- Repeated compliments or attention despite being declined
- Physical touching that appears unwelcome (arm touching, sitting too close)
- Student appears uncomfortable around another student
- Repeatedly asking to "hang out" after being declined
- Comments about appearance ("You look really good today") that seem persistent
- Gifts or attention that feels excessive
- Standing too close, blocking movement, brushing against someone
- Whistling or making suggestive sounds toward a person
- Displaying, watching, or distributing sexually explicit materials
- Comments, jokes, or gestures that target gender or sexuality
- Gender-based harassment or seductive behavior
- Solicitation of sexual activity
- Another student describes situation as "kind of awkward"
- Student trying to avoid someone but "doesn't want to make it a big deal"

## Severity Assessment

### ZERO TOLERANCE (Immediate Dismissal)
- Any form of sexual assault or unwanted physical contact of sexual nature
- Solicitation of sexual activity through threats or promises
- Severe or persistent sexual harassment after warning
- Gender-based harassment that creates unsafe environment
- Distributing sexually explicit materials of other students
- Coercive sexual behavior

### ESCALATING CONSEQUENCE (Warning → Contract → Dismissal)
- First-time inappropriate comments of sexual nature with recognition of harm
- Persistent attention/compliments after initial feedback to stop
- Boundary violations that continue after verbal warning
- Seductive behavior that persists

### IMMEDIATE INTERVENTION (Education + Monitoring)
- Early-stage pursuit behavior (frequent compliments, seeking out)
- Attention that may become problematic but hasn't been explicitly unwelcome yet
- First instance of boundary concern that student recognizes when addressed

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Immediate Intervention

**When Sexual Harassment is Reported or Observed:**

1. **Separate and Support Target Immediately:**
   - Remove target student from situation
   - Private space for conversation
   - "Tell me what's been happening"
   - Validate experience: "Thank you for telling me. That took courage"
   - Assess safety: "Do you feel safe in the program right now?"
   - Assess immediacy: "Is this happening right now or has it been building?"

2. **Assess Target Student's Needs:**
   - Does student need to be separated from alleged harasser immediately?
   - Is student in emotional distress requiring additional support?
   - Does student have safe space to sleep/be if residential?
   - Does student need room change?
   - Is there threat of retaliation?

3. **Secure Safety:**
   - Separate students immediately (different activities, different supervision)
   - No contact between students until investigation complete
   - Alert staff to prevent proximity
   - Consider room change for target if residential

4. **Immediately Alert PD/HQ:**
   - ALL sexual harassment claims must be reported to PD/HQ immediately
   - Do NOT wait to complete investigation
   - Call, don't just email
   - "We have a sexual harassment report. Here's what I know so far..."

**DO NOT:**
- Minimize or dismiss student's report
- Ask "Are you sure that's what they meant?"
- Suggest student is "overreacting"
- Force confrontation between students

### Step 2: Investigation

**CRITICAL: This is Title IX territory. Document everything meticulously.**

**Interview Target Student First:**

1. **Gather Detailed Account:**
   - "Can you describe what happened? Start from the beginning"
   - "When did this start?"
   - "Can you give me specific examples of what [person] said or did?"
   - "How did this make you feel?"
   - "Did you tell them to stop or that you were uncomfortable?"
   - "Has anyone else seen or heard this?"
   - "Have you told anyone else about this?"

2. **Document Exact Details:**
   - Specific words used (exact quotes if possible)
   - Specific actions (where touched, how close stood, etc.)
   - Dates and times
   - Locations
   - Witnesses present
   - Any written evidence (texts, notes, social media)

3. **Ask About Pattern:**
   - "How many times has this happened?"
   - "Has it gotten worse over time?"
   - "Are there others experiencing this from the same person?"

4. **Assess Impact:**
   - "How has this affected your program experience?"
   - "Do you feel safe?"
   - "Are you avoiding certain spaces or activities because of this?"

**Interview Alleged Harasser:**

**DO NOT use accusatory language:**

1. **Open-Ended Start:**
   - "I want to talk with you about your interactions with [target student]"
   - "Tell me about your relationship with [target]"
   - "How would you describe your interactions with them?"

2. **Specific Behavior Questions:**
   - "Have you complimented [target] on their appearance?"
   - "Have you asked them to hang out?"
   - "What did they say when you asked?"
   - "Have you touched them? Describe that"
   - "Did they seem comfortable?"

3. **Listen For:**
   - Do they recognize the behavior occurred?
   - Do they understand it was unwelcome?
   - Do they minimize ("I was just being friendly")?
   - Do they blame target ("They led me on")?
   - Do they show understanding of impact?

4. **Ask About Intent:**
   - "What was your intention with [behavior]?"
   - "How did you think [target] was receiving your attention?"
   - NOT to excuse behavior, but to understand mindset

**Interview Witnesses:**

- "What have you observed between [student A] and [student B]?"
- "Have you seen [alleged harasser] approaching or interacting with [target]?"
- "Did [target] seem comfortable?"
- "What exactly did you see or hear?"
- "Has anyone mentioned this situation to you?"

**Collect Evidence:**
- Screenshots of texts, social media messages, notes
- Written statements from target, alleged harasser, witnesses
- Timeline of incidents
- Photos of any gifts given (if applicable)

**Investigation Timeline:**
- Begin immediately upon report
- Complete within 24-48 hours
- Daily updates to PD/HQ throughout investigation

### Step 3: Discipline Decision

**ALWAYS consult with PD/HQ before making final decision on sexual harassment cases.**

**Decision Factors:**
- Severity of behavior
- Pattern vs. isolated incident
- Whether behavior stopped when addressed
- Impact on target student
- Whether behavior was explicitly unwelcome
- Prior warnings or incidents

**Decision Matrix:**

**IMMEDIATE DISMISSAL:**
- Sexual assault or unwanted physical contact of sexual nature
- Solicitation of sexual activity
- Severe sexual harassment (explicit comments, coercion)
- Pattern of harassment despite warning
- Behavior creating unsafe environment
- Distributing sexual materials involving students

**WRITTEN CONTRACT:**
- Persistent inappropriate comments after verbal warning
- Boundary violations that continue after feedback
- First-time serious incident with recognition of harm
- Creating uncomfortable environment that's addressable

**VERBAL WARNING:**
- First-time boundary issue (excessive compliments, seeking out)
- Behavior stops immediately when addressed
- Student shows genuine understanding of impact
- No pattern of predatory behavior

**IMPORTANT DISTINCTION:**

**Unwelcome vs. Unclear:**
- If target has explicitly said "stop" or shown clear discomfort → stronger consequence
- If target hasn't yet expressed discomfort but is clearly uncomfortable → educational intervention

**Intent vs. Impact:**
- Intent doesn't matter as much as impact
- "I didn't mean it that way" doesn't excuse harm
- Focus on impact on target, not harasser's intentions

### Step 4: Implementation

**For DISMISSAL:**

**BEFORE informing student:**
1. Confirm with PD/HQ (REQUIRED)
2. Notify parent of target student first
3. Arrange for alleged harasser's departure
4. Plan for target student's safety and support

**Dismissal Conversation (Two staff present):**

"[Student], we've completed our investigation into reports of your behavior toward [target student]. Based on what we've learned, including [specific behaviors], we've determined this constitutes sexual harassment."

"Sexual harassment is strictly prohibited and results in immediate dismissal from the program."

"You will need to return home. What needs to happen now is you'll call your parents and explain the situation, and then we'll speak with them about arrangements."

**DO NOT:**
- Reveal details of target's emotional state
- Share what target said word-for-word
- Allow student to contact target to "apologize"

**For WRITTEN CONTRACT:**

**Contract Must Include:**
- Specific behaviors that were problematic
- Clear expectations: No contact with target student
- How to handle shared spaces (who leaves, who stays)
- Consequence of any further violation: immediate dismissal
- Daily check-ins with CD

**Review Process:**
- Student, CD, witness sign contract
- Upload to incident report
- Call parent same day
- Give student copy

**For VERBAL WARNING:**

**Warning Conversation (Two staff present):**

"[Student], I need to talk with you about your interactions with [target student]. I've heard that you've been [specific behaviors - complimenting frequently, asking to hang out, sitting close]."

"[Target] has expressed feeling uncomfortable with this level of attention. Even though you may not have intended to make them uncomfortable, the impact of your behavior has been that they feel [pursued/uncomfortable/awkward]."

"At Summer Springboard, we need everyone to feel safe and comfortable. When someone declines your invitations or shows discomfort, the expectation is that you respect that boundary immediately."

"Going forward, you need to [specific behavior changes]. If this behavior continues, the next step is a written contract which puts you at risk of dismissal."

"I'm going to call your parents to let them know about this conversation. Do you have questions?"

### Step 5: Documentation

**Incident Report REQUIRED within 24 hours:**

**Must Include:**
1. **Report Details:**
   - Who reported (target, witness, staff observation)
   - Date/time of report
   - Date/time of alleged incidents

2. **Target Student Account:**
   - Exact description of behaviors
   - Quotes of what was said (as close to verbatim as possible)
   - Description of physical behaviors
   - Timeline of incidents
   - Impact described by target

3. **Alleged Harasser Account:**
   - Their description of interactions
   - Their explanation of behavior
   - Whether they recognized it was unwelcome
   - Their response to confrontation

4. **Witness Statements:**
   - What witnesses observed
   - Their interpretation of interactions
   - Any relevant context

5. **Evidence:**
   - Screenshots of texts/messages
   - Photos of notes or gifts
   - Written statements

6. **Actions Taken:**
   - How students were separated
   - Discipline imposed
   - Parent notifications
   - Support provided to target

7. **PD/HQ Consultation:**
   - Who you consulted
   - Their guidance
   - Their approval of discipline decision

8. **Follow-Up Plan:**
   - How target student will be supported
   - Monitoring plan for contract (if applicable)
   - Room change details (if applicable)

**Alert Email:**
- Send to alerts@summerspringboard.com immediately
- Triggers case creation in Salesforce
- Subject: "Sexual Harassment Report - [Campus] - [Date]"

**Title IX Considerations:**
- Document that this may fall under Title IX
- Note if this involves gender-based harassment
- HQ will determine if additional reporting required

## Conversation Scripts

### Initial Response to Target Student

**Supportive, Validating Tone:**

"Thank you for coming to me with this. I know this probably wasn't easy to talk about."

"I want you to know that I'm taking this seriously. What you're describing sounds uncomfortable and concerning."

"You're not overreacting. If someone's attention is making you uncomfortable, that matters, and we need to address it."

"I'm going to need to ask you some detailed questions so I can understand exactly what's been happening. Is that okay?"

"After we talk, I want you to know that we'll make sure you're safe and that this behavior stops."

**During Interview:**
"Tell me exactly what [person] said to you. What were their exact words if you can remember?"

"Where were you when this happened? Who else was around?"

"How did this make you feel when it happened?"

"Did you tell them you were uncomfortable or ask them to stop?"

### Interview with Alleged Harasser

**Non-Accusatory But Direct:**

"I want to talk with you about your interactions with [target student]. Tell me about your relationship with them."

"Have you been spending time with them or trying to hang out with them?"

"What kinds of things have you said to them? Have you complimented them?"

"When you've asked to hang out, what have they said?"

"Have you noticed whether they seem comfortable when you're around?"

**If They Minimize:**
"I hear that you felt you were just being friendly. But I need you to understand that [target] experienced it differently. When someone declines your invitations multiple times or seems uncomfortable, that's a signal to step back."

**Educational Moment:**
"Intent and impact aren't always the same thing. You may not have meant to make them uncomfortable, but that was the impact. At Summer Springboard, we need everyone to feel safe and respected."

### Dismissal Conversation

**Two Staff Present:**

"[Student], we need to talk about a serious situation. We've been investigating reports about your behavior toward [target student]."

"Based on our investigation, including speaking with you, [target], and witnesses, we've determined that your behavior constitutes sexual harassment."

"Specifically, you [describe behaviors: repeatedly approached despite being declined, made comments about appearance, touched arm without consent, etc.]"

"[Target student] has made it clear this attention was unwelcome and made them uncomfortable. You continued this behavior despite signs of discomfort."

"Our policy on sexual harassment is clear: any form of sexual harassment may result in immediate dismissal. That's the decision we've made."

"You'll need to return home from the program. I need you to call your parents now and explain what happened."

**If Student Says "I Didn't Mean It That Way":**
"I understand that wasn't your intention. But the impact of your behavior was that [target] felt uncomfortable and unsafe. We have to act on the impact, not just the intention."

**If Student Says "They Never Told Me to Stop":**
"When someone repeatedly declines invitations, seems uncomfortable, or tries to avoid you, those are signals that your attention is unwelcome. You don't need someone to explicitly say 'stop' to recognize they're uncomfortable."

### Parent Notification - Dismissal

**Structure:**

"Hi [Parent], this is [CD Name] from Summer Springboard. I need to speak with you about a serious situation involving [student]."

"We've completed an investigation into reports that [student] engaged in sexual harassment toward another participant."

"Specifically, [student] [describe behaviors without revealing target identity: repeatedly approached another student despite being declined, made persistent comments about appearance despite visible discomfort, made unwelcome physical contact]."

"We take sexual harassment extremely seriously. Our policy is clear that this behavior results in dismissal from the program."

"[Student] needs to return home. We need you to arrange transportation by [timeframe]. Can you drive to pick up [student] or arrange a flight?"

"We'll send you a detailed incident report within 24 hours. [Student] will remain supervised until departure."

**If Parent Says "My Kid Would Never":**
"I understand this is shocking. We've conducted a thorough investigation including speaking with [student], the other student involved, and witnesses. I can walk you through what we documented."

**If Parent Says "Was It Mutual?":**
"The other student has made clear the attention was unwelcome. They expressed discomfort and tried to avoid the situation. This was not a mutual interaction."

**If Parent Says "Kids Flirt":**
"There's a difference between mutual interest and unwelcome attention. When someone repeatedly declines invitations and shows discomfort, continuing to pursue them crosses into harassment."

### Parent Notification - Warning

"Hi [Parent], this is [CD Name]. I need to talk with you about a behavioral concern involving [student]."

"[Student] has been pursuing another student in the program - giving frequent compliments, repeatedly asking to hang out, sitting very close to them during activities."

"The other student has expressed feeling uncomfortable with this level of attention. They've declined invitations to hang out, but [student] has continued seeking them out."

"I had a conversation with [student] today about boundaries and respecting when someone isn't interested in that level of friendship or attention."

"This is serving as a verbal warning. The expectation going forward is that [student] respects the other student's space and stops pursuing them."

"If this behavior continues, [student] would be placed on a written behavioral contract, which could lead to dismissal."

"I wanted you to be aware so you can help reinforce these boundaries with [student]."

## What NOT to Say/Do

### DO NOT:
- Dismiss report as "kids being kids" or "harmless flirting"
- Suggest target is "too sensitive" or "reading into things"
- Force target to confront harasser directly
- Allow "apology" to make situation go away
- Minimize because "nothing physical happened" (verbal harassment still counts)
- Share target's identity with parents of alleged harasser
- Delay reporting to PD/HQ
- Make final dismissal decision without PD/HQ approval
- Allow students to remain in proximity during investigation
- Tell target what alleged harasser said about them

### NEVER SAY:
- "Are you sure they meant it that way?"
- "Maybe they just have a crush on you"
- "You should feel flattered"
- "Boys will be boys"
- "That's just how they show they like you"
- "Did you do anything to lead them on?"
- "Just ignore them"
- "Can't you two just work it out?"

### Legal/Policy Pitfalls:
- Sexual harassment may fall under Title IX (gender-based discrimination)
- Must report to PD/HQ immediately - they determine if further reporting required
- NEVER retaliate against student who reported
- Document everything - these cases can become legal
- Maintain confidentiality - don't share target's name unnecessarily
- Don't make promises to target about outcome before investigation complete

## Parent Notification

### WHEN to Call - Target Student's Parent:
- Within 24 hours of report being made
- After initial safety measures in place
- Before calling parent of alleged harasser (usually)
- Update after discipline decision made

### WHEN to Call - Alleged Harasser's Parent:
- After investigation complete
- After PD/HQ consultation
- Same day as discipline decision when possible
- Before student is told of dismissal (for dismissal cases)

### WHO Calls:
- Campus Director for both calls
- PD may join for complex cases
- Document both calls thoroughly

### WHAT to Say to Target's Parent:

"Hi [Parent], this is [CD Name] from Summer Springboard. I'm calling to let you know that [student] came to me today to report feeling uncomfortable with another student's behavior toward them."

"[Student] has been receiving unwelcome attention from another participant - [brief, appropriate description without too much detail]."

"I want you to know we're taking this very seriously. We've immediately separated the students and are conducting a thorough investigation."

"[Student] is safe. We've [describe support - room change if needed, staff check-ins, etc.]."

"We'll keep you updated as we complete our investigation and determine appropriate consequences for the other student."

"How is [student] doing when you've talked to them? Have they mentioned this to you?"

### How to Handle Pushback:

**From Target's Parent: "I want that kid expelled immediately":**
"I understand your concern for your child's safety. We're conducting a thorough investigation and will take appropriate disciplinary action based on our findings. I can assure you we're treating this with the seriousness it deserves."

**From Alleged Harasser's Parent: "They're just being friendly":**
"When someone repeatedly declines invitations and shows discomfort, continuing to pursue them isn't friendly - it's unwelcome attention. The other student has been clear about their discomfort."

**From Alleged Harasser's Parent: "This will ruin their future":**
"Sexual harassment is a serious issue that we take seriously at all levels of education and in workplaces. Learning about boundaries and respecting them is an important lesson."

**"How do you know my kid did this?":**
"We've conducted a thorough investigation including interviews with both students and witnesses, and we've reviewed [any evidence]. I can walk you through our findings."

**"Why didn't anyone supervise them better?":**
"This behavior occurred during normal program activities where students have some social freedom. Even with supervision, we rely on students to treat each other respectfully. When behavior crosses the line, we investigate and address it."

## Follow-Up Procedures

### Immediate (Day Of):
- Separate students completely
- Provide target student with safe spaces and check-in schedule
- Alert all staff to situation (maintain appropriate confidentiality)
- Begin documentation

### Within 24 Hours:
- Complete incident report
- Update PD/HQ with full details
- Implement discipline decision
- Follow up with target: "How are you feeling? Do you feel safe?"

### If Alleged Harasser Remains in Program (Warning/Contract):

**Daily Check-Ins with Target Student:**
- "Has there been any further contact or behavior?"
- "Do you feel safe in the program?"
- "Are there any situations where you feel uncomfortable?"

**Daily Check-Ins with Student on Contract:**
- "How are you doing with the expectations we set?"
- "Any challenges staying away from [target]?"
- Monitor compliance

**Staff Monitoring:**
- Brief all staff on need to prevent proximity
- Staff intervention if students end up in same space
- Document any violations immediately

### Within One Week:
- Formal check-in with target's parent
- Update on how student is doing in program
- Confirm target feels safe and supported

**If on Contract:**
- Weekly check-in with parent of student on contract
- Progress report
- Reinforce expectations

### End of Contract Period (If Applicable):
- Assess: Did student comply with expectations?
- If yes: "You've shown understanding and respected boundaries. Contract is complete"
- If no: Escalate to dismissal

### Case Closure:
- Target student reports feeling safe
- No further incidents
- All documentation complete in Salesforce
- Parent communications concluded
- Staff debriefed
- Lessons learned documented
