# Behavioral Incident Scenarios - Complete Index

## Overview
This directory contains comprehensive behavioral incident response protocols extracted from the Summer Springboard 2026 Director Training. Each document provides step-by-step protocols, conversation scripts, and implementation guidance for handling specific behavioral violations.

## How to Use These Documents
1. **Identify the incident type** using the recognition triggers in each document
2. **Assess severity** using the severity matrices (zero tolerance vs. escalating vs. restorative)
3. **Follow the 5-step protocol** for each incident:
   - Step 1: Immediate Intervention
   - Step 2: Investigation
   - Step 3: Discipline Decision
   - Step 4: Implementation
   - Step 5: Documentation
4. **Use conversation scripts** for consistency and legal protection
5. **Follow parent notification protocols** for proper communication

## Complete List of Behavioral Scenarios

### 1. Physical Fighting
**File:** `physical-fighting.md`

**When to Use:**
- Students engaged in physical altercation
- Pushing, hitting, striking between students
- Play fighting that escalates
- Use of physical violence

**Severity:** ZERO TOLERANCE (Immediate Dismissal)

**Key Protocols:**
- Immediate separation of students
- Medical assessment for injuries
- Investigation with all parties
- Dismissal process (requires PD/HQ consultation)
- Parent notification same day
- Supervised departure within 24 hours

**Critical Reminders:**
- NEVER tell student they're going home before confirming with PD
- TWO staff must be present for dismissal conversation
- Student calls parent first, then CD speaks with parent
- Staff stays at airport until student passes through security

### 2. Bullying/Harassment
**File:** `bullying-harassment.md`

**When to Use:**
- Microaggressions about race, culture, religion, appearance
- Name-calling, insults, targeted comments
- Social exclusion or "shunning"
- "I'm just kidding" comments that cause harm
- Cyberbullying threats
- Pattern of targeting same student

**Severity:** Ranges from Restorative to Immediate Dismissal

**Key Decision Points:**
- **Dismissal:** Severe bullying, cyberbullying threats, racist/discriminatory harassment
- **Contract:** Repeated microaggressions after warning, pattern of targeting
- **Warning:** First-time microaggression with recognition of harm

**Critical Reminders:**
- Impact matters more than intent
- Document exact quotes
- Protect target student throughout process
- May trigger Title IX if protected characteristics involved
- Monitor for retaliation after discipline

---

### 3. Substance Violations
**File:** `substance-violations.md`

**When to Use:**
- Alcohol, vaping, tobacco, drug possession or use
- Smell of substances in dorms
- Hangover symptoms (pale, sweaty, nauseous)
- Student confession or witness reports
- Discovery during room checks

**Severity:** ZERO TOLERANCE (Immediate Dismissal)

**Critical Policy:**
- **ALL possession or use = immediate dismissal**
- NO warnings for possession/use
- Only progressive discipline for "being present but not using"

**Key Protocols:**
- Room searches require HQ approval
- TWO staff must conduct searches
- Confiscate all substances (never return)
- Separate students before interviews
- Call PD/HQ before dismissal decision
- If theft involved: restitution required

**Special Considerations:**
- "My parents let me drink" = irrelevant
- Bystanders who don't report also violate policy
- Substances brought from home still = dismissal

---

### 4. Sexual Harassment
**File:** `sexual-harassment.md`

**When to Use:**
- Unwelcome attention or compliments
- Repeatedly asking to hang out after being declined
- Unwanted physical touching (arm, sitting too close)
- Comments about appearance that feel persistent
- Student feeling pursued or uncomfortable
- Gender-based harassment
- Displaying/distributing sexual materials

**Severity:** Ranges from Educational to Immediate Dismissal

**Key Decision Points:**
- **Dismissal:** Sexual assault, solicitation, severe harassment, pattern despite warnings
- **Contract:** Persistent inappropriate comments after warning
- **Warning:** First-time boundary issue with recognition

**Critical Reminders:**
- ALL sexual harassment claims reported to PD/HQ immediately
- Title IX territory - document meticulously
- Separate students immediately upon report
- Support target student throughout
- Intent doesn't excuse impact
- "They didn't say stop" isn't a defense

**Investigation Requirements:**
- Interview target first (private, supportive)
- Interview alleged harasser separately
- Interview witnesses
- Collect text/social media evidence
- Document exact quotes and behaviors
- Check for pattern

---

### 5. Curfew/Room Violations
**File:** `curfew-room-violations.md`

**When to Use:**
- Students awake/noisy after lights out
- Students in rooms other than assigned
- Missing from room during checks
- Visiting other rooms without permission
- Repeated noise after warnings

**Severity:** Progressive Discipline (Warning → Contract → Dismissal)

**Key Decision Points:**
- **Dismissal:** Opposite-gender room for sexual activity, leaving campus after curfew, third violation
- **Contract:** Second formal violation after warning
- **Warning:** First offense of noise/being in wrong room

**Room Check Protocol:**
- Knock and identify yourself
- Request full door opening
- Visual scan of room
- Note unusual behavior (nervousness, items knocked over)
- Document who is where
- Second staff member for entries

**Critical Reminders:**
- First violation = warning (with parent call)
- Second violation = contract
- Third violation or contract violation = dismissal
- Compassionate violations (helping upset friend) may be educational only
- Always have two staff for room searches

---

### 6. Technology/Cell Phone Violations
**File:** `technology-violations.md`

**When to Use:**
- Phone use during academic sessions
- Recording without consent
- Posting inappropriate content
- Excessive phone use affecting engagement
- Instructor reports disruption

**Severity:** Ranges from Redirection to Immediate Dismissal

**Key Decision Points:**
- **Dismissal:** Recording in private areas, distributing explicit content, cyberbullying
- **Contract:** Third offense during class, severe disruption
- **Confiscation:** Second offense (for day/activity)
- **Warning:** First offense during class

**Progressive Response:**
1. First time: Verbal reminder to put away
2. Second time: Confiscate for remainder of activity
3. Third time: Written contract
4. Fourth time: Dismissal

**Critical Reminders:**
- Phone searches require student OR parent consent
- Cannot force student to unlock phone
- Recording without consent = potential dismissal
- Store confiscated phones securely
- If serious content suspected: call PD/HQ immediately

## Universal Protocols Across All Incidents

### Disciplinary Process (3-Step System)

**Step 1: Official Warning**
- May be delivered verbally or in writing
- TWO staff must be present
- Clearly explain violation and expectations
- Contact parents same day
- Document the incident

**Step 2: Written Behavioral Contract**
- Used when behavior doesn't improve after warning OR serious misconduct occurs
- Developed with PD (always involved)
- Must clearly describe: violations, expectations, consequences
- Signed by: student, CD, witness
- Uploaded to Incident Report
- Parent called same day

**Step 3: Dismissal from Program**
- PD or HQ ALWAYS involved
- Violation of contract or serious misconduct
- Made in consultation with HQ and PD
- Students must return home at parent expense
- Tuition non-refundable
- Dismissal is final

### Dismissal Process (Standard for All Violations)

**Before Informing Student:**
1. Call PD/HQ to confirm (REQUIRED)
2. Complete investigation
3. Secure evidence
4. Plan supervision until departure

**Informing Student (Two Staff Present):**
1. Private location
2. Clear statement of violation and consequence
3. Explain dismissal policy
4. Student calls parent (staff overhears)
5. CD speaks with parent about logistics

**Logistics:**
6. Parents arrange/pay for transportation
7. Student departs within 24 hours
8. Removed from all activities immediately
9. Supervised at all times until departure
10. If flying: staff stays until through security
11. Call HQ when departed

**After Departure:**
12. Debrief with remaining students
13. Debrief with staff
14. Complete incident report within 24 hours

### Parent Notification Best Practices

**When to Call:**
- Verbal warning: Same day
- Written contract: Same day signed
- Dismissal: Immediately (before telling student for dismissals)

**Who Calls:**
- Campus Director for all formal discipline
- PD may join for complex cases

**Structure of Call:**
1. Identify self and program
2. State the situation (specific behavior)
3. Explain policy violated
4. State consequence given
5. Explain next steps if behavior continues
6. Answer questions
7. Request parental support

**Tone:**
- Factual, not emotional
- Serious but not accusatory
- Collaborative approach
- Clear about consequences

### Documentation Requirements

**Every Incident Requires:**
1. **Incident Report in Salesforce** (within 24 hours)
   - Timeline of events
   - All parties involved
   - Exact quotes when possible
   - Witness statements
   - Evidence (photos, screenshots)
   - Discipline action taken
   - Parent communication log
   - PD/HQ consultation notes

2. **Attachments:**
   - Written witness statements
   - Photos of evidence
   - Screenshots (texts, social media)
   - Signed contracts (if applicable)

3. **Case Creation:**
   - Email to alerts@summerspringboard.com
   - Creates case in Salesforce automatically
   - Reply "handling" to prevent duplicates

### Escalation Protocol

**Always Escalate to PD/HQ for:**
- Any potential dismissal decision
- Sexual harassment or assault reports
- Substance violations
- Missing students
- Serious safety concerns
- Parent threatening legal action
- Media involvement
- Police involvement

**How to Escalate:**
- CALL (don't just email) for urgent situations
- Provide: student names, situation summary, immediate safety concerns
- Ask for: guidance on discipline, parent communication support, next steps
- Document: who you spoke with, their guidance, time of call

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Investigation Errors:
- Rushing to judgment without full investigation
- Not separating students before interviews
- Failing to interview witnesses
- Not documenting exact quotes
- Making assumptions based on "good kid" reputation
- Believing first account without hearing all sides

### Discipline Errors:
- Making dismissal decision without PD/HQ
- Telling student they're going home before confirming with PD
- Giving too many "unofficial warnings" without documentation
- Making exceptions for certain students
- Not following through on stated consequences
- Skipping contract step in escalation

### Communication Errors:
- Delaying parent notification
- Using emotional or accusatory language
- Sharing private details about other students
- Making promises about outcomes before investigation complete
- Not documenting parent conversations

### Documentation Errors:
- Waiting too long to document (memories fade)
- Using vague language ("student was disruptive")
- Not including timeline
- Failing to upload evidence
- Not linking related incidents

## Legal and Policy Reminders

**Title IX Considerations:**
- Sexual harassment based on gender may fall under Title IX
- Discriminatory harassment (race, religion, etc.) has legal implications
- Document everything meticulously
- HQ determines if additional reporting required

**Privacy Rights:**
- Cannot search rooms without HQ approval
- Cannot search phones without student OR parent consent
- Cannot share student discipline details with other parents
- Maintain confidentiality throughout investigations

**Safety Protocols:**
- Never leave students unsupervised during investigations
- Always have two staff present for dismissal conversations
- Separate students immediately if safety concerns
- Medical assessment if injuries or intoxication

**Parent Rights:**
- Parents have right to know about discipline
- Parents arrange and pay for transportation home
- Tuition non-refundable in dismissal cases (per enrollment agreement)
- Parents can request call with PD but dismissal decisions are final

## Emergency Quick Start

**If you only have 30 seconds:**

1. **Ensure immediate safety** (separate students, medical check if needed)
2. **Call PD/HQ** for anything serious (dismissal-level, safety concerns, sexual harassment)
3. **Don't tell student they're going home** until PD confirms
4. **Two staff** for all serious conversations
5. **Document everything** within 24 hours

**If something feels serious, it probably is. When in doubt, escalate.**
