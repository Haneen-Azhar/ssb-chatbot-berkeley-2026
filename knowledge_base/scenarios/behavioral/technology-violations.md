# Technology/Cell Phone Violations - Behavioral Issue

**Tags:** phone, cell phone, technology, recording without consent, social media, inappropriate content, phone use in class, distraction, code of conduct violation, behavioral issue, confiscation, warning

## Recognition Triggers
- Student using phone during academic sessions under desk
- Multiple students using phones during structured activities
- Instructor reports phone use impacting learning environment
- Student checking phone intermittently despite being asked to put it away
- Students recording video/audio without consent
- Posting inappropriate content on social media
- Sharing photos of other students without permission
- Using phone in illicit, illegal, or offensive manner
- Phone use during transitions between activities
- Students constantly on phone during free time to point of isolation

## Severity Assessment

### ZERO TOLERANCE (Immediate Dismissal)
- Recording audio/video without consent (especially in bathrooms, changing areas)
- Distributing sexually explicit materials of students
- Using phone for illegal activities (buying/selling prohibited items)
- Cyberbullying through phone/social media
- Posting harmful content about students or program online

### ESCALATING CONSEQUENCE (Warning → Contract → Confiscation → Dismissal)
- First offense during class: Verbal warning
- Second offense: Phone confiscated for remainder of activity
- Third offense: Written contract + phone restrictions
- Fourth offense or contract violation: Dismissal

### RESTORATIVE (Mediation/Education)
- First-time phone use in class with immediate compliance when redirected
- Forgetting to silence phone (rings during class)
- Checking message from parent during appropriate time but wrong location

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Immediate Intervention

**During Academic Sessions - When Phone Use Observed:**

**Instructor's Response:**
1. **First Instance:**
   - Non-verbal cue (pointed look, gesture to put it away)
   - If that fails: "Please put your phone away"
   - If still using: "I need you to put your phone in your bag now"

2. **Second Instance Same Session:**
   - "Your phone is becoming a distraction. I'm going to need you to bring it to the front and you can pick it up at the end of class"
   - Store phone in secure location (instructor's bag or SSB office)
   - Return at end of session

3. **Alert CD:**
   - After class: "I had to confiscate [student's] phone today because [behavior]"
   - If it's pattern: "This is the third time this week [student] has been on phone during my class"

**Staff Response During Non-Academic Time:**
1. **If Phone Use is Appropriate but Excessive:**
   - "I notice you've been on your phone a lot today. Everything okay?"
   - "Let's put the phone away for a bit and join the group activity"

2. **If Phone Use is Inappropriate:**
   - During tour/professional setting: "Phones need to be put away right now"
   - During meal: "We expect you to be present with your peers during meals"

3. **If Recording Without Consent:**
   - IMMEDIATELY: "Stop recording right now"
   - Confiscate phone
   - Alert CD immediately
   - Potential dismissal-level violation

### Step 2: Investigation

**For Repeated Phone Use in Class:**

**Gather Information:**
1. Interview instructor:
   - How many times has this student used phone in class?
   - Was student distracted from learning?
   - Were other students impacted?
   - Did student comply when asked to put it away?

2. Interview student:
   - "Why were you using your phone during class?"
   - "Do you understand the phone policy during academic time?"
   - "Is there something urgent you're trying to handle?"

3. Review pattern:
   - Check other incident reports
   - Ask other instructors if same student has phone issues
   - Check if this is isolated or pattern

**For Inappropriate Content/Recording:**

**Urgent Investigation:**
1. **Confiscate Phone Immediately**
2. **With Student or Parent Consent - Review Content:**
   - What was recorded?
   - Who was recorded?
   - Was it posted/shared?
   - Who else has seen it?

3. **Interview Student:**
   - "Why were you recording?"
   - "Did you have permission from everyone in the video?"
   - "Have you shared this anywhere?"
   - "Who else knows about this?"

4. **Interview Anyone Who Was Recorded:**
   - "Did you know [student] was recording you?"
   - "Did you give permission?"
   - "How do you feel about being recorded?"

5. **Check Social Media (if applicable):**
   - Has it been posted?
   - How many people have seen it?
   - What's the content?

**IMPORTANT:** Cell phone searches require student OR parent consent. If serious violation suspected and consent refused, call PD/HQ immediately for guidance.

### Step 3: Discipline Decision

**Decision Matrix:**

**IMMEDIATE DISMISSAL:**
- Recording in bathrooms, changing areas, or other private spaces
- Distributing sexual or explicit content of students
- Using phone for illegal activities
- Cyberbullying or harassment via phone/social media
- Recording students without consent and posting online
- Severe or repeated violations after written contract

**WRITTEN CONTRACT:**
- Third offense of phone use during academic sessions
- Phone use that significantly disrupted learning environment
- Inappropriate content posted about program/students (first offense)
- Pattern of excessive phone use despite verbal warnings

**PHONE CONFISCATION (Temporary):**
- Second offense during academic session (confiscate for remainder of activity)
- Excessive use during structured programming (confiscate for day)

**VERBAL WARNING:**
- First offense of phone use during academic session
- Phone rang during class (forgot to silence)
- Briefly checking phone during transition time

**NO DISCIPLINE (Educational Redirection):**
- Checking phone during break time
- Using phone for program purposes (taking notes, photos with permission)
- Quick check of urgent parent message during appropriate time

**Factors to Consider:**
- How many previous warnings?
- Was learning significantly disrupted?
- Did student comply when redirected?
- Was content harmful or just policy violation?
- Student's explanation and intent

### Step 4: Implementation

**For VERBAL WARNING:**

**Conversation with Student (Can be one-on-one or with witness):**

"[Student], I need to talk with you about phone use during class today. Your instructor asked you [number] times to put your phone away."

"Our policy is that phones need to be put away and silenced during academic sessions. This helps everyone focus and get the most out of the learning experience."

"This is a verbal warning. If your instructors have to redirect you about phone use again, your phone will be confiscated for the rest of the day, and you could be placed on a behavioral contract."

"Do you understand the expectations around phone use?"

**Determine if Parent Call Needed:**
- If first minor offense with student showing understanding: may not need parent call
- If pattern emerging or student was resistant: call parent

**Document:**
- Note in student's file
- Alert instructors to watch for pattern

**For PHONE CONFISCATION:**

**During Confiscation:**
- "Because you've continued to use your phone after being asked to put it away, I need to hold onto it for [rest of class / rest of the day]"
- Store in secure location (SSB office)
- Give student receipt/confirmation
- "You can pick it up at [time/end of day]"

**End of Day Conversation:**
- "Here's your phone back. Let's talk about what happened today"
- "This is now your second offense. If this happens again, you'll be placed on a written contract"
- Decide if parent call needed

**For WRITTEN CONTRACT:**

**Develop Contract (with PD consultation):**

Contract includes:
- Specific violations that occurred (dates, classes, instructors)
- Clear expectations:
  - Phone must be silenced during all academic sessions
  - Phone must be put away when asked by instructor/staff
  - Phone use limited to break times and free time
  - No recording others without explicit consent
- Daily check-ins with CD
- Consequence: Next violation = dismissal

**Contract Review (Two staff, student):**
- Review each section
- Student signs, CD signs, witness signs
- "One more violation and you go home"

**Call Parent:**
- Same day as contract
- Explain pattern of violations
- Explain contract terms
- "Next violation results in dismissal"

**For DISMISSAL:**

**Consult PD/HQ FIRST** (REQUIRED for all dismissals)

**Dismissal Conversation (Two staff):**

"[Student], we need to talk about a very serious situation. Our investigation has revealed that you [recorded students without permission / posted inappropriate content / cyberbullied another student / etc.]"

"This is a serious violation of our Code of Conduct and our technology policy. Using your phone to [describe behavior] creates an unsafe environment for other students."

"The decision has been made that you will need to return home from the program."

[Follow standard dismissal protocol - see Physical Fighting doc]

### Step 5: Documentation

**For ALL Phone Violations - Document:**

1. **Incident Details:**
   - Date, time, location
   - What student was doing on phone
   - Who observed it (instructor, staff)
   - How many times student was redirected
   - Student's response

2. **Pattern Tracking:**
   - Is this 1st, 2nd, 3rd offense?
   - Link to previous incident reports
   - Note escalation path

3. **Discipline Action:**
   - Warning/confiscation/contract/dismissal
   - Parent call (if made)
   - Student's response

4. **For Inappropriate Content:**
   - Description of content (screenshot if appropriate and with consent)
   - Who was affected
   - Whether it was shared/posted
   - Steps taken to remove content

**Timeline:**
- Minor violations: document within 24 hours
- Inappropriate content: immediate incident report
- Dismissals: within 24 hours

**For Serious Violations:**
- Photos/screenshots of evidence (if appropriate)
- Written statements from students who were recorded
- Social media screenshots (if posted publicly)
- Consent forms for phone searches

## Conversation Scripts

### Initial Redirection - In Classroom

**Instructor's Progression:**

**1st Time (Non-Verbal):**
[Make eye contact, gesture to put phone away]

**2nd Time (Verbal, Gentle):**
"Please put your phone away so you can focus"

**3rd Time (Firm):**
"I need you to put your phone in your bag now. Phones need to be put away during class time"

**4th Time (Confiscation):**
"I've asked you multiple times to put your phone away. I'm going to need you to bring it up here. You can get it back at the end of class"

### Checking Message from Parent

**Student Says: "I'm just checking a message from my parent"**

**Staff Response:**
"I understand you want to stay in touch with your family. If it's urgent, please step outside the classroom to check it. Otherwise, please wait until break time. If you need to communicate with your parents regularly, we can work out a plan with specific times."

**If Student Says: "My mom wants me to text her throughout the day"**

**Staff Response:**
"I understand your mom wants to hear from you. Let's set up specific times during breaks when you can check in with her. During academic time, you need to be focused on the program."

[Follow up with parent if this becomes pattern]

### Verbal Warning Conversation

"[Student], your instructor told me they had to ask you three times today to put your phone away during class. That's distracting for you and for other students."

"I know phones are a big part of your life, and we're not saying you can't use them. But during academic sessions, you need to be fully present and engaged."

"This is a verbal warning. Our policy is clear: phones need to be silenced and put away during class. If your instructors have to keep redirecting you, your phone will be confiscated."

"Can you commit to keeping your phone put away during academic time going forward?"

### Written Contract Conversation

**Two Staff Present:**

"[Student], you're being placed on a written behavioral contract today for repeated phone use during academic sessions."

"On [date], you received a verbal warning. Yesterday, your phone was confiscated during class. Today, your instructor had to redirect you again."

"Your phone use is impacting your learning and disrupting the classroom environment for others."

[Review contract]

"This contract makes clear: if there's another incident with phone use during academic time, you'll be dismissed from the program."

"You'll check in with me every morning so we can talk about how it's going. Do you understand the expectations?"

### Parent Notification - Pattern of Use

"Hi [Parent], this is [CD Name] from Summer Springboard. I need to talk with you about [student's] phone use during academic sessions."

"Over the past [timeframe], instructors have had to redirect [student] multiple times for using [his/her] phone during class. It's becoming a pattern that's affecting [his/her] learning and focus."

"We know phones are important for communication, but during academic sessions, students need to be fully engaged. That's what makes the program valuable."

"I've spoken with [student] about this, and this is serving as a [warning/contract]. Going forward, [he/she] needs to keep [his/her] phone put away during class time."

"I wanted you to be aware of this pattern. Can you help reinforce these expectations with [student]?"

**If Parent Says "My Kid Needs to Check in with Me":**
"I completely understand wanting to stay in touch. We can arrange specific check-in times during breaks. But during academic sessions, [student] needs to be focused on learning. That's what you're paying for."

### Parent Notification - Recording Without Consent

"Hi [Parent], this is [CD Name]. I need to inform you of a serious situation involving [student]."

"Today we discovered that [student] was recording [video/audio] of other students without their permission. This is a serious violation of privacy and our technology policy."

"The students who were recorded did not consent and were not aware they were being filmed. This behavior makes other students feel unsafe."

"[Student] is being dismissed from the program. We need you to arrange transportation for [him/her] to return home by [timeframe]."

"Using technology in this way violates our Code of Conduct and creates a harmful environment. I'll send you a detailed incident report within 24 hours."

## What NOT to Say/Do

### DO NOT:
- Ignore phone use because "everyone does it"
- Take phone away without storing it securely
- Search phone without student or parent consent
- Make exceptions for "good students"
- Allow constant phone checking "as long as they're quiet"
- Dismiss concerns about recording ("kids record everything these days")
- Delete content from student's phone yourself
- Share phone content with other students

### NEVER SAY:
- "I use my phone all the time too"
- "Phones aren't that distracting"
- "Just be more subtle about it"
- "As long as I don't see it..."
- "Everyone's on their phone anyway"

### Legal/Privacy Issues:
- Recording without consent may violate privacy laws (especially in bathrooms, changing areas)
- Cannot search phone without consent (student OR parent)
- Cannot force student to unlock phone
- Cannot delete content from student's phone
- If serious illegal content suspected: contact PD/HQ immediately, may need to involve law enforcement

## Parent Notification

### WHEN to Call:

**Pattern Emerging:** After second incident in short time period

**Written Contract:** Same day contract is signed

**Dismissal:** Immediately (before telling student)

**Single Minor Incident:** Use judgment - may just need documentation

### WHO Calls:
- Campus Director for contracts and dismissals
- CD or instructor for patterns
- Always CD for serious violations

### WHAT to Say:

**For Pattern of Use:**
- Specific examples (how many times, which classes)
- Impact on learning
- Expectations going forward
- Consequences if it continues
- Request for parental support

**For Inappropriate Content:**
- What was recorded/posted
- Who was affected
- Why it's serious
- Discipline action
- Legal implications if applicable

### How to Handle Pushback:

**"All kids are on their phones"**
"That may be true in general, but during our academic program, we need students fully engaged to get value from the experience. The policy is designed to maximize learning."

**"I need my child to check in with me"**
"We absolutely want [student] to stay in touch with you. Let's schedule specific times during breaks for check-ins. During class time, [he/she] needs to be focused on the program."

**"The instructor is being too strict"**
"Our instructors need students' full attention to teach effectively. When students are on phones, it disrupts the learning environment for everyone. We support our instructors in enforcing this policy."

**"My kid needs their phone for anxiety/mental health"**
"I understand [student] may find comfort in phone use. If there are mental health accommodations needed, let's discuss that with our team and [student's] provider. We can create a plan that addresses [his/her] needs while maintaining classroom expectations."

## Follow-Up Procedures

### For Students on Phone Contract:

**Daily Check-Ins:**
- Morning: "How are you doing with phone use expectations?"
- Check with instructors: "Any phone issues with [student] today?"
- Evening: "How did today go? Any challenges?"

**Weekly:**
- Call/email parent with update
- "Here's how [student] is doing with the phone contract..."
- Document progress

### Monitoring:

**Alert Instructors:**
- Let all instructors know student is on contract for phone use
- Ask them to report immediately if any violations
- Thank them for supporting consistency

**Track Compliance:**
- Document each day: compliant or violated
- If violation: immediate escalation per contract

### End of Contract:

**If Successful:**
- "[Student], you've done great with keeping your phone put away during class. Your contract is complete. Keep it up"
- Positive call to parent
- Document success

**If Violation:**
- Dismiss per contract terms
- Call PD/HQ first
- Follow dismissal protocol

### Case Closure:
- Contract completed or dismissal executed
- All documentation complete
- Parent communication concluded
- Instructors notified of outcome
