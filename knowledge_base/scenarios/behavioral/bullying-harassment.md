# Bullying/Harassment - Behavioral Issue

**Tags:** bullying, harassment, mean, excluded, targeted, intimidation, teasing, picking on, cyber bullying, social exclusion, code of conduct violation, behavioral issue, feels unsafe

## Recognition Triggers
- Student reports feeling targeted, excluded, or intimidated
- Staff observes repeated negative interactions toward same student
- Microaggressions about accent, appearance, culture, religion, ethnicity
- Name-calling, insults, or derogatory comments
- Social exclusion or "shunning" from friend groups
- Comments followed by "I'm just kidding" or "It's just a joke"
- Cyberbullying threats (social media posts, group chat exclusion)
- Student becoming withdrawn or isolated after previously being engaged
- Other students reporting concerns about how someone is being treated
- Repeated "jokes" that target protected characteristics

## Severity Assessment

### ZERO TOLERANCE (Immediate Dismissal)
- Severe bullying involving threats or intimidation
- Cyberbullying threats (threatening to post damaging/pornographic photos)
- Discriminatory harassment based on race, gender, sexual orientation, religion, disability
- Gang-up bullying (multiple students targeting one)
- Sustained pattern of harassment despite warnings
- Bullying that creates unsafe environment for targeted student

### ESCALATING CONSEQUENCE (Warning → Contract → Dismissal)
- Repeated microaggressions after initial warning
- Social exclusion that continues after intervention
- Name-calling or insults (first offense gets warning)
- "Joking" that crosses line repeatedly
- Creating hostile environment through comments

### RESTORATIVE (Mediation/Education)
- First-time microaggression with immediate recognition of harm
- Unintentional offense with genuine apology and behavior change
- Minor social conflict that can be mediated

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Immediate Intervention
**When Harassment is Observed or Reported:**

1. **Separate and Support:**
   - Separate student who is being targeted from situation
   - Provide private space to talk
   - Listen without judgment: "Tell me what's been happening"
   - Validate feelings: "Thank you for telling me. That must have been difficult"
   - Assure safety: "We're going to address this and make sure you're safe"

2. **Assess Immediate Safety:**
   - Is student in emotional distress requiring immediate support?
   - Is there ongoing threat or intimidation?
   - Does student feel safe returning to group activities?
   - Are there other students who need to be separated?

3. **Stop the Behavior:**
   - If witnessed in real-time, intervene immediately
   - Pull offending student(s) aside separately
   - Do NOT publicly shame or call out in front of group
   - "I need to talk with you about something that just happened"

### Step 2: Investigation
**Gather Complete Picture Before Determining Discipline:**

1. **Interview Target Student:**
   - "Can you walk me through what's been happening?"
   - "When did this start?"
   - "Is this the first time or has it happened before?"
   - "Who else has seen or heard this?"
   - "How has this been affecting you?"
   - "Do you feel safe in the program right now?"
   - Document exact quotes of what was said/done
   - Ask about witnesses

2. **Interview Alleged Harasser(s):**
   - Conduct separately if multiple students involved
   - "I want to hear your perspective on what happened with [situation]"
   - "Tell me about your interactions with [target student]"
   - "What did you say/do? What was your intention?"
   - Listen for: Do they recognize harm? Show remorse? Minimize behavior?
   - Document their exact words

3. **Interview Witnesses:**
   - "What did you see/hear?"
   - "How long has this been going on?"
   - "Have you seen other incidents between these students?"
   - Get at least 2-3 witness accounts if possible

4. **Review Digital Evidence (if applicable):**
   - Request screenshots of group chats, social media
   - With student consent: review messages showing pattern
   - Document cyberbullying evidence
   - Check timestamps to establish pattern

5. **Check for Pattern:**
   - Has this student been warned before?
   - Are there other students reporting similar treatment?
   - Is there a documented history in incident reports?

**Investigation Timeline:**
- Initial interviews: within 2 hours of report
- Complete investigation: same day
- Decision on discipline: within 24 hours

### Step 3: Discipline Decision
**Campus Director determines consequence - must consult with PD for dismissals:**

**Decision Matrix:**

**IMMEDIATE DISMISSAL:**
- Threatening to post damaging photos online
- Racist, sexist, homophobic slurs
- Pattern of harassment despite previous warnings
- Severe cyberbullying
- Creating genuinely unsafe environment

**WRITTEN CONTRACT:**
- First offense of name-calling or targeted comments
- Microaggressions after verbal warning
- Social exclusion that persists
- "Joking" that crosses line after being told to stop

**VERBAL WARNING:**
- First-time microaggression with recognition of harm
- Insensitive comment without malicious intent
- Minor social conflict
- When student shows genuine remorse and understanding

**Factors to Consider:**
- Intent vs. Impact (impact matters more)
- Pattern vs. isolated incident
- Severity of harm to targeted student
- Protected characteristic involved (race, gender, religion, etc.)
- Previous disciplinary history
- Genuine remorse and understanding shown

### Step 4: Implementation

**For VERBAL WARNING:**

1. **Conversation with Offending Student** (Two staff present):
   - "We need to talk about what happened with [target student]"
   - Clearly explain what behavior violated expectations
   - Explain impact: "When you made that comment about [their accent], it made them feel excluded and targeted"
   - Set clear expectation: "This behavior is not acceptable at Summer Springboard"
   - Explain consequence: "If this happens again, you'll be placed on a written contract which could lead to dismissal"
   - Document who was present, what was said, student's response

2. **Call Parent/Guardian:**
   - "I'm calling to let you know about a behavior concern with [student]"
   - Explain what happened (factual, specific)
   - Explain warning given
   - Explain expectations going forward
   - "If behavior continues, next step is written contract"
   - Document call

3. **Document:**
   - Incident report in Salesforce within 24 hours
   - Include witness statements
   - Note warning given and parent call

**For WRITTEN CONTRACT:**

1. **Develop Contract with PD:**
   - Clearly describe behaviors that violated expectations
   - List specific expectations moving forward
   - State consequences for additional violations (dismissal)
   - Include check-in plan (daily meetings with CD)

2. **Contract Review Meeting** (Two staff, student):
   - Review contract line by line
   - Student signs, CD signs, witness signs
   - Give student copy

3. **Call Parent/Guardian:**
   - More serious tone than warning
   - "This is a formal behavioral contract. Any additional violations will result in dismissal"
   - Remind: "Parents are responsible for arranging and paying for transportation home if dismissal occurs"

4. **Upload Contract to Incident Report**

**For DISMISSAL:**

1. **Consult PD/HQ BEFORE telling student** (REQUIRED)

2. **Follow dismissal protocol** (see Physical Fighting doc for detailed steps)

3. **Special Considerations for Harassment Dismissals:**
   - Protect targeted student throughout process
   - Consider whether targeted student wants to know outcome
   - Monitor for retaliation from peers of dismissed student
   - Debrief carefully with group (maintain privacy)

### Step 5: Documentation
**Required Within 24 Hours:**

1. **Incident Report in Salesforce:**
   - Detailed description of harassment/bullying behavior
   - Exact quotes when possible
   - Timeline of events
   - All parties involved
   - Witness statements
   - Screenshots of messages/posts (if applicable)
   - Discipline action taken
   - Parent communication log

2. **Attachments to IR:**
   - Written witness statements
   - Screenshots of cyberbullying evidence
   - Written contract (if applicable)
   - Photos if vandalism or physical evidence involved

3. **Pattern Tracking:**
   - Note in Salesforce if this is repeat offense
   - Connect to previous incident reports for same student
   - Track for program-wide patterns

## Conversation Scripts

### Initial Response to Target Student
**Empathetic, Validating:**

"Thank you for telling me about this. I know it takes courage to speak up when you're being treated this way."

"What you're describing is not okay, and it's not your fault. No one should be made to feel excluded or targeted because of [who they are]."

"I want to hear everything that's been happening so we can address it. Take your time and tell me what you've experienced."

"Your safety and comfort in this program matters to me. We're going to take this seriously."

### Intervention with Student Who Committed Microaggression
**Educational, Firm:**

"I heard you make a comment about [student's accent] during lunch. Even though you said you were joking, that kind of comment can make someone feel singled out and uncomfortable."

"Impact matters more than intent. Even if you didn't mean harm, [student] felt targeted by that comment."

"At Summer Springboard, we don't make jokes about someone's accent, appearance, culture, or background. Those comments can be hurtful even when you think it's just fun."

"I need you to understand that this behavior isn't acceptable here. If it happens again, there will be more serious consequences. Do you understand?"

**If student says "They didn't say they were upset":**
"People often don't speak up when they're hurt, especially if everyone around them is laughing. That doesn't mean it's okay. We're responsible for treating everyone with respect regardless of whether they tell us we've hurt them."

**If student says "Everyone jokes like that":**
"Not here. That might be how your friend group communicates at home, but here we expect everyone to feel safe and respected."

### Verbal Warning Conversation
**Two Staff Present:**

"[Student], we need to talk about what's been happening between you and [target student]. Over the past [timeframe], there have been multiple reports of you making comments about [their accent/appearance/etc.]"

"This is a verbal warning. The behavior I'm talking about is [specific examples with quotes]. This violates our Code of Conduct section on respect and harassment."

"Here's what needs to change: [specific behaviors]. You need to stop making comments about [target student's characteristics]. You need to treat [them] with the same respect you'd want for yourself."

"If this behavior continues, the next step is a written behavioral contract, which means you're at risk of being dismissed from the program. Do you understand?"

"I'm going to call your parents today to let them know about this warning. Is there anything you want me to tell them?"

### Written Contract Conversation
**Two Staff Present:**

"[Student], you're being placed on a written behavioral contract. This is a serious step. Let me explain what this means."

"You received a verbal warning on [date] for [behavior]. Despite that warning, [new incident] happened yesterday."

"This contract outlines the exact behaviors that are not acceptable and what we expect from you going forward. If you violate this contract or the Code of Conduct again in any way, you will be dismissed from the program immediately."

[Review contract line by line]

"Your parents know this is happening. They understand that any additional violations mean you go home, and they'll need to arrange and pay for your transportation."

"Do you have any questions about what's expected of you?"

### Dismissal Conversation for Severe Bullying
**Two Staff Present:**

"[Student], we need to talk about a very serious situation. We've completed an investigation into reports that you [specific behavior - threatened to post photos of another student/made racist comments/etc.]"

"This behavior is a serious violation of our Code of Conduct. We've spoken with [witnesses/target student], and based on our investigation, we've made the decision that you will need to return home from the program."

"Bullying and harassment create an unsafe environment for other students, and we can't allow you to remain in the program."

"What needs to happen now is you'll call your parents and let them know what occurred and that you need to go home. I'll be here during that call, and then I'll speak with them about arrangements."

**If student blames target:**
"I've heard your perspective. Regardless of any conflict between you, threatening [behavior]/making [comments] violates our policy. The decision is final."

### Parent Notification - Verbal Warning

"Hi [parent], this is [CD name] from Summer Springboard. I'm calling about a behavior concern with [student]."

"Today [student] made comments to another student about [their accent/appearance]. This isn't the first time, and the other student has been feeling targeted."

"I had a conversation with [student] about this, and this is serving as a verbal warning. We've explained that comments like these make other students feel excluded and uncomfortable, which violates our Code of Conduct on respect."

"The expectation going forward is that [student] treats all students with respect and doesn't make comments about people's backgrounds, appearance, or characteristics."

"If this behavior continues, the next step would be a written behavioral contract, which puts [student] at risk of dismissal. I wanted you to be aware so you can reinforce these expectations."

"Do you have any questions about what happened or what we expect?"

### Parent Notification - Written Contract

"Hi [parent], this is [CD name]. I need to talk with you about a serious behavioral situation with [student]."

"[Student] is being placed on a written behavioral contract today. This happened because [describe pattern of behavior and escalation]."

"[He/She] received a verbal warning on [date], and we had a conversation about expectations. Unfortunately, the behavior continued."

"The contract outlines specific expectations and makes clear that any further violations of the Code of Conduct will result in immediate dismissal from the program."

"I want to be clear about what this means: if there's another incident, [student] will be sent home from the program, and you'll need to arrange and cover the cost of transportation. The tuition is non-refundable in dismissal cases."

"We're giving [student] another chance because we believe [he/she] can turn this around. But this is the final chance."

"Do you have questions about the contract or the expectations?"

### Parent Notification - Dismissal for Bullying

"Hi [parent], this is [CD name] from Summer Springboard. I need to inform you that [student] will need to return home from the program."

"We've completed an investigation into reports that [student] [specific behavior - was cyberbullying another student/made threatening comments/engaged in a pattern of harassment]."

"This is a serious violation of our Code of Conduct. [Briefly describe investigation process - spoke with target student, witnesses, and your child]."

"Our policy on bullying and harassment is clear - these behaviors create an unsafe environment and result in dismissal from the program."

"What we need from you now is to arrange transportation for [student] to return home by [timeframe]. Can you drive to pick up [him/her], or do you need to arrange a flight?"

"[Student] will remain supervised and safe until departure. We'll send you a detailed incident report within 24 hours."

"I know this is very disappointing. Do you have questions about what happened?"

**If parent defends behavior:**
"I understand you may see this differently. Our investigation was thorough, and the behavior we documented violated our Code of Conduct that was agreed to at enrollment. The decision is final."

## What NOT to Say/Do

### DO NOT:
- Minimize microaggressions ("It was just a joke")
- Tell target student to "ignore it" or "toughen up"
- Confront alleged harasser in front of group
- Force apology or mediation before investigation
- Dismiss behavior because "kids are just like that"
- Share details of target's distress with alleged harasser
- Allow "restorative conversation" for severe cases
- Rush to judgment without full investigation
- Fail to follow up on reports ("Let me know if it happens again")
- Make promises to target you can't keep
- Reveal target's identity to other parents

### NEVER SAY:
- "Boys will be boys"
- "That's just how teenagers talk"
- "They probably didn't mean it that way"
- "You're being too sensitive"
- "Can't you two just work it out?"
- "I'm sure they were just joking"
- "Maybe you misunderstood"
- "Are you sure that's what they meant?"

### Legal/Policy Pitfalls:
- Bullying based on protected characteristics may trigger Title IX obligations
- Document EVERYTHING - harassment cases can become legal issues
- Never retaliate against student who reported
- Maintain confidentiality - don't share target's personal info
- Don't make decisions based on "who started it" - focus on behavior observed
- Consult PD on any harassment involving protected characteristics

## Parent Notification

### WHEN to Call:
- SAME DAY as verbal warning is given
- Within 2 hours of written contract being signed
- IMMEDIATELY for dismissal (before student leaves)
- After investigation is complete, not during

### WHO Calls:
- Campus Director for all discipline calls
- PD may join for escalated cases
- Two staff present for dismissal calls

### WHAT to Say - Key Points:

**For ALL Calls:**
- Specific behavior (with examples, not vague "being mean")
- When it occurred
- Impact on other student
- Discipline action taken
- Expectations going forward
- What happens if behavior continues

**Tone:**
- Serious but not accusatory
- Factual, not emotional
- Collaborative ("We need your help reinforcing this")
- Clear about consequences

### How to Handle Pushback:

**"My child would never do that"**
"I understand this may not match what you see at home. We've conducted a thorough investigation with multiple witnesses. I can walk you through what we documented."

**"Kids today are too sensitive"**
"Our program is designed to be inclusive and respectful for all students. Comments about someone's background or characteristics aren't acceptable here, regardless of intent."

**"What did the other kid do to provoke this?"**
"We've investigated the full situation. Regardless of any conflict, making [comments/threats] violates our Code of Conduct. We hold all students to the same standard."

**"This is going to ruin my child's record"**
"Our goal is educational. This is a learning opportunity about respect and impact. If [student] corrects the behavior, there won't be further consequences."

**"I don't want you to tell my child someone reported them"**
"We protect the identity of students who come forward. We'll address the behavior we observed without naming who reported it."

**"Can my child apologize and make this go away?"**
"An apology is a good first step, but doesn't erase the consequence. The discipline stands. If [student] shows changed behavior going forward, that's what matters most."

## Follow-Up Procedures

### Immediate Follow-Up (Day Of):
- Check in with target student: "How are you doing? Do you feel safe?"
- Monitor interactions between students involved
- Brief staff team on situation (maintain privacy)
- Alert staff to watch for retaliation

### Daily Check-Ins (If on Contract):
- Meet with student on contract daily
- "How are things going? Any challenges following the expectations?"
- Document each check-in
- Provide positive reinforcement for good behavior

### Within 48 Hours:
- Follow up with target student: "Have there been any more incidents?"
- Observe interactions during group activities
- Check in with roommates/close friends
- Email parents with update

### Weekly (For Contracts):
- Formal check-in with student on contract
- Progress report to parents
- Assess: Can contract end early with good behavior? Or escalate?
- Document all check-ins in Salesforce

### Monitoring for Retaliation:
- Watch for peer retaliation against target student
- Monitor social dynamics (exclusion by friends of disciplined student)
- Check in regularly: "Is anyone giving you a hard time about reporting?"
- Address retaliation immediately as new violation

### End of Contract Period:
- If behavior improved: "You've shown great growth. Contract is complete."
- If behavior didn't improve: escalate to dismissal
- Document outcome in incident report

### Case Closure:
- Target student reports feeling safe
- No additional incidents
- All documentation complete
- Parent communication concluded
- Staff debrief completed
