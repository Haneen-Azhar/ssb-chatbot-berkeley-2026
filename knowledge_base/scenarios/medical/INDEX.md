## Quick Reference: When to Call 911

### ALWAYS Call 911 for:
- **Anaphylaxis** - Difficulty breathing, swelling of throat/lips, multiple body systems
- **Seizure** - Lasting >5 minutes, first-time seizure, not recovering, injury during seizure
- **Heat Stroke** - Altered mental status, temp ≥103°F, hot dry skin, confusion
- **Severe Bleeding** - Won't stop with pressure, spurting blood, deep wounds
- **Head Injury** - Loss of consciousness >30 seconds, vomiting, confusion, unequal pupils
- **Broken Bones** - Open fracture (bone through skin), severe deformity, limb cold/blue/numb
- **Breathing Problems** - Severe difficulty breathing, blue lips, cannot speak
- **Unconscious** - Won't wake up, unresponsive
- **Chest Pain** - Severe or with other symptoms
- **Severe Trauma** - High-impact injury, multiple injuries, suspected spine injury

### Call SSB Helpline 24/7: (888) 682-6671

For all medical situations requiring guidance, urgent care coordination, or parent notification support.

## Playbook Structure

Each playbook contains:

### 1. Recognition Triggers
- Specific signs and symptoms
- What to look for
- Common presentations

### 2. Severity Assessment
- **IMMEDIATE EMERGENCY (Call 9-1-1)** - Life-threatening criteria
- **URGENT (Call SSB Helpline + Urgent Care)** - Same-day medical evaluation needed
- **NON-URGENT (First Aid + Monitor)** - Self-care with observation

### 3. Step-by-Step Protocol
- **Step 1:** Immediate Response (First 60 seconds)
- **Step 2:** Medical Intervention (specific procedures)
- **Step 3:** Emergency Services (who to call, what to say)
- **Step 4:** Documentation (what to record, incident reports)
- **Step 5:** Follow-Up (0-2 hours, 2-24 hours, 24+ hours)

### 4. What to Say (Exact Scripts)
- To student during emergency
- To 911 operator
- To parents (emergency and non-emergency)
- To SSB Helpline
- To student after incident

### 5. What NOT to Do
- Common mistakes to avoid
- Dangerous practices
- Myths debunked

### 6. Medical Information Needed
- What to check in student medical forms
- Plan of Action (POA) requirements
- Required accommodations
- Health Check protocols

### 7. Who to Call and When
- Emergency contacts with timing
- Contact hierarchy
- Phone numbers and escalation chain

### 8. After-Action Procedures
- Immediate care (0-2 hours)
- Stabilization (2-24 hours)
- Recovery and prevention (24+ hours)

### 9. Key Policy Reminders
- SSB-specific policies
- Documentation requirements
- Staff responsibilities

### 10. Medical References
- Statistics (2026 enrollment data)

- Additional context

## Important SSB Policies

### Plans of Action (POA)
- Students with chronic conditions have POAs on file
- Types: Physical Health POA, Mental Health POA, Allergy Waiver + POA
- Review before program in Student Master List
- Health Check on check-in day required
- POAs located in Summer Staff Portal Student POAs Folder

### Medical Statistics (2026 Enrollment)
- **159 students require EpiPen** (Allergy Waiver + POA on file)
- **7 students with diabetes** (Physical Health POA required)
- **Students with asthma** (Asthma Waiver on file)
- **Students with seizure disorders** (Physical Health POA - Seizure)
- **22 students with mental health history** (Wellness Assessment & POA)

### Emergency Contact Chain
1. **911** - Life-threatening emergencies
2. **SSB Helpline (24/7)** - (888) 682-6671
3. **Campus Director** - Immediate notification via batphone
4. **Program Director** - Escalation and support
5. **Student's Emergency Contact** - Parent/guardian notification
6. **Conference Representative** - For serious campus incidents (CD notifies)
7. **Campus Police/Security** - As needed
8. **Medical Director** - Via SSB HQ for complex questions

### Staff Training Requirements
- **CPR/First Aid Certification** - Required for all campus staff
- **Online Epinephrine Training** - Required before program
- **Mandated Reporter Certification** - CD/ACD and Mentors
- **POA Review** - All staff must read POAs for students on their team
- **Staff Manual Protocols** - Anaphylactic Policy, Diabetic Protocol (required reading)

### First Aid Resources
- **Who carries first aid kits:** Mentors, Leadership, EVERYONE
- **Large kit:** Program office
- **Contents:** Bandages, antibiotic ointment, pain relievers, antihistamines, cold packs, gloves
- **Inventory:** Check and restock regularly

### Incident Reporting (IR)
**Required for:**
- All medical emergencies requiring 911
- Urgent care or ER visits
- Significant medical issues (anaphylaxis, seizures, heat exhaustion, fractures, concussions)
- Any medical issue affecting program participation
- Parent complaints or concerns
- Obvious crisis situations

**Who files:** CD or designated staff
**When:** Same day
**Submit to:** SSB HQ via incident reporting system

### Medication Policies
- **Student Self-Administration:** Students manage their own medications
- **Staff Role:** Support, monitoring, emergency response only
- **EpiPen:** Student carries one, mentor carries backup
- **Rescue Medications:** Only if in POA and staff trained
- **OTC Medications:** Parent authorization required, check allergies, document
- **Never:** Give staff's personal medication to student

### Documentation Requirements
- Timeline of all events
- Symptoms observed
- Interventions provided
- Student response
- Communications (911, parents, SSB)
- Photos of scene/hazards (NOT of student's medical condition)
- Witness statements if applicable
- Follow-up plans

### Return to Activities
- **After 911 call:** Medical clearance required
- **Concussion:** Graduated return-to-play protocol, doctor clearance
- **Heat stroke:** Medical clearance, may need 1-2 weeks
- **Fracture:** Per doctor restrictions, accommodations needed
- **Illness:** Fever-free 24 hours without medication, contagious period passed

## Using These Playbooks

### For Staff Training:
- Review all playbooks before program starts
- Familiarize with recognition triggers
- Practice emergency scripts
- Know when to call 911 vs SSB Helpline
- Understand escalation protocols

### During Emergencies:
- Reference quickly for step-by-step guidance
- Use exact scripts provided
- Follow protocols precisely
- Document as you go
- Don't hesitate to escalate

### For Student-Specific Preparation:
- Read student's POA
- Review relevant playbook for their condition
- Conduct Health Check on check-in day
- Ensure supplies accessible (EpiPen, inhaler, glucose, etc.)
- Plan for specific scenarios

### After Incidents:
- Use debrief questions
- Complete after-action procedures
- Implement prevention measures
- Update POAs if needed
- Recognize staff who responded well

## Medical Emergency Philosophy

**SSB Duty of Care:**
- Student safety is paramount
- When in doubt, escalate
- Better to over-respond than under-respond
- Parents trust us with their children - honor that trust
- Document everything
- Learn from incidents to prevent future ones

**Staff Empowerment:**
- You are trained and capable
- Trust your instincts
- Ask for help when needed
- SSB Helpline available 24/7
- You're never alone in handling emergencies

**Student Support:**
- Medical conditions don't define students
- Enable full participation when safe
- Provide accommodations, not exclusion
- Respect privacy and dignity
- Empower students to self-advocate
