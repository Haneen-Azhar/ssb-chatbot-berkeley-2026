# Homesickness - Mental Health Concern

**Tags:** homesickness, homesick, misses home, wants to go home, missing family, missing parents, lonely, sad, crying, doesnt want to be here, wants to leave, not adjusting, isolated, withdrawal, depressed, cant sleep, missing friends

**⚠️ NOTE:** This protocol is inferred from director_training_2026.txt references to "being away from home" and "new environment stress" (lines 5519-5520). Full SSB protocols may exist in Staff Manual or POAs.

## Recognition Triggers
- Student frequently mentions wanting to go home
- Student calls parents multiple times per day
- Student cries when talking about family
- Student compares everything to "how we do it at home"
- Student withdraws from group activities
- Student doesn't engage with peers
- Student refuses to participate in program
- Student says "I don't belong here"
- Student skips meals or can't sleep
- Student asks when parents are picking them up (when it's not checkout day)

## Severity Assessment

### URGENT (Immediate CD Involvement)
- Student refuses to eat for 24+ hours
- Student self-isolates completely (won't leave room)
- Student has panic attacks triggered by homesickness
- Student expresses self-harm or suicidal thoughts
- Student's homesickness impacts roommate/other students significantly
- Parent demands student come home immediately
- Student attempts to leave campus on their own

### CONCERNING (Active Support + Monitor)
- Student cries daily but participates in activities
- Student makes excessive parent calls (4+ times/day)
- Student withdraws from social situations
- Student's academic engagement suffering
- Homesickness worsening instead of improving after Day 3
- Student mentions feeling "alone" or "different from everyone"

### NORMAL (Reassurance + Light Support)
- Mild homesickness Days 1-3 (very common)
- Student misses family but engages with program
- Student calls parents once daily
- Temporary sadness that improves with distraction
- Student responds well to mentor support
- Improving each day

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Normalize & Validate (First Conversation)

**Timing:** As soon as you notice signs

1. **Private conversation:**
   - "Hey, can we talk for a minute? I noticed you seem a bit down."
   - Pull student aside from group, not in closed room

2. **Normalize the feeling:**
   - "Homesickness is really common. About half our students feel this way in the first few days."
   - "It's totally normal to miss your family and your routine."
   - "Being in a new place with new people is hard."

3. **Validate their emotions:**
   - "It makes sense that you're feeling this way."
   - "Missing home doesn't mean you're weak or that you can't do this."
   - "This is a big change, and your feelings are valid."

4. **Assess severity:**
   - "How long have you been feeling this way?"
   - "Is it getting better, worse, or staying the same?"
   - "Are you able to enjoy parts of the program, or does it feel bad all the time?"

### Step 2: Engagement Strategies (Days 1-5)

**Goal:** Help student connect to program and peers

**Buddy system:**
- Pair student with friendly, inclusive peer
- "I'm going to introduce you to [student name]. They're really welcoming and I think you'd get along."
- Don't force friendship, just create opportunity

**Incremental participation:**
- "I know you don't feel like joining the group activity. Would you be willing to just sit with us for 10 minutes?"
- Start small, build confidence
- Praise every small step: "I noticed you sat with your mentor group today. That took courage."

**Responsibility/purpose:**
- Give student a small job (help with setup, take photos, be team timekeeper)
- Purpose reduces rumination
- "I could really use your help with [task]. Would you be willing?"

**Scheduled parent calls:**
- Work with student to set specific call times (not all day)
- "Let's plan for you to call home at 7pm each night. That way you have something to look forward to."
- Too many calls can make homesickness worse
- Coordinate with CD if parent calls are excessive

### Step 3: When to Escalate

**Notify CD immediately if:**
- Homesickness persists beyond Day 5 without improvement
- Student refuses meals or activities
- Student's distress level is high (crying for hours, can't be consoled)
- Parents are pressuring student to come home
- Student's homesickness is preventing them from sleeping
- Other students are being negatively impacted

**CD will:**
- Call parents to discuss situation
- Assess if student should continue program
- Determine if additional support needed
- Potentially arrange early departure if necessary

### Step 4: Parent Coordination

**CD handles parent calls, not mentors.**

**When CD calls parents, they may discuss:**
- Student's current state and coping
- Encouraging parents to support student staying (if appropriate)
- Setting boundaries on parent-student contact
- Plan for improvement or decision to withdraw

**Parent dynamics:**

**Helpful parent response:**
- "I miss you too, but I'm so proud of you for trying something new."
- "Let's give it three more days and see how you feel."
- "Tell me one good thing that happened today."

**Unhelpful parent response:**
- "If you're miserable, I'll come get you right now."
- "I knew this was a bad idea."
- "You don't have to stay if you don't want to."
- (CD should coach parents if they're enabling early departure)

### Step 5: Decision Point - Stay or Go

**Student should stay if:**
- Homesickness is mild to moderate
- Student participates in activities (even reluctantly)
- Student is eating and sleeping (even if reduced)
- Improvement visible day over day
- Student says "I want to try to stay"
- Parents supportive of student staying

**Student should go home if:**
- Severe distress not improving after 5-7 days
- Complete refusal to participate
- Self-harm risk or mental health crisis
- Student adamantly wants to leave
- Parents insist on early pickup
- Homesickness is causing medical issues (can't eat/sleep)

**CD makes final call in consultation with:**
- Parents
- Program Director
- SSB Helpline (if mental health component)

## What to Say (Exact Scripts)

### Initial conversation:
"I've noticed you seem really down. Want to talk about what's going on?"

[Student says they miss home]

"That makes total sense. Being away from your family and your routine is really hard, especially the first few days."

"A lot of students feel this way at first. You're not alone in this."

### Encouraging participation:
"I know you don't feel like joining the group right now. Would you be willing to just try it for 10 minutes? If you still want to step away after that, that's okay."

"I saw you sitting with your mentor group today. I know that probably felt hard, and I'm really glad you showed up."

### When student wants to go home:
"I hear that you want to go home. That's a big decision. Can we talk about what's making you feel that way?"

"Let's give it until [specific date - usually 2-3 more days]. Sometimes it takes a few days to settle in. If you still feel this way on [date], we'll talk to your parents and figure out the best plan."

### Building connection:
"Have you met [student name] yet? I think you two would really click. Want me to introduce you?"

"What's one thing you usually do at home when you're feeling down? Is there any way we can do something like that here?"

## What NOT to Say

❌ "Just try to have fun" (dismissive)
❌ "Everyone else is fine" (comparison, shameful)
❌ "You're being a baby" (cruel)
❌ "You begged your parents to come here" (guilt)
❌ "Suck it up" (invalidating)
❌ "Stop crying" (can't control it)
❌ "You're ruining the program for yourself" (adds pressure)
❌ "If you leave, you're a quitter" (shameful)

## What TO Say

✅ "Missing home is really normal"
✅ "This is hard, and you're being brave by being here"
✅ "Let's take it one day at a time"
✅ "What would help you feel a little bit better right now?"
✅ "I'm here to support you"
✅ "You're not alone in feeling this way"
✅ "It's okay to feel sad and still try to participate"

## Who to Call and When

| Situation | Contact | When |
|-----------|---------|------|
| Mild homesickness Days 1-3 | No escalation needed | N/A - normal, handle with support |
| Homesickness not improving by Day 5 | CD | Day 5 |
| Student refuses to eat/sleep | CD | Immediately |
| Student expresses self-harm thoughts | CD + SSB Helpline | Immediately |
| Student attempts to leave campus | CD + 9-1-1 if needed | Immediately |
| Parent demands early pickup | CD | Immediately |
| Severe emotional distress | CD | Same day |
| All cases of homesickness | Incident Report | If persists beyond Day 3 or is severe |

## Follow-Up Procedures

### Daily check-ins (if student staying):
- Brief morning check-in: "How are you feeling today?"
- Evening check-in before bed: "How did today go?"
- Notice improvements: "I saw you laughing with [student] today. That made me happy to see."

### Monitor for:
- Eating at meals
- Sleeping (ask roommate if student seems to sleep)
- Engaging in activities (even minimally)
- Connecting with at least one peer
- Reducing parent call frequency

### Document:
- Daily notes on student's mood and participation
- Incident Report if homesickness severe or prolonged
- All CD conversations

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Ignoring early signs** - Homesickness can escalate to crisis
2. **Forcing participation** - Increases resistance
3. **Allowing constant parent contact** - Can worsen homesickness
4. **Comparing to other students** - "Everyone else is fine" is harmful
5. **Not taking it seriously** - Homesickness can be genuinely severe
6. **Promising student they can leave** - Only CD can make that decision
7. **Not documenting** - Important to track progression
8. **Isolating student further** - They need connection, not alone time
9. **Not coordinating with CD** - CD needs to know what's happening
10. **Telling parents without CD approval** - CD handles parent communication

## Differentiating: Normal Homesickness vs. Mental Health Crisis

| Normal Homesickness | Mental Health Crisis |
|---------------------|---------------------|
| Improves with support/distraction | Worsens or stays severe |
| Student can still participate | Complete withdrawal |
| Eating and sleeping (even if reduced) | Refuses to eat/can't sleep |
| No mention of self-harm | Self-harm thoughts or behavior |
| Responds to engagement strategies | Nothing helps |
| "I miss home but I'll try" | "I need to leave NOW" |

**If it crosses into mental health crisis → Follow mental health emergency protocols + SSB Helpline**

## Prevention Strategies

**During orientation:**
- Normalize homesickness: "Many of you will miss home. That's okay and expected."
- Set expectations: "First 3 days are often the hardest. It usually gets better."
- Point out support: "Your mentor is here for you. Come talk to us."

**First days:**
- High engagement activities (less time to ruminate)
- Build mentor group bonds quickly
- Encourage students to make one friend by Day 2
- Structured schedule (predictability helps anxiety)

**Ongoing:**
- Positive peer culture
- Celebrate small wins
- Fun, engaging programming
- Regular mentor check-ins with all students

## When to Send Student Home Early

**CD decides with Program Director input. Typical reasons:**

1. **Student's mental health at risk**
   - Severe depression, anxiety, panic attacks
   - Self-harm ideation
   - Inability to function

2. **Student refuses to participate**
   - Won't leave room
   - Won't eat
   - Total shutdown

3. **Parental insistence**
   - Parents demand immediate pickup
   - (Note: SSB can refuse if safety not an issue, but rare)

4. **No improvement after intervention**
   - Full week of severe homesickness with no progress
   - Student miserable, impacting health

**Process:**
1. CD calls parents
2. Arrange transportation home
3. No refund (per SSB policy)
4. Complete Incident Report
5. Debrief with student: "This doesn't mean you failed. This just means this wasn't the right time/fit."

## Staff Self-Care

**Supporting homesick students is emotionally draining.**

- Debrief with CD or fellow mentors
- Don't take it personally if student wants to leave
- Recognize your limits - you can't "fix" severe homesickness
- Celebrate small wins with students who stay and improve
- Know when to hand off to CD

## Resources

**For student:**
- Scheduled parent calls (structure, not all-day access)
- Mentor support
- Buddy system with peer
- Engaging activities
- Small job/responsibility
- Letters from home (if helpful)

**For staff:**
- CD for escalation decisions
- SSB Helpline: +1.858.779.0555 (if mental health crisis)
