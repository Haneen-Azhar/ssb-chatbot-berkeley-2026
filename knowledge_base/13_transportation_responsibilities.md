# Transportation Responsibilities - Summer Springboard 2026

## Transportation Overview

**CRITICAL:** Transportation is handled in TWO phases - planning BEFORE program and execution DURING program.

---

## BEFORE Program Starts

### DOCO (Director of Campus Operations) and CD Responsibilities

**DOCO and CD arrange ALL transportation contracts and logistics before staff arrive:**

1. **Contract Negotiation**
   - Book bus companies and vendors
   - Negotiate rates and terms
   - Secure transportation for entire program duration
   - Confirm vehicle capacity and availability

2. **Pre-Program Planning**
   - Schedule all known transport needs (excursions, airport runs, field trips)
   - Add transport details to Master Schedule
   - Create transport sheet with all bookings
   - Confirm driver credentials and insurance

3. **Documentation**
   - Transport sheet in Master Schedule shows all bookings
   - Contact info for bus companies
   - Pickup/dropoff locations documented
   - Emergency contact numbers for vendors

**Bottom line:** All transport is booked and paid for BEFORE the program starts.

---

## DURING Program

### SPA (Student Program Assistant) Responsibilities

**SPA handles day-to-day transport execution and coordination:**

1. **Confirmation Calls**
   - Call bus companies night before to confirm pickup
   - Verify driver assignment and vehicle
   - Confirm pickup time and location
   - Check headcount capacity

2. **Using Transport Sheet**
   - Reference Master Schedule transport sheet
   - Track all scheduled transport
   - Coordinate timing with daily schedule
   - Update any last-minute changes

3. **Day-of Coordination**
   - Ensure buses arrive on time
   - Coordinate student boarding
   - Work with mentors on headcounts
   - Communicate delays or issues to CD

4. **What SPA Does NOT Do**
   - Book new transport contracts
   - Negotiate rates
   - Arrange transportation not already scheduled
   - Handle billing or vendor payments

**Bottom line:** SPA executes what's already been arranged. Confirms, coordinates, doesn't book.

---

## CD Role During Program

**CD handles problems and changes:**
- Last-minute transport needs
- Vendor issues or no-shows
- Route changes or cancellations
- Emergency transport arrangements

**CD is backup when:** SPA encounters transport problems that need immediate resolution.

---

## Transport During Activities

### Field Trips and Excursions
1. **Before departure:**
   - SPA confirms bus with vendor (night before)
   - Mentors prepare student lists
   - First aid kits loaded on bus
   - Staff assignments confirmed

2. **During transport:**
   - Maintain 10:1 staff-to-student ratio
   - One staff member per bus minimum
   - Headcounts at boarding and arrival
   - Monitor student behavior

3. **After return:**
   - Final headcount
   - Report any incidents to CD
   - Document any transport issues

### Airport Runs
- SPA coordinates pickup times with flight schedules
- Confirms drivers night before
- Assigns mentors to each airport run
- Tracks student arrivals via transport sheet

---

## Emergency Transport

**Medical emergencies requiring immediate transport:**
1. Call 9-1-1 first
2. Notify CD immediately
3. Staff member rides with student
4. CD calls PD and parents

**Non-emergency issues:**
- Late bus: SPA calls vendor, updates CD
- Wrong pickup location: SPA coordinates with driver
- Capacity issues: CD handles rebooking

---

## Key Takeaways

**Who does what:**
- **DOCO/CD BEFORE program:** Book buses, negotiate contracts, arrange all transport
- **SPA DURING program:** Confirm buses, coordinate timing, execute scheduled transport
- **CD DURING program:** Handle problems, last-minute changes, emergency transport

**Resources:**
- Master Schedule transport sheet: All scheduled transport
- Vendor contact numbers: In Summer Staff Portal
- Emergency procedures: See Emergency Procedures doc

**Questions about transport?**
- "Who booked the bus?" → DOCO/CD before program
- "Who confirms the bus?" → SPA night before
- "Who handles problems?" → CD during program
- "Who rides on the bus?" → Mentors (maintaining 10:1 ratio)
