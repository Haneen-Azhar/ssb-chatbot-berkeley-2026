## Course Support Structure

### Classroom Mentors
- Each course has assigned classroom mentor
- Mentor supports instructor during class
- Assists with materials and student questions
- Monitors student engagement
- Helps with classroom management if needed

### Teaching Assistants (TAs)
- Some courses have dedicated TAs
- Example: Steven (TA) for Fundamentals of Engineering
- Support instructor with technical aspects
- May lead lab sections or breakout groups

### Shadowing Mentors
- Some mentors shadow courses for training
- Example: Juan (Shadowing) for Business & Entrepreneurship
- Observe and learn instructional techniques
- May assist as backup support

## Course Materials and Supplies

### General Academic Supplies
- Provided through campus supplies inventory
- Tracked by Campus Director
- Replenished between sessions

### Course-Specific Supplies
- Engineering: Lab materials, tools, prototyping supplies
- Medical courses: Simulation equipment, anatomical models
- Art/Design: Drawing materials, design software access
- Science: Lab equipment (at Stanley Hall)
- Business: Case study materials, presentation equipment

### Technology Requirements
- Students may need laptops for some courses
- Campus computer labs available
- Software licenses as needed per course
- Wi-Fi access in all classroom locations

## College Readiness Workshops (CRW)

### Structure
- Separate from academic courses
- All students participate
- Led by CRW Mentors
- Scheduled throughout program

### Topics
- College application process
- Essay writing
- Interview preparation
- Resume building
- Campus tour insights
- Financial aid overview
- College selection strategies

### Mentor Assignments
- Specific mentors assigned as CRW Mentors
- Rotate based on schedule
- Support students through college prep process

## Excursions and Field Experiences

### Academic Excursions
- Course-related field trips
- Coordinated by instructor and Academic Manager
- Require transportation and logistics planning
- Enhanced learning experiences

### Weekend Excursions
- Saturdays and Sundays
- All students participate
- Separate from academic courses
- Cultural and recreational focus
- All hands on deck for staff

## Course Evaluation and Feedback

### Student Feedback
- Course evaluations at end of session
- Informal feedback to mentors throughout
- Academic Manager monitors student satisfaction

### Instructor Feedback
- Academic Manager provides observations
- Campus Director may observe courses
- Continuous improvement focus

## Academic Integrity and Policies

### Attendance
- Students expected to attend all classes
- Mentors track attendance
- Absences reported to Campus Director
- Medical excuses coordinated with health office

### Academic Honesty
- SSB academic integrity policy applies
- Instructors enforce in coursework
- Violations reported to Campus Director
- May result in program consequences

### Accommodations
- Students with documented needs
- Coordinated before program start
- Instructors informed of accommodations
- Mentors support implementation

## Session Structure

### Program Sessions
Based on schedule patterns, there are 6 sessions throughout summer:
- **Session 1**
- **Session 2**
- **Session 3**
- **Session 4**
- **Session 5**
- **Session 6**

Each session typically runs:
- **Sunday to Friday** (one week format, exact days vary)
- Includes academic instruction Monday-Friday
- Weekend excursions Saturday-Sunday
- Student arrival Sundays (Sessions 1, 3, 5)
- Student departure Fridays/Sundays

### Course Intensity
- Full-day academic engagement
- Hands-on learning emphasis
- Project-based coursework
- College-level rigor
- Prepare students for college academics

## Special Course Features

- May include hospital or clinic visits
- Guest speakers from medical field
- Simulation lab experiences

- Networking opportunities

### Arts and Design Courses

- Use of university research facilities

### With Students
- Professional and engaging
