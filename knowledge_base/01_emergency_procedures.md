## EARTHQUAKE EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### Recognition
**You will recognize an earthquake by:**
- Sudden shaking or rolling motion
- Items falling from shelves
- Difficulty standing or walking
- Sounds of breaking glass or structural stress

### FIRST 60 SECONDS - IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

#### If INDOORS:
1. **DROP** to the floor immediately (within 1-2 seconds)
2. Take **COVER** under sturdy furniture (desk, table) or near an interior wall
3. **Protect your head** with your elbows and arms
4. **HOLD ON** to furniture legs until shaking completely stops
5. Stay in position - do NOT move until shaking stops completely

**CRITICAL DON'Ts:**
- Do NOT run outside during shaking
- Do NOT stand in a doorway (common myth - safer under sturdy furniture)
- Do NOT use elevators
- Stay away from windows, mirrors, heavy objects that can fall

#### If OUTDOORS:
1. Move away from buildings, power lines, trees (within 3-5 seconds)
2. **DROP** to ground
3. **COVER** head and neck with arms
4. **HOLD ON** - stay low and stable
5. If driving: Pull over to clear area immediately, stay in vehicle with seatbelt on

### Who to Call and In What Order

**After shaking stops:**

1. **First:** If anyone is injured or trapped - call 911 immediately
   - From campus phone: 9-1-1
   - From cell phone: 510-642-3333 (UCPD)

2. **Second:** Campus Director for your team
   - HS Team A: [TBD]

4. Keep group together at EAA

### If Trapped by Debris

**Signaling for Help:**
1. Do NOT yell (may inhale dangerous dust)
2. **Tap on metal objects** for attention
   - 3 consecutive taps = universal SOS signal
   - Repeat every few minutes
3. Use mobile phone to call 911 (510-642-3333 on campus)
4. Text location and situation to Campus Director if can't speak
5. Use cell phone flashlight (Do NOT light matches - potential gas leaks)
6. Cover mouth and nose with clothing to filter dust
7. Conserve phone battery - use sparingly

**What to Say:**
- "I am trapped in [building name, floor, room number]"
- "I am [alone / with X number of people]"
- "Injuries: [describe]" OR "No injuries"
- "I can/cannot move"
- "I smell [gas/smoke/nothing unusual]"
- Keep phone on and nearby

## ACTIVE SHOOTER / ACTIVE THREAT EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### Recognition
**You will recognize an active shooter situation by:**
- Sounds of gunshots (loud pops or bangs)
- People running and screaming
- Visible person with firearm
- WarnMe alert notification
- Emergency siren
- Someone shouting about shooter/weapon

### FIRST 60 SECONDS - IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

Berkeley has adopted the **"RUN-HIDE-FIGHT"** model as standard protocol.

#### OPTION 1: RUN (If safe escape route available)
**First 5-10 seconds:**
1. Grab ONLY your cell phone
2. Leave everything else
3. Identify nearest exit
4. Go immediately

**Next 10-50 seconds:**
5. Run in different direction from sounds
6. Keep hands visible
7. Encourage others but DO NOT lag behind to wait
8. Help others escape if possible but don't stop your own escape

**Once safe:**
9. Call 911 immediately: **510-642-3333** (from cell phone on campus)
10. Keep running until completely away from building

#### OPTION 2: HIDE (If cannot safely reach exit)
**First 3-5 seconds:**
1. Find room or space with door
2. Get inside immediately
3. Lock door from inside

**Next 5-15 seconds:**
4. If no lock: Barricade door with furniture (desks, chairs, filing cabinets)
5. Turn off ALL lights
6. Close blinds/cover windows if possible
7. Silence cell phones (put on silent, not vibrate)

**Next 15-40 seconds:**
8. Move away from door and windows
9. Find cover/hiding spot (behind desk, in closet)
10. Stay low to ground
11. Spread out (don't huddle in one spot if in group)
12. Stay absolutely silent
13. Text 911 if can't speak: 510-642-3333

**Stay hidden:**
- Do NOT open door for anyone until police give all-clear
- Do NOT respond to voices (shooter may try to lure people out)
- Keep phone on silent and monitor WarnMe alerts

#### OPTION 3: FIGHT (Last resort only - if shooter enters your location)
**Immediate:**
1. Commit to your actions - this is life or death
2. Work together if with others - coordinate silently
3. Grab nearest object as weapon:
   - Chairs, desks
   - Fire extinguisher
   - Books, laptops
   - Scissors, letter opener
   - Hot coffee
   - Any heavy or sharp object

**When shooter enters:**
4. Act with extreme aggression
5. Throw items at shooter's face/head
6. Aim to incapacitate: strike head, neck, knees
7. Do NOT stop until threat is neutralized
8. Disarm if possible - throw weapon away immediately

### Who to Call and In What Order

**If you can safely call (have escaped or hidden):**

1. **FIRST - CALL 911 IMMEDIATELY**
   - From cell phone on campus: **510-642-3333** (UC Berkeley Police - FASTER)
   - From campus phone: 9-1-1

2. **If can't speak, TEXT 911:** Send to 510-642-3333

   - "I am hiding in [location]"
   - Keep texting updates

3. **After calling 911, IF SAFE to do so:**

**DO NOT call Campus Director before 911 - every second counts**

**When calling 911/UCPD (speak clearly and calmly):**
- "Active shooter at [building name and address]"
- "I am at [specific location - building, floor, room number]"
- "I heard shots coming from [direction/location]"

### Evacuation Routes and Assembly Points

**DO NOT USE NORMAL ASSEMBLY POINTS IN ACTIVE SHOOTER SITUATION**

#### If You Evacuate (RUN):
1. Run away from building in any safe direction
2. Run at least 2-3 blocks away
3. Do NOT go to normal Emergency Assembly Areas
4. Find shelter in another building/business
5. Call 911 once completely safe
6. Follow police instructions on where to go
7. Expect to be searched/questioned by police

#### Police Will Establish:
- Staging areas (they will communicate location)
- Reunification point (after threat is eliminated)
- Perimeter - stay outside perimeter

### After First Responders Arrive

**If You Are Hiding:**
1. Do NOT leave hiding spot until police come to you
2. Do NOT open door - wait for police to open it
3. When police arrive at your location:
   - Keep hands visible above head
   - Follow all commands immediately
   - Do NOT make sudden movements
   - Do NOT ask questions - just follow commands
   - Expect to be searched
   - Expect to be evacuated with hands up/on head

**If You Are Evacuating:**
1. Keep hands visible above your head at all times
2. Do NOT carry anything
3. Do NOT stop to help injured (police/medics will handle)
4. Follow police commands exactly
5. Move quickly where directed
6. Do NOT use phone while evacuating
7. Expect to be searched

### Assembly Points After All-Clear

### Instructor/Staff Protocol During Active Shooter Alert

#### If Teaching/In Classroom When Alert Occurs:

**Immediate (First 10 seconds):**
1. Command students: "Lockdown now - everyone away from door"
2. Lock classroom door from inside
3. If no interior lock: Barricade door with desks/furniture immediately

**Next 20 seconds:**
4. Turn off lights
5. Close blinds
6. Direct students away from door and windows
7. Everyone get low to ground

**Next 30 seconds:**
8. Silence all phones
9. Take quick attendance (mental count)
10. Monitor WarnMe alerts (silent)
11. Prepare to fight if needed (identify potential weapons in room)

**Ongoing:**
- Keep students calm and silent
- Do NOT open door for anyone
- Wait for police to come to classroom
- If students panic, quietly and firmly redirect them

#### For Buildings That Lock from Outside (No Interior Lock):

**Immediate barricading critical:**
1. Stack furniture against door (desks, chairs, anything heavy)
2. Wedge door shut if possible (door stop, belt, etc.)
3. Everyone move away from door
4. Prepare to counter (fight) if shooter breaches door
5. Identify heaviest objects in room to throw
6. Position strongest people nearest to door (only if fight is necessary)

#### If Shooter Enters Building But Not Yet in Classroom:
- Stay completely silent
- Do NOT peek out
- Listen for:
  - Police commands ("Police! Show your hands!")
  - Gunshots getting closer or farther
  - WarnMe updates
- Be prepared to fight if door is breached

## BUILDING FIRE EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### Recognition
**You will recognize a fire emergency by:**
- Smell of smoke
- Fire alarm sounding
- Visible flames or smoke
- Heat from walls or doors
- People shouting "fire"

### FIRST 60 SECONDS - IMMEDIATE ACTIONS

#### If Fire Alarm Sounds:
1. **Immediately evacuate** - within 5 seconds of alarm
2. Grab cell phone if within arm's reach
3. Leave everything else
4. Do NOT waste time gathering belongings

#### Choosing Exit Route:
1. Identify closest exit (within 3 seconds)
2. **Check door before opening:**
   - Feel door handle with **back of your hand**
   - If warm or hot: DO NOT OPEN - find different exit
   - If cool: Open slowly and check for smoke
3. If smoke visible in hallway: close door, find alternate exit

#### Evacuating Building:
1. Use stairs ONLY - **NEVER use elevators**
2. Stay low if smoke present (better air near floor)
3. Cover nose and mouth with shirt/cloth if smoky
4. **Pull fire alarm** as you exit (if not already sounding)
5. Close doors behind you (slows fire spread)
6. Move quickly but don't run
7. Help others if safe to do so
8. Once outside, move at least 50 feet from building

### If You Cannot Exit

7. Wave bright cloth/shirt out window to signal for help
8. If smoke entering room: stay low to floor where air is better
9. Cover nose and mouth with damp cloth if possible
10. Seal vents with tape/clothing if smoke coming through

**Seek Designated Waiting Area:**
- Some buildings have Designated Waiting Areas for people who cannot use stairs

## MEDICAL EMERGENCY PROCEDURES

### Recognition of Medical Emergencies Requiring Immediate Care

**Call 911 IMMEDIATELY if person has:**
- Unconsciousness or unresponsiveness
- No breathing or difficulty breathing/gasping
- No pulse or weak pulse
- Chest pain or pressure
- Severe bleeding that won't stop
- Seizure lasting more than 5 minutes

- Broken bones with bone visible or severe deformity
- Severe allergic reaction (difficulty breathing, swelling of face/throat)

#### Campus Lockdown (Secure in Place)
**Called if there is violent or aggressive person on campus.**

**If Asked to Secure in Place:**
1. Lock and barricade doors immediately
2. Move away from doors and windows
3. Close window shades/blinds
4. Turn off ALL lights
5. Stay low to ground
6. Silence cell phones (silent, not vibrate)
7. Do NOT open door for anyone
8. Monitor WarnMe alerts (silently)
9. Wait for all-clear from police

**Difference:**
- **Shelter in Place:** Protection from external hazard (chemical, weather). Stay indoors, windows closed.
- **Secure in Place (Lockdown):** Protection from violent threat on campus. Lock doors, lights off, hide.

   - CPS or law enforcement may interview you
   - Answer questions honestly
   - Provide any additional information requested

### Protection for Reporters

**Legal Protection:**
- Mandated reporters are legally protected when making good faith reports
- Cannot be sued or prosecuted for reporting in good faith
- Identity kept confidential to extent possible by law

**Consequences of NOT Reporting:**
- Mandated reporters who fail to report can face:
  - Criminal charges
  - Civil liability
  - Termination from SSB
  - Professional consequences

### If Student Discloses Abuse to You

**Immediate Response:**

1. **Stay calm and listen**
   - Do NOT show shock or disbelief
   - Do NOT appear upset or angry
   - Remain supportive and calm

2. **Believe the student**
   - Say "Thank you for telling me"
   - Say "This is not your fault"
   - Say "I'm glad you told me"
   - Do NOT question or doubt their account

3. **Do NOT promise confidentiality**
   - Say "I need to share this with people who can help you"
   - Say "I'm required by law to report this"
   - Explain this is to keep them safe

4. **Do NOT interrogate**
   - Let student tell you in their own words
   - Do NOT ask leading questions
   - Do NOT ask for details beyond what student volunteers
   - Do NOT ask "why" questions

5. **Document exactly what student said**
   - Write down student's exact words as soon as possible
   - Note date, time, location of disclosure
   - Note who else was present

6. **Make required report immediately**
   - Follow mandated reporting procedures above

### Special Notes

**Training Requirement:**
- All staff working with minors required to complete mandated reporter training before working on program
- Training provided by SSB
- Certification required

**Applies to:**
- Any abuse/neglect of minors under 18
- Abuse by parent, guardian, or caretaker
- Abuse by another adult
- Abuse by another student
- Current abuse OR past abuse that may indicate ongoing risk

**Remember:**
- Your job is to REPORT, not to investigate
- CPS and law enforcement will investigate
- Err on side of reporting if unsure
- Better to report and be wrong than not report and leave child at risk

   - Remove people from immediate danger
   - Call 911 if life-threatening

2. **Follow Emergency Response Protocols**
   - Use specific protocol for emergency type (earthquake, fire, medical, etc.)
   - Refer to this Emergency Action Plan

3. **Apply First Aid as Necessary**
   - If trained and safe to do so
   - Do NOT exceed your training level
   - Wait for paramedics for serious injuries

4. **Contact SSB Campus Director**
   - Report emergency immediately
   - Provide critical information
   - Follow Campus Director's instructions

5. **Continued Monitoring and Care**
   - Monitor affected individuals
   - Support student group
   - Provide updates to Campus Director

6. **Documentation**
   - Document incident thoroughly
   - Complete required reports
   - Preserve evidence if applicable

## EMERGENCY RESOURCES WITHOUT CELL/INTERNET

### If You Cannot Contact SSB HQ

**In event without cellular/internet connection:**

**Priority Order:**
1. **Stabilize group**
   - Ensure all students safe
   - Account for all students
   - Address immediate medical needs
   - Find shelter if needed

2. **Communicate as team**
   - Gather all staff
   - Share information
   - Assign roles
   - Coordinate actions

3. **Follow instructions from local & rescue teams**
   - UC Berkeley emergency personnel
   - Local police and fire
   - Emergency management officials
   - Follow all official directions

4. **Use emergency phone features**
   - Many cellphones have emergency feature for 911 even without service
   - Emergency calls may work when regular calls don't
   - Try texting if voice calls don't work

5. **Contact Insurance**
   - SSB Insurance: GLM N14286032-SSB
   - Phone: +1.312.935.1703

6. **Find working communication**
   - Use campus landlines if working
   - Ask emergency personnel to relay message
   - Use other people's phones if available
   - Go to emergency operations center if established

7. **Continue trying to contact HQ**
   - SSB 24/7 Helpline: +1.858.779.0555
   - Try every 30 minutes
   - Leave voicemail with critical info and callback number

## SUMMARY OF KEY PROTOCOLS

### EARTHQUAKE: DROP, COVER, HOLD ON
- Indoors: Under furniture, protect head
- Outdoors: Away from buildings
- After: Evacuate to EAA, don't re-enter
- Call 911 if injuries
- Memorial Glade for Stanley Hall

### ACTIVE SHOOTER: RUN, HIDE, FIGHT
- RUN if safe escape route
- HIDE if can't escape (lock, lights off, silent)
- FIGHT as last resort
- Call 510-642-3333 immediately
- Follow police commands exactly

### FIRE: EVACUATE IMMEDIATELY
- Feel door before opening
- Stairs only, never elevators
- Pull alarm
- Go to EAA
- Call 911, don't re-enter

### MEDICAL EMERGENCY: CALL 911 FIRST
- Assess safety and responsiveness
- Call 510-642-3333 (cell) or 9-1-1 (campus phone)
- Begin first aid if trained
- Send someone to meet ambulance
- Document everything

### MISSING STUDENT: ACT FAST
- 15 min: Call Campus Director
- 30 min: Call SSB HQ
- 60 min or any concern: Call UCPD 510-642-3333
- Search systematically
- Document everything

### MANDATED REPORTING: REPORT IMMEDIATELY
- Any suspected abuse/neglect of minor
- Call UCPD 510-642-3333 AND Alameda County CPS 510-259-1800
- Don't investigate, don't promise confidentiality
- Document exact words
- Notify Campus Director and SSB

### CAMPUS SIREN: SHELTER—SHUT—LISTEN
- Go indoors immediately
- Close all doors and windows
- Get information: 800-705-9998, emergency.berkeley.edu, KALX 90.7FM
- Stay until all-clear

### MEDIA: NO COMMENT
- Refer all media to Program Director
- Do NOT discuss on social media
- Do NOT share photos/videos

### PARENTS: SSB CONTACTS
- Do NOT contact parents directly
- All parent communication through Campus Director and SSB HQ
- Provide updates to Campus Director
