# Staff Roles and Responsibilities - Summer Springboard 2026

## Staff Structure Overview

### Hierarchy
```
Program Director (PD)
    ├── Campus Director (CD)
    │   ├── Academic Manager (AM)
    │   ├── Summer Program Assistant (SPA)
    │   └── Mentors
    └── HQ Support Staff
```

## Program Director (PD)

### Primary Responsibilities

#### Before Summer
1. **Campus Team Setup**
   - Prepare Summer Staff Portal (holy grail of links)
   - Organize reports, master schedules, transportation, classrooms
   - Ensure access to all excursion confirmations
   - Prepare pre-program training slides
   - CD leads majority of PPT, PD covers Terra policies

2. **Operational Readiness**
   - Confirm all logistics in place
   - Review and approve budgets
   - Coordinate with conference reps
   - Finalize instructor contracts
   - Review student enrollments and special accommodations

#### During Summer
1. **Behind-the-Scenes Support**
   - Available when CD has exhausted all resources
   - Crisis support
   - Direction when information unclear
   - Brainstorming and problem-solving support

2. **Communication Flow**
   - Weekly reports from CDs
   - On-call updates
   - Incident report reviews
   - Major site updates

3. **Regular Check-ins**
   - Call or text with CDs to ensure support
   - Monitor program health
   - Address concerns proactively

#### Communication Expectations
- CDs communicate directly with Conference Reps
- PDs support from behind the scenes
- CDs CC PDs on summer communication
- Instructor issues → AM handles first (unless curriculum/pay/serious escalation)
- Mentor concerns → SPA first, then escalate to CD

### When PDs Get Involved

**Always Involved:**
- Behavioral contracts (Step 2 of discipline)
- Program dismissals (Step 3 of discipline)
- Room searches
- Cell phone searches
- Major crises
- Safety incidents
- Serious Code of Conduct violations

**As Needed:**
- Complex parent situations
- Staff performance issues
- Budget concerns
- Logistical challenges

## Mentors

### Core Responsibilities

#### 1. Student Safety
- Primary responsibility always
- Know medical info for mentor group students
- Carry EpiPens if assigned
- Monitor student well-being constantly

#### 2. Relationship Building
- Deep connections with ~10 students (HS) or ~8 students (MS)
- Daily touchpoints through meals and activities
- Trusted confidant
- Positive role model

#### 3. Academic Support
- Attendance taking
- Classroom presence (as POC)
- Support instructors
- CRW facilitation
- Monitor engagement

#### 4. Residential Supervision
- Bed checks
- Floor monitoring
- Enforce rules
- Create safe environment

#### 5. Activity Leadership
- Evening activities
- Clubs
- Excursions
- Mentor group activities

#### 6. Communication
- Relay info to students
- Report concerns to leadership
- Use REACH for check-ins
- Professional with parents (through CD/AM)

### What Makes a Great Mentor

**The 6 M's:**

1. **Mediator:** Mediate conflict and maintain positive group dynamic
2. **Model:** Be role model to students
3. **Caretaker:** Take care of physical and mental health of students
4. **Listener:** Be active, empathetic listener
5. **Facilitator:** Facilitate meaningful discussion and reflection
6. **Teammate:** Offer proactive assistance to team members

### Mentor Group Leadership

**Know Your Students:**
- Medical information (allergies, POAs, physical limitations, EpiPens)
- Privileged information (mental health history, triggers, gender identity)
- Academic courses and interests
- Personality and social needs

**Daily Touchpoints:**
- Breakfast together
- Lunch together
- Dinner together
- Before excursions (attendance and bus boarding)
- Evening check-ins
- Bed checks

**Mentor Group Best Practices:**
1. Find space conducive to inclusivity and privacy
2. Encourage students to sit within group
3. Consider ratios (residential vs commuter, courses, rooming, gender, age)
4. Don't skip mentor groups
5. Be the person students trust most

### Daily Schedule for Mentors

**See Daily Schedule Operations document for detailed timeline**

**Morning:** Breakfast with mentor group, support check-in
**9-12:** Academic classroom support (if assigned)
**12-1:** Lunch with mentor group
**1-6:** Afternoon activities (as assigned)
**6-7:** Dinner with mentor group
**7-9:30:** Evening activities

### Time Off
- Minimum 1 night off (6 PM - 8 AM) per week
- No time off Saturday, Sunday, or final night
- Must stay within 2 hours of campus
- Coordinate with team for coverage

### Mentor as Instructor Liaison

**As Mentor POC for a course:**
- Take attendance at 9:00 AM
- Report missing students to AM immediately
- Remain available (in or near classroom)
- Support instructor as needed
- Attend academic excursions for that course

**For CRWs:**
- Take attendance upon arrival
- Stay in room entire workshop
- Assist with classroom management
- Support facilitator (don't facilitate unless trained)

**Do NOT:**
- Make last-minute copies (instructor's responsibility)
- Facilitate class (unless specifically requested 1+ week in advance)

## Coaching vs. Performance Management

### Coaching Culture
**SSB treats most staff challenges as coaching opportunities first**

**Questions to ask:**
- Does staff member understand expectations?
- What support do they need?
- What tools/resources are missing?
- What leadership adjustments may help?
- Have expectations been clearly communicated?

**Document using:** SSB Staff Coaching and Performance Management Report

### When Coaching Becomes Performance Management

**Performance Improvement Plan (PIP) needed when:**
- Issues continue after coaching
- Accountability conversations are documented
- Student/staff safety or experience is impacted
- Repeated operational concerns
- Ongoing professional issues
- Failure to improve after coaching
- Multiple documented concerns

**Always involves PD and HQ**

### Staff Support and Preventing Burnout

**CD Responsibilities:**
- Regular check-ins with team
- Monitor for exhaustion
- Encourage time off
- Create supportive culture
- Address concerns early
- Provide resources
- Model self-care

**Team Support:**
- Cover for each other
- Communicate needs
- Share workload
- Celebrate wins together
- Debrief hard moments
- Build community

## Staff Policies (Key Points)

**See Staff Operating Manual for complete policies**

### Alcohol and Substances
- This is a dry campus
- No smoking or drinking during program
- Designated outings may allow adult beverages (off program time)

### Visitors
- No visitors on campus
- No guests in residence halls
- Maintain professional environment

### Social Media
- No photos with students on personal accounts
- Can tag SSB official accounts
- Professional posts only
- Protect student privacy

### Relationships
- No relationships between staff members during program
- Maintain professional boundaries
- Focus on students and program

### Banned Practices
- Sharing room with student
- Being alone with student in closed room
- Touching student when no one else around
- Flirting with students
- Any sexual conduct with students
- Allowing students to kiss
- Relationships with fellow staff

### Acceptable Practices
- Being in room with 3+ students present
- Accepting hug in open space with others around
- Hanging out with other staff during free time
- Building appropriate mentor-student relationships
- Professional, caring interactions
