# Physical Fighting - Behavioral Issue

**Tags:** fight, fighting, physical altercation, hitting, punching, pushing, violence, assault, attack, students fighting, dismissal, code of conduct violation, behavioral issue

## Recognition Triggers
- Student reports of physical altercations between students
- Staff witnessing pushing, hitting, striking, or other physical contact
- Play fighting that escalates or appears aggressive
- Verbal threats preceding physical contact
- Crowd gathering around students engaged in conflict
- Injuries or marks on students
- Student behavioral changes after conflicts (isolation, agitation)

## Severity Assessment

### ZERO TOLERANCE (Immediate Dismissal)
- Actual physical fighting between students
- Severe bullying involving physical violence
- Use of weapons in altercations
- Causing significant injury to another student
- Gang-up scenarios (multiple students attacking one)

### ESCALATING CONSEQUENCE (Warning → Contract → Dismissal)
- Play fighting that doesn't stop when redirected
- Aggressive posturing or threatening physical contact
- Minor shoving or pushing during arguments

### RESTORATIVE (Mediation/Education)
- NOT APPLICABLE - physical violence is always disciplinary track

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Immediate Intervention
**Safety First:**
1. Immediately intervene to stop the physical contact
2. Separate students involved (different locations, different staff supervision)
3. Check for injuries - assess if medical attention needed
4. Ensure all other students are safe and supervised
5. Secure the area - prevent crowd gathering or escalation
6. DO NOT leave students unsupervised at any point

**Initial Assessment:**
- Are there injuries requiring immediate medical care?
- Are students calm enough to speak, or do they need cooling-off period?
- Are there weapons involved?
- How many students were involved?

### Step 2: Investigation
**Gather Information Systematically:**

1. **Separate Interviews** - Interview all parties separately
   - Student A's account (what happened, what led to it, who was involved)
   - Student B's account (same questions)
   - Witness accounts (at least 2-3 if available)
   - Staff who first observed or intervened

2. **Documentation During Investigation:**
   - Take photos of any injuries (with student consent)
   - Document exact timeline (when fight started, when staff intervened)
   - Note exact location
   - Preserve any physical evidence (torn clothing, etc.)
   - Screenshot any relevant messages/social media if applicable

3. **Key Questions to Ask Each Student:**
   - "Tell me what happened from your perspective"
   - "What led up to this situation?"
   - "Did anyone else see what happened?"
   - "Have you had previous conflicts with this person?"
   - "Were you injured? Do you need medical attention?"
   - DO NOT ask leading questions or assign blame during investigation

### Step 3: Discipline Decision
**Campus Director determines consequence in consultation with PD/HQ:**

**Physical Fighting = Immediate Dismissal Track**
- Call Program Director IMMEDIATELY
- Call HQ to loop in leadership
- DO NOT tell student they're going home until PD/HQ confirms

**Decision Factors:**
- Who initiated physical contact?
- Was this self-defense or mutual combat?
- Prior disciplinary history of students involved
- Severity of physical contact
- Injuries sustained
- Context (long-standing conflict vs. isolated incident)

**Timeline:**
- Investigation should be completed within 2-4 hours of incident
- Dismissal decision made same day
- Student departs program within 24 hours

### Step 4: Implementation
**Dismissal Process for Physical Fighting:**

1. **Before Informing Student - Call PD/HQ to Confirm** (REQUIRED)

2. **Inform Student of Dismissal:**
   - TWO staff members must be present
   - Private location (office or separate room)
   - Clear, direct language: "Due to the physical altercation that occurred today, we've made the decision that you will need to return home from the program"
   - Explain clearly what behavior violated Code of Conduct
   - Stay calm and factual, not emotional

3. **Student Calls Parents/Guardians:**
   - Have student call their parent/guardian to inform them of violation
   - Staff must be present to overhear call (ensure student isn't faking call or calling friend)
   - Student explains: "I got into a physical fight and I need to come home"
   - CD/Staff gets on phone with parent to explain next steps and flight arrangements

4. **Remove from Program Activities:**
   - Student is removed from all programming immediately
   - Student must be supervised at all times until departure
   - Ensure student is kept safe and fed
   - Separate from other students to prevent further conflict

5. **Coordinate Departure:**
   - Parent arranges and pays for transportation home
   - If flying: staff stays at airport until student passes through security
   - Call HQ when student has departed

6. **Debrief:**
   - Debrief with remaining students (address what happened, reinforce safety)
   - Debrief with staff team (process incident, emotional support)

### Step 5: Documentation
**Complete Within 24 Hours:**

1. **Incident Report in Salesforce** (REQUIRED)
   - Include all factual details
   - Timeline of events
   - All parties involved (names)
   - Witness statements
   - Photos of injuries (if any)
   - Action taken (dismissal)
   - Flight/departure information

2. **Upload to IR:**
   - Witness statements (written or typed summaries)
   - Photos
   - Any relevant text/social media screenshots
   - Parent communication log

3. **Alert Communication:**
   - Email sent to alerts@summerspringboard.com triggers case creation
   - CD replies "handling" to prevent duplicate responses

## Conversation Scripts

### Separation/Initial Intervention
**Calm, Firm Voice:**
"Stop right now. We need to separate. [Name], you come with me. [Name], you go with [Staff Name]. We're going to talk about what happened."

### Investigation Interview
"I want to hear your side of what happened. Walk me through exactly what occurred, starting from [time/location]. Take your time."

"I'm not here to decide who's right or wrong yet. I need to understand the facts from everyone involved."

### Dismissal Conversation
**Two staff present, private location:**

"[Student name], we need to talk about what happened today. As you know, you were involved in a physical altercation with [other student]. Physical fighting is a serious violation of our Code of Conduct."

"We've completed our investigation and spoken with everyone involved. Based on what occurred, we've made the decision that you will need to return home from the program."

"I know this is disappointing, but physical violence is something we take very seriously for everyone's safety."

"What needs to happen now is you need to call your parents and let them know what happened and that they need to arrange for you to go home. I'll be here with you during that call, and then I'll speak with them about next steps."

**If student protests:**
"I understand you're upset. This decision has been made and it's final. Let's focus on getting you home safely."

**If student blames other person:**
"I hear that you feel [other person] was at fault. We've spoken with everyone involved. Regardless of how it started, physical fighting is against our Code of Conduct and we can't allow you to stay in the program."

### Parent Notification Call
**After student tells parent:**

"Hi [parent name], this is [Your Name], the Campus Director for Summer Springboard at [campus]. I'm here with [student]. As [student] just explained, there was a physical altercation today between [student] and another participant."

"We take the safety of all students very seriously, and physical violence is grounds for immediate dismissal from the program according to our Code of Conduct that was signed at enrollment."

"What we need from you now is to arrange transportation for [student] to return home. [He/She] will need to depart by [timeframe]. Can you tell me what works best for your family - driving to pick up or arranging a flight?"

"[Student] will remain supervised and safe until departure. We'll send you a detailed incident report within 24 hours."

"I know this is very disappointing for everyone. If you have questions about the incident, I'm happy to answer them now or we can schedule a call after [student] has departed."

**If parent is angry/defensive:**
"I completely understand your frustration. I'd like to walk you through what we observed and the investigation we conducted. [Share factual timeline]. Our priority is the safety of all students in the program."

**If parent says "my child was defending themselves":**
"I understand [student] may have felt threatened. We've investigated the full situation. Unfortunately, our policy on physical fighting doesn't allow us to make exceptions, even in cases where a student feels they were acting in self-defense. Our expectation is that students remove themselves from situations and find staff immediately."

## What NOT to Say/Do

### DO NOT:
- Take sides during investigation before hearing all accounts
- Leave students who fought in same space unsupervised
- Allow other students to crowd around or film the incident
- Rush to judgment without completing investigation
- Delay calling PD/HQ for dismissal decisions
- Tell student they're going home before confirming with PD
- Use inflammatory language ("You beat him up", "You were acting crazy")
- Make promises ("This will blow over", "Maybe you can stay if...")
- Allow students to "work it out" themselves after physical contact
- Skip parent notification
- Fail to document thoroughly
- Allow dismissed student access to other students before departure

### NEVER SAY:
- "Boys will be boys"
- "It was just playing around"
- "I'm sure it won't happen again"
- "Let's just forget this happened"
- "If you apologize you can stay"
- "I don't want to send you home but..."

### Legal/Policy Pitfalls:
- NEVER allow physical discipline or restraint beyond separation for safety
- DO NOT search student belongings without HQ approval
- DO NOT share details of one student's discipline with other students' parents
- DO NOT make dismissal decision without PD/HQ consultation

## Parent Notification

### WHEN to Call:
- IMMEDIATELY after determining dismissal will occur
- Before student leaves campus
- Within 2-4 hours of incident at latest

### WHO Calls:
- Campus Director makes the call
- PD may join call if needed
- Two staff should be present during student portion of call

### WHAT to Say:
**Structure of Call:**
1. Student tells parent first (staff overhears)
2. Staff gets on phone
3. Explain facts of what occurred (timeline, behavior observed)
4. Explain Code of Conduct violation and dismissal policy
5. Explain next steps (transportation arrangements, supervision until departure)
6. Offer to answer questions
7. Confirm contact method for follow-up

**Key Points to Cover:**
- Physical fighting is zero-tolerance policy
- Student's safety is being maintained until departure
- Tuition is non-refundable in dismissal cases
- Incident report will be sent within 24 hours
- Transportation costs are family's responsibility

### How to Handle Pushback:

**"This isn't fair"**
"I understand this is disappointing. Physical violence is a serious safety issue and our policy is clear - it results in dismissal from the program."

**"The other kid started it"**
"We've conducted a thorough investigation with all students involved. Regardless of how the conflict started, physical fighting violates our Code of Conduct. Our expectation is that students find staff when conflicts arise."

**"My kid has never done this before"**
"I hear that this is out of character. Unfortunately, we have to apply our policies consistently to keep all students safe."

**"I paid thousands of dollars"**
"I understand the financial investment. Our Code of Conduct and dismissal policies were outlined in the enrollment agreement. Tuition is non-refundable in cases of dismissal for safety violations."

**"I want to speak to someone higher up"**
"You're welcome to request a call with our Program Director [name]. I want to be clear that the dismissal decision is final, but [PD] can answer any questions about our policies and process."

## Follow-Up Procedures

### Immediate Follow-Up (Day of Incident):
- Ensure dismissed student has safely departed
- Staff member stays at airport until student passes through security (if flying)
- Call HQ to confirm student departure
- Complete incident report in Salesforce
- Debrief with staff team

### Within 24 Hours:
- Send detailed incident report to parents
- Debrief with remaining students (age-appropriate, maintain privacy)
- Check in with witnesses and students affected by incident
- Monitor program climate for remainder of session

### Within 48 Hours:
- Follow up with parent via email confirming student arrived home safely
- Document any parent questions/concerns in Salesforce case
- Conduct team debrief (what went well, what could improve)

### Ongoing:
- Monitor for any lingering impact on program climate
- Check in with students who witnessed incident
- Support staff who handled incident (emotional processing)
- Update risk management logs

### Case Closure:
- All documentation complete in Salesforce
- Parent communication concluded
- No outstanding safety concerns
- Team debrief completed
