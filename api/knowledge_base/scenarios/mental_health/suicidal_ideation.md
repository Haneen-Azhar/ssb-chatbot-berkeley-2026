# Suicidal Ideation - Mental Health Emergency

**Tags:** suicide, suicidal thoughts, suicidal ideation, wants to die, self harm, self-harm, cutting, kill myself, end my life, mental health crisis, emergency, depression, hopeless, cant go on, doesnt want to live, harm themselves, crisis intervention, 988, suicide hotline

## Recognition Triggers
- Student says "I want to die" or "I don't want to be here anymore"
- Student mentions suicide directly or indirectly
- Student talks about feeling hopeless, trapped, or having no reason to live
- Student mentions having a plan or means to harm themselves
- Student gives away possessions
- Sudden improvement in mood after period of depression (concerning sign)
- Withdrawn behavior + expressing worthlessness

## Severity Assessment

### IMMEDIATE EMERGENCY (Call 9-1-1)
- Student has a specific plan ("I'm going to jump off the bridge tonight")
- Student has means available (pills, weapon, access to heights)
- Student is actively attempting self-harm
- Student is in immediate danger

### URGENT (Call SSB Helpline + CD immediately)
- Student expresses suicidal thoughts but no immediate plan
- Student says they've been thinking about it
- Student mentions past suicide attempts
- Student is highly distressed but not in immediate danger

### CONCERNING (Document and notify CD)
- Passive suicidal ideation ("Sometimes I wish I wasn't here")
- Student says they would never act on it
- Temporary feelings of hopelessness

## Step-by-Step Protocol

### Step 1: Immediate Response (First 60 seconds)

1. **Stay with the student. Never leave them alone.**
   - Cancel your other duties immediately
   - Have another staff member cover your responsibilities
   - Get the student to a private, safe location

2. **Stay calm. Your reaction sets the tone.**
   - Don't panic or overreact
   - Speak slowly and calmly
   - Make eye contact
   - Take the student seriously - never dismiss their feelings

3. **Ask the direct question:**
   "Are you thinking about hurting yourself?"

   If yes: "Do you have a plan for how you would do it?"

   If yes to both: **This is a 9-1-1 emergency. Call now.**

### Step 2: While Waiting for Help (If emergency)

1. **Remove any means of self-harm**
   - Pills, sharp objects, belts, shoelaces
   - Move away from windows, balconies, stairs
   - Take them to ground floor if possible

2. **Keep talking**
   - "I'm here with you"
   - "You're not alone"
   - "Help is coming"
   - Listen more than you talk
   - Don't try to "fix" their problems
   - Don't say "you have so much to live for" (invalidating)

3. **Call the chain:**
   - 9-1-1 (if immediate danger)
   - SSB 24/7 Helpline: +1.858.779.0555
   - CD immediately
   - Have another staff member notify these contacts while you stay with student

### Step 3: If Not Immediate Danger (Urgent category)

1. **Assessment conversation:**

   Ask: "Have you thought about how you would hurt yourself?"
   - No plan = lower immediate risk
   - Has plan = higher risk, get help NOW

   Ask: "Have you tried to hurt yourself before?"
   - Past attempts = significantly higher risk

   Ask: "What's been happening that's made you feel this way?"
   - Listen without judgment
   - Don't minimize their pain
   - Validate their feelings: "That sounds really difficult"

2. **Make the calls:**
   - **SSB 24/7 Helpline: +1.858.779.0555** (they will guide you)
   - **CD** (notify immediately)
   - Follow helpline's instructions exactly

3. **Create a safety plan:**
   - "Can you promise me you'll tell staff if these feelings get stronger?"
   - "What helps you feel better when you're struggling?"
   - Identify their support system
   - Remove access to means (lock up medications, etc.)

### Step 4: Documentation

**Fill out Incident Report immediately** via:
- [Online form](https://docs.google.com/forms/d/e/1FAIpQLSdvScv2Kfcxbkh2N81ukoJLocuTXmVt1d1xrjpqX5q_Rk-IKg/viewform)
- Email: alerts@summerspringboard.com
- Photo to PD

**Include:**
- Exact words the student used (quote directly)
- Time and location of conversation
- Whether student has plan or means
- Past suicide attempts mentioned
- Current mental state assessment
- Who was notified and when
- Any action taken

### Step 5: Parent Notification

**CD makes the parent call, not you.**

The CD will inform parents:
- Their child is safe and being monitored
- Their child expressed suicidal thoughts
- SSB's response and safety plan
- Recommendation for professional evaluation
- Potential need for student to return home

**Never promise the student you won't tell anyone. Mental health emergencies require disclosure.**

## What to Say (Exact Scripts)

### Initial conversation:
"Hey, can we talk privately for a minute? I'm noticing you seem really down lately."

[If they open up]
"Thank you for trusting me with this. I'm really glad you told me."

[Ask directly]
"I need to ask you something important. Are you thinking about hurting yourself or ending your life?"

### If they say yes:
"I'm so glad you told me. You're not alone in this. I'm going to stay with you and we're going to get you help."

"Do you have a plan for how you would do it?"

[If yes] "Okay, I need to call for help right now. I'm staying with you."

### If they make you promise not to tell:
"I care about you too much to keep this a secret. Your safety is the most important thing. I need to get you help, and that means telling people who can support you."

### What NOT to say:
❌ "You have so much to live for"
❌ "It could be worse"
❌ "Just think positive"
❌ "Don't talk like that"
❌ "You're being dramatic"
❌ "Other people have it harder"
❌ "This is just a phase"

### What TO say:
✅ "I'm here with you"
✅ "Thank you for telling me"
✅ "You're not alone"
✅ "Help is available"
✅ "This feeling won't last forever"
✅ "We're going to get through this together"

## Follow-Up Procedures

### First 24 Hours:
- Student is NOT left alone
- Assign staff member for continuous supervision
- Room check every 30 minutes overnight
- Remove all potential means of self-harm from room
- Monitor bathroom access
- No excursions or off-campus activities

### Ongoing:
- Daily check-ins with designated staff member
- CD coordinates with parents about next steps
- Student may need to withdraw from program for professional care
- Document all interactions and student's mood
- Watch for changes in behavior

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

1. **Leaving student alone** - Never acceptable, even for a minute
2. **Making promises you can't keep** - "Everything will be okay"
3. **Trying to solve their problems** - You're not a therapist
4. **Minimizing their feelings** - Their pain is real
5. **Waiting to see if they're serious** - Always assume serious
6. **Not documenting** - Write everything down immediately
7. **Keeping it secret** - This is not a confidence you keep

## Red Flags for Escalation

Watch for:
- Student becomes more withdrawn after initial disclosure
- Student stops eating or sleeping
- Student starts giving away possessions
- Sudden calmness after period of distress (may indicate decision made)
- Isolating from friends
- Writing goodbye letters or social media posts

Any of these = immediate escalation to CD and re-assessment

## Legal and Ethical Requirements

- **Mandated reporting applies** - suicidal ideation must be reported to CD
- **No confidentiality** - safety overrides privacy
- **Parents must be informed** - CD handles this
- **Documentation required** - for legal protection
- **Duty of care** - you are responsible for their safety while in SSB custody
