## Key Policies by Category

### IMMEDIATE DISMISSAL OFFENSES

**Staff:**
1. Sharing room with student
2. Being alone with student in private space (unless 3+ students present)
3. Touching student when no one else around
4. Flirting with students
5. Sexual contact with student
6. Allowing students to engage in sexual acts
7. Romantic/sexual relationship with fellow staff during program
8. Substance use during program
9. Giving student personal medication
10. Falsifying safety-related records
11. Sexual harassment or assault
12. Abandoning assigned duties

**Students:**
1. Alcohol or drugs (possession, use, distribution)
2. Sexual activity between students
3. Violence or threats
4. Leaving campus without permission (may be case-by-case)
5. Theft
6. Intentional vandalism or property damage

### SUPERVISION REQUIREMENTS

**Staff-to-Student Ratios (MUST BE MAINTAINED AT ALL TIMES):**
- High School: 10:1 (10 students per 1 mentor)
- Middle School: 8:1 (8 students per 1 mentor)

**Never-Alone Policy:**
- Staff cannot be alone with single student in private space
- Acceptable: 1 staff + 3+ students with door open
- Acceptable: 1 staff + 1 student in fully public space
- NOT acceptable: 1 staff + 1 student in closed room or private space

**Student Grouping:**
- Students must be in groups of 3 or more during unstructured time
- Students cannot leave residence hall alone
- Buddy system enforced during all activities

### ESCALATION PATHWAYS

**"If in doubt, escalate it!!! We can always de-escalate!"**

**Student/Parent → Admissions/On-Call → Campus Director → Program Director → VP → Executive Director**

**Immediate escalation to PD required for:**
- Medical issues (especially if urgent care or more)
- Any early departure
- Code of Conduct violations (especially if dismissal possible)
- Sexual harassment/assault (real or perceived)
- Student or staff safety concerns
- Near misses
- Accumulation of minor issues forming larger issue
- Risk to SSB reputation
- Student/parent dissatisfaction
- Any crisis situation

**Escalation to Executive Level (PD → VP → ED) required for:**
- Sexual harassment or assault
- Grave programmatic challenges
- Near misses with life/limb risk
- Clear and urgent crisis situations

### INCIDENT REPORTING TIMELINE

**Required within 24 hours:**
- All incident reports
- Parent notification (non-emergency)
- Mandated reporting
- Case follow-up from PD

**Required within 48 hours:**
- Insurance notification (some cases)

**Required immediately:**
- Life-threatening emergencies (call, then document)
- Sexual harassment/assault reports (call, then document)
- Student missing (call, then document)
- Mandated reporting (if emergency)

### DOCUMENTATION REQUIREMENTS

**"If it isn't written down, it didn't happen!"**

**Must be:**
- Objective and factual (no opinions)
- Include exact times and dates
- Include WHO, WHAT, WHEN, WHERE, WHY, HOW
- Include direct quotes when possible
- Include all witnesses
- Completed within 24 hours

**Types of documentation:**
1. Green-level Staff Performance Report (coaching)
2. Yellow-level Incident Report (verbal warning)
3. Orange-level Incident Report (written warning + PIP)
4. Critical-level Incident Report (dismissal)
5. Student Code of Conduct Incident Reports
6. Medical Incident Reports
7. Parent Communication Logs
8. Daily Medical Logs
