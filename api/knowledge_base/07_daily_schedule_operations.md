## Detailed Daily Procedures

### Morning: 7:00 AM - 9:00 AM

#### Breakfast (7:00 AM - 7:45 AM)
**Location:** Campus dining hall (varies by campus)
**Staff:** All mentors with their assigned mentor groups

**Procedures:**
1. Mentors meet students at designated location
2. Walk to dining hall together as mentor group
3. Sit with students during meal
4. Use time for relationship building and casual check-ins
5. Ensure all students eat and stay hydrated

**Common Issues:**
- Students sleeping through breakfast: Knock on doors at 6:45 AM
- Dietary restrictions not available: Contact dining services immediately
- Students feeling unwell: Report to CD/AM, may need medical check

#### Commuter Check-In (8:30 AM - 8:45 AM)
**Location:** Main entrance/designated check-in area
**Staff:** Assigned commuter mentors (rotation schedule)

**Procedures:**
1. Arrive 15 minutes early to set up
2. Use REACH app to check in each commuter student
3. Verify student has all materials for the day
4. Direct student to first activity or meeting location
5. Note any late arrivals or absences

**Commuter Schedule:**
- **Monday & Tuesday:** Check in at 8:45 AM (later due to orientation)
- **Wednesday - Friday:** Check in at 8:30 AM
- **Daily check-out:** 6:00 PM - 6:15 PM at main gates

### Academic Time: 9:00 AM - 12:00 PM

#### Academic Course (9:00 AM - 12:00 PM)
**Locations:** Varies by course (see classroom assignments)
**Staff:** Academic Manager oversees, assigned mentors per course

**Mentor POC Responsibilities:**
1. Arrive at classroom 5 minutes before instructor
2. Take attendance at 9:00 AM using REACH
3. Escalate any missing students to AM immediately
4. Remain in classroom or immediately outside
5. Do NOT facilitate class (unless specifically requested by instructor)
6. Report any instructor needs to AM

**For CRW Sessions:**
- Mentor POC MUST remain in room entire workshop
- Assist with classroom management
- Support facilitator as needed

**Important Notes:**
- Mentors are liaison, not teaching assistants (unless requested 1 week in advance)
- Instructors responsible for copies and supplies
- Any instructor issues reported to AM first
- Missing students = immediate escalation

### Midday: 12:00 PM - 1:00 PM

#### Lunch
**Location:** Campus dining hall or designated area
**Staff:** All mentors with mentor groups

**Procedures:**
1. Gather mentor group after academic courses end
2. Account for all students using REACH
3. Walk to lunch together
4. Sit with students and continue relationship building
5. Monitor student interactions and well-being
6. Ensure students eat adequate food and hydrate

**For off-campus meals:**
- Students receive cash or PEX card funds ($20 lunch, $20-25 dinner)
- Students must stay within boundaries
- Rule of 3 always applies
- Students must check in by designated time

### Afternoon: 1:00 PM - 6:00 PM

#### Afternoon Activities
**Staff:** SPA coordinates, mentors assigned to specific activities
**Duration:** Varies by activity type

**Activity Types:**

**1. College Readiness Workshops (CRW)**
- Mentor assigned stays in room entire time
- Assist with materials and classroom management
- Take attendance upon arrival
- Support facilitator (do not facilitate unless trained)

**2. Academic Excursions**
- Related to student course content
- Mentor POC from that course attends
- May occur during morning block or Wednesday afternoons
- Follow excursion protocols (see below)

**3. Campus Activities**
- Clubs (meet up to 4 evenings during program)
- Recreation
- Study time
- Social activities

**4. Free Time / Boundaries**
- Students may explore within boundary map
- Rule of 3 always enforced
- Students must check in/out via REACH
- Mentors visible and available

#### Commuter Check-Out (6:00 PM - 6:15 PM)
**Location:** Main gates/designated location
**Staff:** Assigned commuter mentors

**Procedures:**
1. Verify all commuters present
2. Check out each student using REACH
3. Ensure students picked up safely
4. Note any late pickups and contact parents
5. Last commuter must leave by 6:15 PM

### Evening: 6:00 PM - 11:00 PM

#### Dinner (6:00 PM - 7:00 PM)
**Location:** Campus dining hall or activity location
**Staff:** All mentors with mentor groups

**Procedures:**
1. Same as lunch
2. Sit with students
3. Continue building rapport
4. Monitor for any issues or concerns
5. Ensure adequate nutrition and hydration

#### Evening Activities (7:00 PM - 9:30 PM)
**Staff:** SPA leads planning, all mentors support execution

**Activity Types:**

**Monday Night - ALL HANDS ON DECK:**
- Club Fair (1st Monday of session)
- Cupcake social or game night
- Whole group activity in large common area
- All mentors required

**Regular Evening Activities:**
- Movie Night
- Karaoke
- Flag Painting
- Talent Show
- Cultural Exchange Night
- Science Fair
- Game nights
- Club meetings (up to 4 times per session)

**Mentor Responsibilities:**
1. Actively engage with students
2. Monitor behavior and safety
3. Enforce Code of Conduct
4. Encourage participation
5. Model positive behavior
6. Document any concerns

#### Unstructured Time (8:30 PM - 9:30 PM)
**Staff:** All mentors on duty, visible and available

**Student Rules:**
- Cannot leave residence hall
- Must be in groups of three minimum
- Must stay within designated areas
- Quiet hours approaching

**Mentor Responsibilities:**
- Be visible in hallways and common areas
- Help students with homework or questions
- Monitor for rule violations
- Ensure safety
- Build relationships through casual interaction

#### Back to Room (9:30 PM - 10:00 PM)
**Staff:** Floor mentors

**Procedures:**
1. Students return to dorm rooms
2. Get ready for bed (shower, brush teeth, etc.)
3. Mentors monitor hallways
4. Ensure students in correct rooms
5. Quiet hours begin

#### Bed Checks (10:30 PM)
**Staff:** Assigned mentors (rotation schedule)
**Location:** All student rooms

**Procedures:**
1. Knock on door
2. Visually confirm all roommates present and accounted for
3. Do quick emotional check-in ("Rose and Thorn" or similar)
4. Check completion of required forms (Day 1: RCR and Roommate Contract)
5. Mark students as checked in on REACH
6. Note any concerns for team huddle

**After bed checks, students should NEVER have "Checked In" location on REACH - should show current room location**

**Red Flags:**
- Student not in room (immediate escalation to CD)
- Student appears unwell
- Roommate conflicts
- Emotional distress
- Room damage or violations

#### Team Huddle (10:30 PM - 11:00 PM)
**Participants:** CD, AM, SPA, HQ Staff (if on campus), All Mentors
**Location:** SSB Office or designated meeting space

**Agenda:**
1. Debrief the day
2. Shout-outs (celebrate wins and good moments)
3. Report concerns or issues
4. Review tomorrow's schedule
5. Confirm mentor assignments for next day
6. Address any questions
7. Coordinate coverage and time off

**Important:** This is mandatory for all staff not on approved time off

- Student's own expense
- Must stay within campus boundaries

## Mentor Group Procedures

### Purpose of Mentor Groups
1. Foster deeper connections between students and staff
2. Functional safety measure for student counts
3. Easier regrouping and information relay
4. Daily touchpoints for student well-being

### Mentor Group Organization Tips

**Consider these ratios when forming groups:**
1. Residential vs Commuters
2. Enrolled courses
3. Rooming arrangements
4. Gender and age
5. HS ratio: 1:10 / MS ratio: 1:8

**Best Practices:**
1. Find space conducive to inclusivity and privacy
2. Encourage students to sit within group (not outside it)
3. Don't skip mentor groups - crucial for student experience
4. Understand key medical info (allergies, POAs, EpiPen carriers)
5. Build trust as students' primary contact

### Daily Mentor Group Touchpoints

**Minimum touchpoints:**
- Breakfast together
- Lunch together
- Dinner together
- Before excursions (roll call and boarding bus)
- Evening check-ins as needed

**Common Mentor Group Activities:**
- Icebreakers (especially first few days)
- Rose and Thorn check-ins
- Group discussions
- Shared meals
- Excursion preparation
- Problem-solving student issues

## Quiet Hours and Curfew

### Quiet Hours (Residential)
- **Sunday - Thursday:** 11:00 PM - 8:00 AM
- **Friday - Saturday:** 1:00 AM - 8:00 AM

**Noise levels must be conducive to sleeping**

### Curfew and Check-In Times

**Daily:**
- Students must be back on campus by 9:30 PM
- Students in room ready for bed checks by 10:30 PM

**No exceptions unless approved leave request**

### Lights Out
- Not enforced, but quiet hours are
- Students may stay up in their rooms quietly
- Common areas close at designated times

## Wake-Up Procedures

**Standard wake-up:** Natural (students responsible for own alarms)

**For students who oversleep:**
1. Knock on door at 6:45 AM if student has history of sleeping in
2. Phone call to room if available
3. Louder knocking if no response
4. Enter room with another staff member if still no response
5. Check student is breathing and responsive
6. If cannot wake or appears ill, escalate to CD immediately

## Campus Boundaries and Rule of 3

### Boundary Map
- Provided to all students during orientation
- Students must stay within boundaries unless on supervised excursion
- Boundaries ensure safety and security

### Rule of 3
**Students must NEVER travel alone - minimum group of 3**

**Applies to:**
- Walking around campus
- Going to meals
- Free time exploration
- Any movement outside of supervised activities

**Exceptions:**
- Going to own dorm room
- Using bathroom
- Official 1-on-1 with staff

### Enforcement
- Staff monitor boundaries during free time
- Students check in/out via REACH when leaving designated areas
- Violations = Code of Conduct issue, may result in warning or contract

## Daily Huddle (Team Meeting)

### Timing
10:30 PM - 11:00 PM (after bed checks)

### Attendance
Mandatory for all staff not on approved time off

### Agenda
1. **Attendance check** - all staff present
2. **Shout-outs** - celebrate successes and positive moments
3. **Concerns** - report any student or program issues
4. **Medical updates** - any student health issues
5. **Tomorrow's schedule** - review activities and logistics
6. **Assignments** - confirm who's doing what tomorrow
7. **Questions** - staff can ask anything
8. **Time off coordination** - who has night off, coverage plan

### Setting the Tone
- CD sets the energy and attitude
- Model positivity even when things don't go as planned
- Clear, consistent communication
- Relationship building among staff
- Address conflicts promptly and professionally

## Staff Wellness and Burnout Prevention

### Time Off
- Minimum 1 night off (6 PM - 8 AM) per week
- Plan coverage as a team
- Fair distribution of time off
- CD has final say on schedule

### Preventing Burnout
1. Eat meals (don't skip)
2. Stay hydrated
3. Take breaks when possible
4. Use free time to recharge
5. Support each other
6. Communicate needs early
7. Ask for help when needed

### Between Sessions
- Two nights between sessions for rest
- Prep for next session
- 1-on-1s with leadership
- Team bonding activities (optional)
- Personal time once prep complete

**If covering early/late students:** hours divided among mentors fairly

## Common Daily Issues and Solutions

### Issue: Student Late to Class
**Solution:**
1. Note in REACH
2. Speak to student about punctuality
3. If recurring, escalate to CD
4. May become Code of Conduct issue

### Issue: Student Unwell at Breakfast
**Solution:**
1. Ask student about symptoms
2. Take to quiet area
3. Contact CD/AM immediately
4. May need medical evaluation
5. Document in REACH

### Issue: Instructor Absent/Late
**Solution:**
1. Mentor POC contacts AM immediately
2. AM contacts instructor
3. Backup plan: mentor facilitates review or study time
4. AM coordinates coverage

### Issue: Missing Student at Activity
**Solution:**
1. Check last known location on REACH
2. Check classroom, bathroom, dorm room
3. Contact student via REACH
4. Escalate to CD if not located within 5 minutes
5. Do not leave other students unsupervised

### Issue: Conflict Between Students
**Solution:**
1. Separate students
2. Listen to each side individually
3. Mediate if minor issue
4. Escalate to CD if serious or ongoing
5. Document in incident report if needed
6. May require parent contact

### Issue: Equipment/Material Not Available
**Solution:**
1. Check SSB office inventory
2. Contact AM for academic materials
3. Contact SPA for activity materials
4. Improvise if possible
5. Communicate issue to students professionally
