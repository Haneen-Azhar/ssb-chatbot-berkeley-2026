### 10 - Medical Protocols & Safety
**File:** `10_medical_protocols_safety.md`

Comprehensive medical and safety procedures:

**Staff Scope of Practice:**
- CAN: Basic first aid, assist with student's own medications, monitor wellbeing, contact medical professionals
- CANNOT: Diagnose, prescribe, give personal medication to students, make medical decisions

**Required Certifications:**
- Campus Directors: CPR (Adult) + First Aid
- UC Berkeley staff specifically: Adult + Pediatric CPR
- Must be hybrid/in-person certification (not online-only)
- Must upload by May 15 (CDs) or June 1 (Mentors)

**Life-Threatening Emergencies (Call 9-1-1):**
- Not breathing / difficulty breathing
- No pulse / chest pain
- Unresponsive
- Severe bleeding
- Anaphylaxis (facial/throat swelling, difficulty breathing)
- Seizure (first-time or prolonged)
- Head injury with loss of consciousness
- Poisoning / overdose
- Severe burns

**Emergency Response Protocol:**
1. Call 9-1-1 immediately
2. Start CPR if needed
3. Send for AED if available
4. Call CD immediately
5. CD calls parents immediately
6. Staff accompanies student to hospital
7. Document everything within 24 hours

**Common Medical Situations:**
- Allergic reactions (mild vs. anaphylaxis)
- Asthma attacks
- Seizures
- Concussion/head injury
- Diabetes management (low/high blood sugar)
- Minor injuries (cuts, sprains, suspected fractures)
- Illness (fever, vomiting, cold/flu)
- Mental health emergencies

**Supervision Ratios (MUST MAINTAIN):**
- High School: 10:1 (10 students per 1 mentor)
- Middle School: 8:1

**Never-Alone Policy:**
- Staff NEVER alone with single student in private space
- Always have 3+ students present OR public space with others around

**Water Safety:**
- Lifeguard required for all water activities
- Buddy system enforced
- Headcount every 10 minutes in water
- No swimming without supervision

**Workers Compensation (Staff Injuries):**
- Insurance: Employers Insurance (Policy: EIG550570202)
- Report online: https://www.employers.com/report-claim/
- 24/7 phone: (888) 682-6671

### De-escalation Techniques
**File:** `training/deescalation_techniques.md`

**The 3 C's Framework:**
1. **CARE** - "I can see you're upset. I'm here to help."
2. **COMPASSION & EMPATHY** - "That sounds really frustrating. I understand why you feel that way."
3. **CONTROL** - "Here's what we're going to do next..."

**Four-Phase Engagement:**
1. **LISTEN** - Let them vent, don't interrupt, show you're listening
2. **BRIDGE** - Validate their feelings, find common ground
3. **PIVOT** - Redirect to solutions, offer choices
4. **REPAIR** - Follow up, rebuild relationship, move forward

**De-escalation Scenarios:**
- Student having panic attack
- Student angry at staff decision
- Student-to-student conflict
- Parent upset on phone call
- Staff frustrated with workload

**Impact vs. Intent:**
- Acknowledge impact first, even if intent was good
- "I hear that what I said hurt you. That wasn't my intent, but I understand the impact."
- Never say "I didn't mean it that way" without acknowledging impact

**Managing Divisive Topics:**
- Politics, religion, identity
- Redirect to learning opportunity
- Maintain neutral facilitation
- Shut down if becomes harmful
- Document and report to CD

### Staff Wellness
**File:** `training/staff_wellness.md`

**Reality Check:**
- Long hours (6 AM - midnight most days)
- Weekends are work days (no time off Saturday/Sunday)
- High stress and responsibility
- Limited privacy and personal time
- Emotionally demanding work
- Physical exhaustion

**Time Off Policy:**
- NO time off during session (except emergencies)
- Time off between sessions only
- Must coordinate with CD for coverage
- Cannot leave campus during on-call shifts

**Burnout Warning Signs:**

**Physical:** Exhaustion, getting sick, headaches, sleep problems, appetite changes

**Emotional:** Irritability, anxiety, feeling overwhelmed, crying easily, feeling numb

**Mental:** Trouble concentrating, forgetfulness, negative thoughts, cynicism

**Behavioral:** Withdrawing from others, decreased performance, missing responsibilities

**Prevention Strategies:**

**Daily:**
- Eat all three meals
- Hydrate constantly
- Take 10-minute breaks when possible
- Get fresh air
- Connect with coworker for support
- Stretch or move your body

**Weekly:**
- Get adequate sleep (7-8 hours)
- Video call with family/friends
- Do something you enjoy (even 30 minutes)
- Journal or debrief

**Managing Stress in the Moment:**
- 30 seconds: Deep breathing (4 counts in, 6 counts out)
- 5 minutes: Walk outside, wash face, listen to music
- 30 minutes: Take a real break, eat a meal, talk to friend

**When to Escalate Your Own Wellness:**
- Feeling unable to fulfill duties
- Experiencing panic attacks
- Crying frequently
- Thoughts of self-harm
- Unable to sleep for multiple days
- Feeling hopeless
- Want to quit mid-session

**Tell CD immediately. Staff wellness is program safety.**

## Scenario Playbooks

### Mental Health Crises

#### Suicidal Ideation
**File:** `scenarios/mental_health/suicidal_ideation.md`

**Recognition Triggers:**
- "I want to die" or "I don't want to be here anymore"
- Mentions suicide directly or indirectly
- Talks about feeling hopeless, trapped, no reason to live
- Mentions having a plan or means
- Gives away possessions
- Sudden improvement in mood after depression (concerning sign)

**Severity Assessment:**

**IMMEDIATE EMERGENCY (Call 9-1-1):**
- Specific plan ("I'm going to jump off the bridge tonight")
- Means available (pills, weapon, access to heights)
- Actively attempting self-harm
- In immediate danger

**URGENT (Call SSB Helpline + CD):**
- Suicidal thoughts but no immediate plan
- Mentions past suicide attempts
- Highly distressed but not in immediate danger

**CONCERNING (Document and notify CD):**
- Passive ideation ("Sometimes I wish I wasn't here")
- Says they would never act on it
- Temporary feelings of hopelessness

**Step-by-Step Protocol:**

**Step 1: Immediate Response (First 60 seconds)**
1. Stay with student - NEVER leave alone
2. Stay calm - your reaction sets the tone
3. Ask direct question: "Are you thinking about hurting yourself?"
4. If yes: "Do you have a plan for how you would do it?"
5. If yes to both: Call 9-1-1 NOW

**Step 2: While Waiting for Help**
1. Remove means of self-harm (pills, sharp objects, belts, shoelaces)
2. Move away from windows, balconies, stairs
3. Keep talking: "I'm here with you. You're not alone. Help is coming."
4. Call chain: 9-1-1 → SSB Helpline (+1.858.779.0555) → CD

**Step 3: Documentation**
- Fill out Incident Report immediately
- Include exact words student used (quote directly)
- Note whether student has plan or means
- Document all actions taken and notifications made

**Step 4: Parent Notification**
- CD makes the call, not you
- Parents informed of situation, safety plan, and recommendation for professional evaluation

**What to Say:**
✅ "I'm here with you"
✅ "Thank you for telling me"
✅ "You're not alone"
✅ "We're going to get through this together"

**What NOT to Say:**
❌ "You have so much to live for"
❌ "Just think positive"
❌ "You're being dramatic"
❌ "Other people have it harder"

**Follow-Up:**
- Student NOT left alone for first 24 hours
- Room checks every 30 minutes overnight
- Remove all potential means of self-harm from room
- No excursions or off-campus activities
- Daily check-ins with designated staff
- Student may need to withdraw for professional care

### Incident Response Flowchart

**When ANY incident occurs:**

```
Step 1: STABILIZE
├─ Is anyone in immediate danger?
│  ├─ YES → Call 9-1-1, remove from danger, secure scene
│  └─ NO → Continue to Step 2
│
Step 2: ASSESS
├─ What happened? (facts only)
├─ Who is involved?
├─ Is medical attention needed?
├─ Is CD aware?
│  └─ NO → Notify CD immediately
│
Step 3: CATEGORIZE
├─ Life-threatening emergency → 9-1-1 + CD + SSB Helpline
├─ Serious incident → CD + SSB Helpline
├─ Medical issue → CD + parent notification
├─ Code of Conduct violation → CD investigation
├─ Parent complaint → CD response
│
Step 4: DOCUMENT
├─ Fill out Incident Report within 24 hours
├─ Include: Date, time, location, people involved, exact quotes, actions taken
├─ Submit via online form OR alerts@summerspringboard.com
│
Step 5: FOLLOW UP
├─ CD determines next steps
├─ Parent notification if required
├─ Student support plan
├─ Staff debrief
└─ Lessons learned
```

**Key Decision Points:**
- **Call 9-1-1 if:** Life-threatening, severe injury, not breathing, chest pain, severe allergic reaction, seizure, overdose, violence
- **Call SSB Helpline if:** Dismissal consideration, sexual harassment, missing student, police involvement, suicidal ideation, serious emergency
- **Call CD if:** ANY medical issue, Code of Conduct violation, parent complaint, student crisis, staff concern
- **Call Parents if:** Injury, illness, doctor visit, official warning, behavioral contract, dismissal, safety concern
